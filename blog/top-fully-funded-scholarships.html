Top 10 Fully Funded Scholarships for International Students in 2026

Published July 7, 2026 · 12 min read

Finding a scholarship that covers every expense — tuition, living costs, travel, and health insurance — can feel like searching for a needle in a haystack. The good news is that some of the world's most prestigious scholarship programmes do exactly that. These fully funded opportunities are offered by governments, universities, and international organisations that believe investing in global talent produces returns far beyond the financial cost.

Below are ten of the most sought-after fully funded scholarships available to international students in 2026, ranked by a combination of generosity, prestige, and global search demand. Each entry includes the host country, degree levels covered, key benefits, and a practical tip for strengthening your application.

1. Chevening Scholarship (United Kingdom)

The Chevening Scholarship is the UK government's flagship international awards programme, funded by the Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office. It covers full tuition at any UK university, a monthly living stipend, return airfare, and additional allowances for thesis work and travel. Chevening targets mid-career professionals with at least two years of work experience who demonstrate clear leadership potential. Applications typically open in August and close in November each year. Tip: Your leadership essay must include specific examples of influence, not just job titles.

2. Gates Cambridge Scholarship (United Kingdom)

The funds outstanding postgraduate students from outside the UK to study at the University of Cambridge. It covers the full cost of tuition plus a maintenance stipend of approximately £20,000 per year, plus allowances for academic conferences and family provision. The selection committee weighs intellectual excellence, leadership potential, and commitment to improving the lives of others. Tip: Your personal statement should demonstrate how your academic work connects to a broader social impact.

3. Rhodes Scholarship (United Kingdom)

The Rhodes Scholarship, the world's oldest international graduate scholarship, funds study at the University of Oxford. It covers all university and college fees plus a personal stipend. Rhodes looks for exceptional character, leadership, and energy beyond academic achievement alone. Tip: The interview is weighted heavily — prepare to discuss your values and ethical reasoning, not just your accomplishments.

4. DAAD Scholarships (Germany)

The German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) offers dozens of fully funded programmes across all degree levels. The most popular include the EPOS programme for development-related master's degrees and the Helmut Schmidt Programme for public policy. Benefits vary by programme but typically include monthly stipends of €934-1,300, health insurance, and travel allowances. Tip: DAAD values development impact — connect your study plan to concrete outcomes in your home country.

5. MEXT Scholarship (Japan)

The MEXT Scholarship is Japan's government-funded programme for international students at all degree levels. It covers full tuition, a monthly stipend of approximately ¥143,000-145,000, and round-trip airfare. Applicants apply either through the Japanese embassy in their home country (embassy recommendation) or directly through a Japanese university (university recommendation). Tip: For the embassy route, begin preparing your research plan at least three months before the application window opens.

6. Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Degrees (Global/Europe)

The funds master's degrees taught jointly by consortia of European universities. Scholars study in at least two countries and receive a monthly allowance of €1,400, full tuition coverage, insurance, and travel costs. The programme is open to students worldwide, with particular emphasis on candidates from partner countries. Tip: Choose a programme that genuinely matches your undergraduate background — the selection criteria include academic coherence.

7. Global Korea Scholarship (South Korea)

The GKS programme (formerly KGSP) funds international students for undergraduate and graduate study at Korean universities. Benefits include airfare, full tuition, a monthly stipend of approximately &won;900,000, medical insurance, and a one-year Korean language training programme. Tip: Your personal statement should demonstrate genuine interest in Korean culture and society, not just academic goals.

8. Swiss Government Excellence Scholarships (Switzerland)

The support postgraduate researchers and artists from over 180 countries. Awards include a monthly stipend of CHF 1,920 for PhD students, tuition waivers, and health insurance. Applications are submitted through the Swiss embassy in your country. Tip: Your research proposal must include a letter of acceptance from a Swiss professor — start building that relationship early.

9. Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC)

The is one of the largest government-funded scholarship programmes globally, supporting tens of thousands of students at over 280 Chinese universities. It covers full tuition, free accommodation, a monthly stipend of 2,500-3,500 RMB, and medical insurance. Tip: Apply through multiple channels — both the bilateral programme (through your local embassy) and the university programme — to maximise your chances.

10. Australia Awards Scholarships (Australia)

The fund students from developing countries to study at Australian universities. The package includes full tuition, return airfare, a living stipend of approximately AUD 31,000 per year, establishment allowance, and health cover. Tip: The development impact statement is the most heavily weighted component — be specific about how your study will benefit your home country.

How to Choose the Right Scholarship for You

Not every fully funded scholarship is the right fit for every applicant. Consider these factors when narrowing your choices:

  • Degree level: Some scholarships (Chevening, Gates Cambridge) are exclusively for postgraduate study, while others (MEXT, CSC, GKS) cover all levels.
  • Work experience: Chevening requires two years; most others do not.
  • Return requirement: Australia Awards and Chevening require you to return home after study. Others do not.
  • Country restrictions: Some scholarships target specific regions or developing countries.
  • Language: MEXT and GKS include language training; European programmes may require English proficiency tests.
