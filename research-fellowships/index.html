Research Fellowships

Research Fellowships can make an international education or career plan more achievable when the program fits the applicant. This page is designed for doctoral candidates, postdoctoral researchers, faculty, and specialists seeking funded research time. This collection currently contains 16 matching opportunities. Each listing connects to an official provider page and includes the country, funding position, study level, field, and deadline. Applicants can compare multiple programs without searching across dozens of separate sources. The consistent structure makes it easy to identify which opportunities deserve a deeper review.

16 matching listings.

What applicants can expect

The benefits of these opportunities may include research support, host collaboration, mentorship, publication time, fieldwork access, and specialist networks. The exact package always depends on the provider. Applicants should check whether tuition, travel, insurance, accommodation, or research costs are included. Reading the full terms on the provider's site is essential before making a decision. Some awards also offer mentoring, networking, or professional development opportunities beyond the financial package.

Eligibility and process

A strong candidate normally matches the published eligibility before applying. Key checks include research stage, project fit, host alignment, academic record, methodology, and provider-specific rules. A sensible application workflow is to define a precise research question, identify host fit, prepare a proposal and references, and apply officially. Read both the eligibility section and any exclusions, then confirm your qualification dates and location fit. Where admission and funding use separate processes, complete both and do not assume one submission covers the other. Keep copies of all submitted documents and confirmation emails for your records.

Additional tips

For a more targeted search, connect your project to the host's strengths, make outcomes measurable, and verify eligible costs. Compare each program with your own direction, then rank options by fit, funding, deadline, and preparation time. Keep a base CV and document folder, but tailor each motivation statement to the selection criteria of the specific program. A focused shortlist of well-matched programs is usually more productive than applying to every available option.
