NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Program

Introduction

Since 1952, the National Science Foundation's Graduate Research Fellowship Program has been the United States' oldest and one of its most respected fellowships supporting early-career graduate students in STEM, having funded more than 43,000 fellows out of over 500,000 applicants, over 20 of whom went on to become Nobel laureates. Unlike many fellowships that support established researchers, GRFP is deliberately aimed at people early in their graduate careers, evaluating potential for future contributions rather than a track record of prior publications.

About the Opportunity

GRFP supports full-time, research-based master's and doctoral study in STEM fields, including STEM education, at any accredited nonprofit US institution, with fellows selected based on their demonstrated potential for significant research achievement rather than existing accomplishments alone. The fellowship is portable, meaning fellows who complete a master's degree can continue their award into a subsequent PhD programme, and NSF also recognizes strong applicants who narrowly miss selection with an Honorable Mention, considered a meaningful national academic distinction in its own right.

Benefits

Fellows receive a three-year fellowship, usable across a five-year window, providing an annual stipend of USD 37,000 plus a USD 16,000 cost-of-education allowance paid directly to the host institution to cover tuition and required fees, with the institution required to waive any standard charges beyond that allowance.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants must be US citizens, nationals, or permanent residents at the time of application, intending to enroll or currently enrolled full-time in an eligible research-based STEM or STEM-education master's or doctoral programme, having completed no more than one academic year of graduate study; professional degree programmes such as medicine, law, and business are not eligible, and each individual may apply only once during their graduate career.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields span the NSF-supported STEM disciplines broadly, including biology, chemistry, physics, engineering, computer and information science, mathematics, geosciences, and the social and behavioral sciences, plus STEM education research.

Required Documents

Applicants need the online GRFP application including a Personal, Relevant Background, and Future Goals Statement, a Graduate Research Plan Statement, official transcripts from every institution attended, and three reference letters submitted directly by recommenders through the GRFP system.

Application Process

Applicants complete the eligibility questionnaire and full application online through research.gov/GRFP, selecting a Major Field of Study that determines which disciplinary experts review their file, while arranging for three reference letters to be submitted separately by recommenders ahead of a slightly earlier reference deadline.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 22 October 2026, with reference letters typically due a few days earlier; applicants should confirm the exact reference letter cutoff on the current GRFP solicitation, since it is set separately from the main application date.

Why You Should Apply

For US citizens, nationals, and permanent residents early in a STEM graduate career, GRFP offers substantial, flexible funding and a credential carrying genuine long-term prestige, backed by a 70-plus percent doctoral completion rate among fellows within 11 years, making it one of the most valuable fellowships available to early-stage American STEM researchers.

For full eligibility details and the current solicitation, visit the official NSF GRFP website: https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/grfp-nsf-graduate-research-fellowship-program
