Fellowship - Saudi Arabia

KAUST Global Fellowship Program

Introduction

Founded in 2009 on the shores of the Red Sea, KAUST has grown into the most acclaimed postgraduate research university in the Middle East, and its Global Fellowship Program exists to bring outstanding postdoctoral researchers from around the world into that research-first environment for three fully funded years of independent work. Unlike many postdoctoral fellowships that assign researchers to an existing lab structure, KAUST Global Fellows design and lead their own original research project, mentored by a KAUST faculty member they identify and recruit themselves before ever submitting an application.

About the Opportunity

Fellows conduct genuinely independent research aligned with one of KAUST's priority areas, Health & Wellness, Sustainable Environment & Essential Needs, Energy & Industrial Leadership, or Economies of the Future, within one of three academic divisions: Biological and Environmental Science and Engineering, Computer, Electrical and Mathematical Science and Engineering, or Physical Science and Engineering. Because proposals must demonstrate genuine novelty beyond an applicant's existing PhD work, the fellowship functions less as an extension of doctoral research and more as a deliberate launchpad toward full research independence.

Benefits

Fellows receive a yearly living stipend of USD 75,000, a research budget of up to USD 40,000 per year, plus free housing, medical insurance, schooling for up to two children, and relocation and travel allowances, all for a three-year fellowship term evaluated at the end of the second year.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants of any nationality holding a PhD awarded within the past four calendar years, or expecting to complete one before the fellowship starts, can apply, provided they secure a confirmed KAUST faculty member as their Host PI in advance; KAUST alumni previously affiliated with the university for more than 12 months are not eligible.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible research spans KAUST's core priority areas, water, energy, environment, food and health, and digital transformation, delivered through its three academic divisions covering biological, environmental, computational, electrical, mathematical, and physical sciences and engineering.

Required Documents

Applicants need the online application form, a CV listing publications, a project proposal and abstract following KAUST's specific template, a support letter from their confirmed Host PI, and contact information for referees.

Application Process

Applicants must first identify and secure agreement from a KAUST faculty member willing to serve as Host PI, then submit a complete online application including their proposal, CV, and the Host PI's support letter; a maximum of ten fellowships are awarded each year, with internal division committees pre-screening applications before the strongest proposals proceed to external review.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 1 October 2026, with applications typically accepted from 1 July; successful candidates are announced three to four months after the deadline and generally begin their fellowship between September and December of the following year.

Why You Should Apply

For early-career researchers with a genuinely original research idea and access to a supportive KAUST faculty contact, this fellowship offers exceptional financial support and full research independence rarely matched by other postdoctoral programmes, though success depends heavily on the quality of the Host PI relationship secured before applying.

For full application guidelines, visit the official KAUST Global Fellowship Program page: https://kgfp.kaust.edu.sa/how-to-apply
