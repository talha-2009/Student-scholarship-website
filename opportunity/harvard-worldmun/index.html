Harvard WorldMUN

## Introduction

Harvard WorldMUN bills itself as the world's largest and most diverse collegiate Model United Nations conference, and unlike most Model UN events that return to the same city year after year, it is deliberately hosted in a different global location annually, giving each cohort of delegates a genuinely different backdrop for the same rigorous diplomatic simulation. Organized by Harvard University students through the Harvard International Relations Council, WorldMUN operates at the university level rather than high school, drawing delegations from colleges and universities across dozens of countries to a single week of intensive committee work, negotiation, and cross-cultural exchange.

## About the Opportunity

Delegates are assigned to specific UN or historical committees and represent an assigned country or figure's position throughout the conference, engaging in formal debate, informal negotiation, and resolution drafting alongside hundreds of other student delegates from around the world. Because the host city rotates annually, past WorldMUN conferences have taken place across Asia, Europe, the Americas, and beyond, meaning the experience combines the academic rigor of Model UN with genuine international travel and cultural immersion in a way that most fixed-location conferences cannot replicate. The scale of the event, drawing well over a thousand delegates in a typical year, also means committees are genuinely competitive and diplomatically complex, closer in feel to a real international negotiation than a smaller campus-level simulation.

## Benefits

Participants gain substantial diplomatic simulation experience, working through the kind of complex multilateral negotiation that mirrors real international relations work, along with genuine international networking with student delegates from dozens of countries and direct cultural exchange in whichever city hosts that year's conference.

## Who Can Apply?

University students with a genuine interest in international relations, diplomacy, or Model UN participation can attend, typically registering as part of a university delegation organized through their school's Model UN or international relations society rather than applying as individuals.

## Fields of Study or Available Areas

WorldMUN draws students with interests in international relations, political science, law, and global affairs broadly, though delegates come from a wide range of academic backgrounds since Model UN skills, negotiation, public speaking, and policy analysis, translate across disciplines.

## Required Documents

Delegates need university affiliation confirmation, a delegation registration form completed by their school's organizing group, and payment of the conference fee, which covers registration and typically some conference materials.

## Application Process

Students connect with their university's Model UN or international relations society to confirm delegation participation, since WorldMUN registers through university delegations rather than individual applications; the delegation as a whole then completes registration through the official WorldMUN website ahead of the conference.

## Deadline

Please refer to the official website for the current year's specific host city, conference dates, and registration deadlines, since WorldMUN's rotating location means both dates and travel planning considerations shift meaningfully from year to year.

## Why You Should Apply

For university students who want Model UN experience at genuinely international scale, few conferences match WorldMUN's combination of scale, diplomatic rigor, and rotating global location, making it a standout choice for students who want both the substantive negotiation experience and the cultural exposure of traveling to a new host country each year.

For full registration details, visit the official Harvard WorldMUN website: https://worldmun.org/
