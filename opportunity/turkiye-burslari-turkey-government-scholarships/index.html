Turkiye Burslari (Turkey Government Scholarships)

Introduction

Türkiye Bursları, or Türkiye Scholarships, is the Turkish government's flagship scholarship programme, run by the Presidency for Turks Abroad and Related Communities since 1991 and now drawing well over 100,000 applications a year from more than 160 countries for roughly 5,000 available places. What distinguishes it from many national scholarships is how comprehensive and self-contained the process is: a single application handles both funding and university placement together, so applicants never need a separate admission letter or a second application to a Turkish institution.

About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue associate, bachelor's, master's, or PhD study, or short-term research placements, at one of Turkey's 207 higher education institutions, with the scholarship board handling department and university placement based on applicants' ranked preferences, academic record, and available quotas rather than requiring candidates to secure admission independently. Scholars without C1-level Turkish complete a fully funded year of Turkish language study before beginning their main degree programme, and specialized tracks like the Success Scholarship, Science Scholarship, and Humanitarian Aid Scholarship run on separate timelines with enhanced benefits for specific applicant profiles.

Benefits

Scholars receive full tuition fee exemption, dormitory accommodation, a monthly stipend of roughly 4,500 TL for bachelor's, 6,500 TL for master's, and 9,000 TL for PhD study (doubled under certain special tracks), health insurance, round-trip airfare, and a fully funded one-year Turkish language course for those who need it.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need to be citizens of any country other than Turkey, not currently enrolled at a Turkish university at the same level they are applying for, meeting minimum academic averages of roughly 70 percent for bachelor's, 75 percent for master's and PhD, and 90 percent for health sciences programmes, alongside age limits of under 21 for bachelor's, under 30 for master's, under 35 for PhD, and under 50 for research scholarships.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Türkiye Bursları spans essentially all academic fields across Turkey's public and foundation universities, from engineering and health sciences through humanities and social sciences, with dedicated tracks available for the arts and for scientific/technical research.

Required Documents

Applicants need a valid passport or ID, a recent photo, academic transcripts and diplomas (officially translated if needed), two recommendation letters, a Letter of Intent, and, for master's and PhD applicants, a research proposal; language test scores are only required for programmes taught in English or another non-Turkish language.

Application Process

Applicants create an account on the official TBBS portal, complete the online form with personal and academic details, upload all required documents, and select up to 12 preferred programmes ranked by priority; the scholarship board reviews eligibility, shortlists candidates for interviews conducted through embassies, consulates, or YTB teams, and independently places successful scholars into their final university and programme.

Deadline

The current application window is 10 January to 20 February 2027, for the main bachelor's, master's, and PhD tracks; specialized tracks like Research or Success Scholarships run on separate timelines announced periodically on the official website.

Why You Should Apply

For international students seeking genuinely comprehensive funding without the burden of managing separate university and scholarship applications, Türkiye Bursları offers a rare all-in-one package covering tuition, housing, stipend, language training, and travel, backed by an active alumni network of more than 150,000 graduates across government, academia, and business worldwide.

For full eligibility details and to begin an application, visit the official Türkiye Scholarships website: https://www.turkiyeburslari.gov.tr/

Benefits explained

Required documents

Valid passport or national ID, a recent photograph, academic transcripts and diplomas (officially translated if not in English or Turkish), two recommendation letters, a Letter of Intent/statement of purpose, and, for master's and PhD applicants, a research proposal; language test scores are only required for programmes specifically taught in English or another non-Turkish language.

Application process

Create an account on the Türkiye Scholarships portal (TBBS), complete the online application with personal, academic, and language information, upload all required documents, and select up to 12 preferred university programmes ranked by preference; the scholarship board reviews eligibility and academic merit, shortlists candidates for interviews (conducted via embassies, consulates, or YTB teams), and independently handles final university placement, so no separate university application is needed.
