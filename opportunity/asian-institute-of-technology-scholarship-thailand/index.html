Scholarship - Thailand

Asian Institute of Technology Scholarship

## Introduction

Bangkok's outskirts are home to one of Southeast Asia's most established postgraduate institutions built specifically around regional development challenges. The Asian Institute of Technology, known simply as AIT, has trained engineers, environmental scientists, and technology leaders from across Asia and beyond for decades, and its scholarship program makes that education considerably more affordable through partial to full tuition fee waivers. For students who want postgraduate study grounded in engineering, environmental, or technology fields relevant to a developing region, and who are comfortable with Thailand's notably lower cost of living, AIT offers a genuinely practical path into a master's or PhD.

## About the Opportunity

AIT operates as an international postgraduate institution in its own right, with English as the language of instruction and a student body drawn from across Asia and beyond. The scholarship itself is not a separate award layered on top of admission; it is a tuition fee waiver built directly into the university's own admissions process, applied automatically once an applicant is accepted and has indicated financial need or scholarship interest in their application.

## Benefits

Successful applicants receive a full or partial tuition fee waiver, depending on their academic profile and the specific scholarship tier they qualify for. What the waiver does not cover is worth being clear about: students remain responsible for a registration fee of roughly 20,000 Baht per semester and living costs generally in the range of 11,500 to 15,000 Baht per month, both of which are modest by international standards given Thailand's cost of living. Notably, AIT does not require a post-study work bond, a condition that some Asian government scholarships attach to their funding.

## Who Can Apply?

Master's applicants need a four-year bachelor's degree, while PhD applicants generally need a master's degree with a CGPA of at least 3.50, or, in some cases, a bachelor's degree with a CGPA of 2.75 from a recognized top university. English proficiency is required for all applicants, with AIT accepting IELTS or its own AIT EET test, requiring a score of 5.0 or higher for master's applicants and 5.5 or higher for PhD applicants.

## Fields of Study or Available Areas

AIT's academic strength lies in engineering, environmental science, management, and technology, reflecting its founding mission of supporting technical and development-focused education across the region. Applicants with backgrounds or career goals in these areas are well matched to what AIT offers.

## Required Documents

Applicants need to submit an online application, a CV, certified academic diplomas and transcripts, and their English proficiency test scores. Relevant publications, where an applicant has them, can also strengthen an application, particularly for PhD candidates.

## Application Process

There is no separate scholarship application at AIT. Applicants apply for admission to their intended master's or PhD program through the AIT online admission portal and indicate their interest in scholarship support directly within their statement of purpose. Admission and scholarship consideration are reviewed together, which means a strong, complete application to the academic program itself is the same thing as a strong scholarship application.

## Deadline

AIT accepts applications on a rolling basis rather than through a single annual deadline, so prospective students can apply throughout the year, though earlier applications generally have more time to secure a place in their preferred intake.

## Why You Should Apply

For students who want postgraduate study in engineering, environment, management, or technology without the cost of many Western universities, AIT's combination of tuition waivers and a genuinely low cost of living makes for one of the more accessible fully funded and partially funded scholarship options available in Asia. The absence of a post-study bond requirement also gives graduates more flexibility in where they take their careers afterward, a detail worth weighing against other regional scholarships that tie graduates to specific post-study commitments.

For full admission and scholarship details, visit the official AIT admissions page: https://www.ait.ac.th/admissions/scholarships/
