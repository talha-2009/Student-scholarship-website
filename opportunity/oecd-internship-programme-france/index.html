OECD Internship Programme

The OECD Internship Programme is a verified international internship opportunity for students who want practical exposure to public policy, economics, governance and international cooperation. The programme is hosted by the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development and brings students into policy and corporate teams that support evidence-based work with governments around the world. Interns may contribute to research, data collection, analysis, drafting, event support, communications and other project tasks depending on the directorate or unit that selects them.

This opportunity is especially useful for applicants who want to understand how international policy work is produced in practice. The OECD works across a wide range of themes, including climate change, digital transformation, education and skills, employment, finance, governance, health, trade, taxation, development and regional policy. A successful intern may help prepare studies and documents, review evidence, organise datasets, support publications or participate in meetings and seminars. The exact assignment depends on organisational needs, but the programme is designed to let motivated students build professional skills while contributing to the OECD mission of better policies for better lives.

Applicants must remain enrolled in a fully accredited degree programme during the whole internship. The official eligibility language includes bachelor's, master's and PhD students, and candidates are expected to have a strong command of at least one OECD official language, English or French, plus willingness or ability to work with the other. Quantitative, analytical and IT skills are valuable because many OECD teams use data, research methods and policy evidence in their daily work.

The application is submitted online through the OECD application platform. Candidates should prepare a focused cover letter that explains their academic background, policy interests, skills and availability. Because applications are considered on a rolling basis, there is no single fixed closing date. Applicants should still plan early: the OECD notes that selection can take up to three months, and candidates can update availability information after applying. The strongest applications will show a clear match between the applicant's studies and one or more OECD work areas.

Who should apply

Applicants must be enrolled in a fully accredited bachelor's, master's or doctoral degree programme for the full internship period, be available for at least one month, and have strong language, quantitative and IT skills relevant to OECD work.

Benefits explained

Interns work with OECD policy experts and specialists, contribute to research and policy outputs, build analytical skills in an international environment, and may work in person, hybrid or remotely depending on team needs. The OECD states that interns located in Paris receive a contribution to living expenses and annual leave.

Required documents

Online application form, cover letter entered in the application platform, availability information, and any supporting documents requested by the OECD recruitment platform.

Application process

Applicants complete the online application through the official OECD SmartRecruiters platform, including application details, cover letter and availability. The Internship Office screens applications and shortlisted profiles are made available to OECD directorates. Hiring teams may invite candidates for an interview or written test before issuing an internship offer and agreement.

Selection criteria

Selection is based on fit with OECD directorate needs, academic background, relevant skills, language ability, analytical potential, availability and interview or written-test performance.

Important notes

The OECD welcomes interns on an ongoing basis and allows applications throughout the year. Candidates should apply well before their preferred start period because the selection process can take up to three months.
