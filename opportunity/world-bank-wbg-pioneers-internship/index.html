World Bank Group WBG Pioneers Internship

Introduction

WBG Pioneers is the World Bank Group's flagship internship programme, placing undergraduate and postgraduate students directly on teams working across development finance, policy, research, and institutional operations. Unlike a training or observational placement, the World Bank Group is explicit that WBG Pioneers is a genuine professional internship, with interns contributing directly to ongoing workstreams supporting poverty reduction, shared prosperity, and sustainable development rather than shadowing staff from the sidelines.

About the Opportunity

Interns work within one of two tracks, an Undergraduate Track reserved for final-year bachelor's students, or a Postgraduate Track for those currently enrolled in a master's or PhD programme, contributing to real projects spanning health, education, economics, environment, agriculture, urban development, transport, digital development, energy, private sector development, and corporate support functions like accounting, communications, HR, IT, and finance. The programme runs two annual cycles, spring/summer (roughly April to September) and fall/winter (roughly October to March), placing interns either at World Bank Group headquarters in Washington, D.C., or at one of more than 20 country offices worldwide, with the specific work arrangement, on-site or remote, confirmed during selection.

Benefits

Interns receive an hourly salary for the duration of their placement, access to necessary World Bank Group facilities and IT systems, mentorship and on-the-job supervision, and structured professional development including skill-building workshops, knowledge sessions, and peer networking opportunities; housing, travel costs, and visa fees are not covered and remain the intern's own responsibility.

Who Can Apply?

Undergraduate Track applicants must be in their final year of a bachelor's degree, while Postgraduate Track applicants must be currently enrolled in a master's or PhD programme for the entire internship duration; applicants of any nationality with 0 to 6 years of professional experience can apply, though non-US citizens interning at Washington DC headquarters need valid US work authorization, and applicants with close relatives already working at the WBG face disclosure requirements and, in some cases, disqualification.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields span economics, public policy, finance, data science, environmental science, agriculture, public health, law, communications, gender studies, and corporate support functions, reflecting the genuinely broad scope of World Bank Group operations.

Required Documents

Applicants need a CV or resume (maximum two pages), a cover letter or statement of interest (maximum one page), and proof of eligibility through an education transcript and/or enrollment letter clearly confirming final-year undergraduate or current postgraduate status.

Application Process

Applicants create a profile on the World Bank Group careers portal, search current WBG Pioneers vacancies matching their academic background, and submit a complete application including CV, statement of interest, and enrollment proof for that specific posting; submitted applications are reviewed by the relevant WBG team based on business needs and role requirements, with only shortlisted candidates invited to further assessment before a final offer is extended.

Deadline

The Fall/Winter cycle application window is 13 July to 12 August 2026, for internships running roughly October 2026 through March 2027; the World Bank Group runs strictly two windows per year, so missing one means waiting for the next cycle roughly six months later.

Why You Should Apply

For students and early-career candidates genuinely committed to global development work, WBG Pioneers offers paid, hands-on experience directly on World Bank Group project teams, professional credibility recognized across development, government, and international organization career paths, and real exposure to how one of the world's largest multilateral development institutions actually operates.

For current openings and full application details, visit the official WBG Pioneers page: https://www.worldbank.org/ext/en/careers/talent-programs/wbg-pioneers
