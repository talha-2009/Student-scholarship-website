Global Undergraduate Exchange Program (Global UGRAD)

Introduction

Global UGRAD is the U.S. Department of State's primary vehicle for bringing current undergraduate students from around the world into American colleges and universities for a semester or full academic year, delivered through World Learning on behalf of the State Department. Unlike programmes open only to graduate students or established professionals, Global UGRAD is built specifically for undergraduates still partway through their degree, giving them a fully funded taste of American higher education without requiring them to transfer or restart their studies.

About the Opportunity

Participants spend a semester or a full academic year studying at a US host institution, continuing coursework relevant to their degree while gaining direct exposure to American academic life, campus culture, and, in many cases, a level of independence and civic engagement that differs from their home country's educational system. Because Global UGRAD is explicitly framed around building leadership and mutual understanding rather than simply funding study abroad, the programme typically includes activities and expectations around community engagement alongside standard coursework.

Benefits

Participants receive tuition, housing, a living stipend, and round-trip airfare, with visa support provided throughout the process, together covering the substantial costs that would otherwise make a semester or year in the US financially out of reach for many students.

Who Can Apply?

Current undergraduate students from Global UGRAD-eligible countries, with strong academic records and demonstrated leadership potential, are eligible, though the specific list of participating countries can change over time, so applicants should confirm their country's current status directly.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The programme supports multidisciplinary study, open to undergraduates across virtually any academic field, since participants continue coursework relevant to their existing degree programme rather than switching into a Global UGRAD-specific curriculum.

Required Documents

Applicants need academic transcripts, essays, recommendation letters, and English proficiency proof.

Application Process

Applicants apply through the U.S. Embassy or a designated partner organization in their home country, since Global UGRAD, like several other US State Department exchange programmes, runs through country-specific local processes rather than one central global application. Because timelines and specific requirements can vary by country, applicants should confirm the process that applies to them directly with their local embassy or partner organization.

Deadline

Deadlines vary by country and local implementing partner, so there is no single global date. Applicants should confirm the current cycle's deadline directly with their local US Embassy or partner organization.

Why You Should Apply

For undergraduates who want a substantial taste of American higher education without committing to a full degree transfer, Global UGRAD offers comprehensive funding and structured support for a semester or year abroad. Among exchange programs available to current undergraduates specifically, its explicit focus on leadership development alongside academic coursework distinguishes it from programmes that are purely academic in focus.

For country-specific application details, visit the official Global UGRAD programme page: https://www.worldlearning.org/program/global-ugrad/
