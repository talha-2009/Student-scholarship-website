McCall MacBain Scholarship

## Introduction The McCall MacBain Scholarship is McGill University's flagship graduate leadership award, launched in 2019 with a major gift from John and Marcy McCall MacBain and explicitly modeled on the Rhodes Scholarship's emphasis on leadership alongside academics. It funds a full-time master's or eligible second-entry professional degree at McGill in Montreal, paired with a structured leadership development programme that runs throughout your studies. It suits ambitious graduates or final-year undergraduates who want a genuinely leadership-focused scholarship experience, not just financial support for a Canadian master's. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** McCall MacBain Scholarships / McGill University - **Country of Study:** Canada (Montreal) - **Opportunity Type:** Fellowship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Master's (or eligible second-entry professional programme, 45+ credits) - **Duration:** Length of your McGill graduate programme - **Official Website:** mccallmacbainscholars.org ## Benefits - Full tuition and fees at McGill University - A living stipend of CAD 2,300 per month during academic terms - Mentorship and an interdisciplinary leadership development programme - Funded travel to attend the final interview stage in Montreal - Up to CAD 5,000 in summer funding ## Eligibility Criteria - Be an outstanding undergraduate student or recent graduate, generally within about five years of your first degree - Be applying to an eligible full-time master's programme or a second-entry professional programme of 45 or more credits at McGill - Demonstrate leadership, community engagement, and strong academic performance - Applicants of any nationality are eligible, since the scholarship is not restricted by citizenship ## Required Documents - Online McCall MacBain application, separate from McGill's own admissions system - Essays addressing leadership and community engagement - Two reference forms - Academic transcripts - A separate application to your intended McGill graduate programme ## Application Process **Step 1.** Complete the online application on the McCall MacBain Scholars website, which is a distinct process from McGill's general admissions. **Step 2.** Write your leadership and community-engagement essays and arrange two reference forms. **Step 3.** Separately apply for admission to your intended McGill master's or second-entry professional programme. **Step 4.** Shortlisted candidates advance to regional interviews. **Step 5.** Finalists are invited to a final interview stage in Montreal, with travel funded by the programme. **Step 6.** Successful scholars receive both admission to McGill and full scholarship funding together. ## Important Dates - **Application deadline:** See the deadline listed for this opportunity (typically mid-August) - **Regional interviews:** Following shortlisting - **Final interviews:** Held in Montreal - **Programme start:** The following academic year at McGill Always confirm the current cycle's exact dates on the official website, since both the scholarship and McGill admissions deadlines need to be tracked together. ## Selection Criteria McCall MacBain selection weighs demonstrated leadership and community impact as heavily as academic strength, assessed through essays, references, and a multi-stage interview process that includes both regional and final rounds in Montreal. ## Tips for Applicants - Apply to your McGill graduate programme and the McCall MacBain scholarship in parallel, since they are genuinely separate applications with their own requirements - Use your essays to describe specific instances of leadership and community engagement, not general aspirations - Choose references who can speak concretely to your leadership qualities, not only academic performance - Prepare for a multi-stage interview process, including a final round held in person in Montreal - Apply from any country — this scholarship is open to applicants of any nationality ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the McCall MacBain Scholarship?** Outstanding undergraduates or recent graduates of any nationality applying to an eligible McGill master's programme. **Is the McCall MacBain Scholarship fully funded?** Yes — tuition, a living stipend, and additional summer and travel funding are covered. **Do I apply to McGill and the scholarship separately?** Yes, they are two distinct applications that must both be completed. **How many interview stages are there?** Regional interviews followed by a final interview stage in Montreal. **Can international students apply?** Yes, the scholarship is open to applicants of any nationality. **What kind of McGill programmes are eligible?** Full-time master's programmes and eligible second-entry professional programmes with 45 or more credits. ## Apply Now Start your application on the official McCall MacBain Scholars website: https://www.mccallmacbainscholars.org/

McCall MacBain Scholarship Eligibility

The McCall MacBain Scholarship is open to applicants of all nationalities who hold a bachelor's degree with a minimum GPA of 3.5 out of 4.0 (or equivalent international qualification). You must be applying to an eligible master's or second-entry professional programme at McGill University. There are no age or citizenship restrictions. The selection committee prioritises leadership potential, community engagement, and intellectual curiosity alongside academic excellence. Both Canadian/international applicants and US citizens apply through the same portal, though with different deadlines.

McCall MacBain Scholarship Benefits and Funding

The scholarship is fully funded and covers full tuition for the duration of your McGill programme, a monthly living stipend of approximately CAD 2,300, and a one-time relocation grant to help with initial settling costs. Beyond financial support, scholars gain access to an intensive leadership development programme including workshops, mentoring from senior leaders, networking events, and a lifelong membership in the McCall MacBain Scholars community. Up to 30 scholars are selected each year.

McCall MacBain Scholarship Deadline and Timeline

For the 2027 cohort, the application portal opens on 1 June 2026. The deadline is 19 August 2026 for applicants outside Canada and the US, and 23 September 2026 for Canadian and US citizens and graduates. Shortlisted candidates are invited to a Selection Day in Montreal (typically in November), and final offers are extended in December. This scholarship is separate from McGill's admissions process — you apply to McCall MacBain independently. Always confirm dates on the official website.

Required Documents for the McCall MacBain Scholarship

  • Online application form — submitted through the McCall MacBain Scholars portal
  • Academic transcripts — from all post-secondary institutions attended
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV) — detailing education, work experience, and leadership activities
  • Leadership essay — responding to the programme's prompt about your leadership experience and vision
  • Two reference letters — from individuals who can speak to your leadership potential and character
  • McGill programme selection — indicate your intended master's or professional programme at McGill
  • Proof of degree — bachelor's degree certificate or confirmation of completion

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Submitting a generic leadership essay — the selection committee looks for specific examples of leadership impact, not abstract statements. Use concrete stories that demonstrate your approach to leading others.
  2. Applying after the wrong deadline — there are two separate deadlines depending on your citizenship status. International applicants (outside Canada/US) have the earlier 19 August deadline.
  3. Neglecting the McGill programme choice — you must apply to a specific eligible McGill programme. Research the programme thoroughly and explain why it aligns with your goals.
  4. Underestimating the leadership requirement — academic excellence alone is not enough. The McCall MacBain Scholarship specifically seeks leaders who have made a tangible impact in their communities.
