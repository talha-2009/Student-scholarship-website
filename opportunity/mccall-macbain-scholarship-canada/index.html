McCall MacBain Scholarship

Introduction

McGill University's McCall MacBain Scholarship is Canada's answer to the Rhodes Scholarship, launched in 2019 with a major gift from John and Marcy McCall MacBain and explicitly modeled on that same combination of academic funding and structured leadership development. Unlike scholarships that simply pay tuition, McCall MacBain layers a genuine leadership programme on top of a fully funded McGill master's degree, running throughout a scholar's time in Montreal rather than existing as a one-off orientation event.

About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue a full-time master's or an eligible second-entry professional programme with at least 45 credits at McGill, while also participating in interdisciplinary leadership programming designed to build skills and connections across the entire scholar cohort, not just within their specific academic department. The selection process itself reflects this dual focus, with candidates evaluated on both academic strength and evidence of genuine leadership and community engagement, assessed through multiple stages culminating in interviews held in person in Montreal.

Benefits

Scholars receive full tuition and fees at McGill, a living stipend of CAD 2,300 per month during academic terms, mentorship and interdisciplinary leadership programming, funded travel to attend the final interview stage in Montreal, and up to CAD 5,000 in additional summer funding to support projects or activities outside the standard academic year.

Who Can Apply?

Outstanding undergraduate students or recent graduates, generally within about five years of their first degree, applying to an eligible full-time McGill master's or second-entry professional programme can apply, with the scholarship weighing demonstrated leadership, community engagement, and academic strength together rather than academics alone.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The scholarship covers all academic fields available through eligible McGill master's and second-entry professional programmes, giving scholars genuine flexibility in choosing their area of study.

Required Documents

Applicants need an online application with leadership and community-engagement essays, two reference forms, transcripts, and a separate application to their intended McGill graduate programme.

Application Process

Applicants complete the online McCall MacBain application, separate from McGill's own admissions system, submitting essays and two references, then apply separately to an eligible McGill graduate programme; finalists progress through regional interviews before a final interview round held in person in Montreal.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 19 August 2026. Given the multi-stage interview process and the separate McGill admissions application required alongside it, candidates should begin preparing well ahead of this date.

Why You Should Apply

For graduates of any nationality who want a fully funded McGill master's combined with genuine leadership development rather than funding alone, McCall MacBain offers one of the most comprehensive scholarship packages available in Canada, backed by a structured programme that continues throughout a scholar's entire time in Montreal.

For full application details, visit the official McCall MacBain Scholars website: https://www.mccallmacbainscholars.org/
