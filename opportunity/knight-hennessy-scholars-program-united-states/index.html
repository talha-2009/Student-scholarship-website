Knight-Hennessy Scholars Program

Introduction

Stanford University has long attracted ambitious graduate students, but Knight-Hennessy Scholars adds something most universities cannot offer on their own: a fully funded, multidisciplinary leadership community layered on top of any Stanford graduate degree, from law to medicine to engineering to the humanities. Established in 2018 with a gift from Phil Knight and named for former Stanford president John Hennessy, the program exists on the premise that the world's hardest problems rarely respect the boundaries between academic disciplines, and that future leaders need practice working across them. For admitted Stanford graduate students of any field, it offers full funding and a genuinely cross-disciplinary cohort experience that few peer fellowships attempt to replicate.

About the Opportunity

Knight-Hennessy is not a degree program in its own right. It is a fellowship that sits on top of an existing Stanford graduate degree, whichever school or program a scholar is admitted to. What makes it distinctive is that scholars from Stanford Law, the medical school, the business school, engineering, and the humanities all move through the same leadership development program together, attending shared seminars, workshops, and events specifically designed to build skills in collaborating across professional and disciplinary lines. The goal is to produce graduates who leave Stanford not only with deep expertise in their own field but with real, practiced experience working alongside people trained very differently from themselves.

Benefits

Scholars receive up to three years of tuition funding, which for many Stanford graduate programs represents a substantial reduction in the overall cost of the degree. Alongside tuition, Knight-Hennessy provides a living and academic expense stipend intended to cover day-to-day costs during study, plus an annual travel stipend covering trips to and from Stanford. On top of the financial support, scholars gain access to the multidisciplinary leadership development program itself, along with mentorship and a lasting network of peers across every corner of the university, benefits that extend well past graduation into scholars' subsequent careers.

Who Can Apply?

Knight-Hennessy is open to applicants of any nationality and any undergraduate background, a deliberately broad eligibility profile compared with many peer fellowships. The core requirement is straightforward but firm: applicants must apply to, be accepted by, and enroll in a full-time Stanford graduate degree program, since the fellowship cannot exist independently of that admission. Applicants should generally have earned their most recent bachelor's degree within roughly the last seven years, with some flexibility built in for those who served in the military during that window. No university endorsement or nomination is required, which means candidates can apply on their own initiative rather than needing institutional support to be considered.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Because the fellowship attaches to any Stanford graduate program, the range of eligible fields is essentially the full breadth of what Stanford offers at the graduate level: law, medicine, business, engineering, education, the humanities, and the sciences are all represented among Knight-Hennessy cohorts. Applicants do not choose a Knight-Hennessy-specific track; they choose their intended Stanford graduate program independently, and the fellowship follows from that admission.

Required Documents

Applicants prepare a Knight-Hennessy-specific online application that includes personal essays exploring their background, motivations, and leadership experience, alongside academic transcripts and letters of recommendation. Because Knight-Hennessy candidacy depends on Stanford graduate admission, applicants also need to complete a separate application to their intended Stanford program, which carries its own set of required materials specific to that school or department.

Application Process

Prospective scholars begin by identifying which Stanford graduate school and program fits their academic and professional goals, since that choice shapes the rest of the process. From there, they submit two applications in parallel: one to the Stanford graduate program itself, following that program's standard admissions process, and a separate application directly to Knight-Hennessy Scholars, including essays and recommendation letters. Shortlisted Knight-Hennessy candidates are invited to an interview stage, often structured around group exercises designed to observe how candidates think and collaborate in real time, not just how they answer questions one-on-one. Final scholar selections are confirmed once both the Stanford admission decision and the Knight-Hennessy review are complete.

Deadline

The Knight-Hennessy application deadline typically falls in early October, aligned with the broader cycle of Stanford graduate admissions for the following academic year. Because two separate applications need to come together, applicants should start both processes well ahead of the deadline rather than treating them as sequential steps, and should confirm the current cycle's exact date directly on the Knight-Hennessy website before finalizing their plans.

Why You Should Apply

Few fellowships combine full funding with the kind of genuinely cross-disciplinary community that Knight-Hennessy offers. For a strong candidate who has already secured, or is confident of securing, admission to a Stanford graduate program, the fellowship substantially changes the financial calculus of that degree while adding a leadership development experience most single-department programs simply do not provide. Among fully funded scholarships and fellowships for graduate study in the United States, Knight-Hennessy stands out for its openness to any nationality, any field, and its explicit bet that tomorrow's hardest problems will be solved by people who know how to work across disciplines, not just within one.

For full eligibility rules, application materials, and current deadlines, visit the official Knight-Hennessy Scholars website: https://knight-hennessy.stanford.edu/admission
