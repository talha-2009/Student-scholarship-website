Scholarship - South Korea

Global Korea Scholarship (GKS)

Introduction

South Korea's Global Korea Scholarship, run by the National Institute for International Education, funds international students at every degree level, from undergraduate through PhD, reflecting the country's sustained two-decade investment in building its global academic reputation. Structured around either embassy or university-recommended tracks, GKS gives applicants two genuinely different pathways into Korean higher education depending on how they choose to apply, a structural choice that shapes not just the application process but the entire experience of preparing for and starting the programme.

About the Opportunity

Scholars typically begin with a year of Korean language training before starting their main degree programme, an intentional design choice that recognizes language readiness as a real practical barrier for many prospective international students who might otherwise be deterred from studying in Korea despite academic interest. From there, scholars proceed into their chosen bachelor's, master's, or PhD programme at a partner Korean university, supported throughout by comprehensive funding that removes most of the usual financial calculations international students face. Because Korea has invested heavily in its universities' global rankings and research output over the past two decades, GKS scholars frequently find themselves at genuinely competitive institutions rather than lesser-known regional universities.

Benefits

GKS covers full tuition, a monthly stipend, a one-time settlement allowance upon arrival, round-trip airfare, Korean language training, and health insurance for the full duration of study, a benefits package that is unusually comprehensive even among fully funded government scholarships internationally.

Who Can Apply?

Undergraduate applicants generally need to be under 25, while graduate applicants need to be under 40, applying either through government or university nomination or directly through Korean embassies. Specific tracks include university-recommended, embassy-recommended, and exchange student pathways, each with somewhat different processes, application windows, and, in some cases, different quotas by country.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

GKS covers all academic fields, giving scholars flexibility to pursue study across the full range of programmes offered by partner Korean universities, from engineering and natural sciences through business, humanities, and Korean studies specifically.

Required Documents

Applicants need an application form, academic transcripts and certificates, a personal statement, a study or research plan, recommendation letters, and a health certificate confirming their fitness to study abroad.

Application Process

Applicants apply either via the Korean Embassy in their home country or through a partnered Korean university, depending on which track, embassy-recommended or university-recommended, fits their situation, since the two pathways involve genuinely different procedures, documents, and timelines that should not be confused with one another.

Deadline

Because GKS runs through many separate embassy and university channels rather than one central portal, there is no single global deadline; applicants should confirm the current date directly with their chosen track's administering office well in advance of when they hope to begin their language training or degree programme.

Why You Should Apply

For students at any degree level considering study in Asia, GKS offers comprehensive funding rarely matched by other national scholarships, plus built-in language training that meaningfully lowers the practical barrier to studying in Korea specifically, a country whose universities have climbed steadily in global rankings and research reputation over the past two decades.

For full details, visit the official Study in Korea website: https://www.studyinkorea.go.kr/in/plan/scholarship.do
