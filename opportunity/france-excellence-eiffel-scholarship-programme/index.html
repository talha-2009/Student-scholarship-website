Scholarship - France

France Excellence Eiffel Scholarship Programme

## Introduction

Developed by the French Ministry for Europe and Foreign Affairs, the France Excellence Eiffel Scholarship exists to help French higher education institutions attract the world's top international students into master's and PhD programmes, specifically training future decision-makers in the public and private sectors. Its defining structural feature sets it apart from most scholarships: students cannot apply to Eiffel directly at all, since only French institutions themselves may submit candidate applications to Campus France, which administers the programme.

## About the Opportunity

Students must first secure admission, or be in the process of applying, to a specific master's or PhD programme at a participating French institution in one of the programme's priority fields, then have that institution select and submit their file to Campus France on their behalf. Because each institution can submit only a limited number of candidates each year, competition happens twice, once for admission to the programme itself, and again for the institution's own internal selection of who to put forward for Eiffel funding.

## Benefits

Scholars receive a monthly allowance, roughly €1,200 at master's level and up to about €2,100 at PhD level, along with international and national transport assistance, insurance, and support finding housing; tuition fees themselves are not covered and remain the responsibility of the student or institution.

## Who Can Apply?

Applicants must be non-French foreign nationals, since dual nationals holding French citizenship are not eligible; master's applicants must be 29 or younger and not already studying in France, while PhD applicants must be 35 or younger and applying for the first year of a co-supervised thesis, and no applicant may already hold another French government, Erasmus+, or AUF scholarship.

## Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eiffel covers two major disciplinary groups across seven specific fields: science and technology (biology and health, ecological transition, mathematics and digital, science and engineering) and human and social sciences (French history, language and civilization, law and political science, and economics and management).

## Required Documents

Applicants need their standard admission application to the chosen French institution and programme, a CV, academic transcripts and diplomas showing class ranking, and, for most PhD applicants, a signed thesis co-supervision agreement between the French and foreign institutions involved.

## Application Process

Applicants first apply for admission to a specific programme at a participating French institution and indicate interest in Eiffel funding within that application; the institution reviews candidates for academic excellence, then submits its selected files directly to Campus France, meaning strong institutional support is essential and individual applications sent directly to Campus France are not accepted.

## Deadline

The current application deadline for institutions to submit files to Campus France is 8 January 2027, though individual institutions typically set their own earlier internal deadlines for students to apply through them, often in November.

## Why You Should Apply

For outstanding international students targeting a specific French institution and priority field, Eiffel offers meaningful monthly funding and a genuinely prestigious government-backed credential, though success depends on engaging directly and early with your target institution's international relations office, since that relationship, not a direct Campus France application, determines whether you are even considered.

For full details, visit the official Campus France Eiffel programme page: https://www.campusfrance.org/en/france-excellence-eiffel-scholarship-program
