OECD Co-operative Research Programme Fellowship

Introduction

The OECD's Co-operative Research Programme on Sustainable Agricultural and Food Systems funds short-term international research fellowships for scientists working on agriculture, forestry, fisheries, and food systems, with a specific goal of building lasting scientific partnerships across its 32 participating countries. According to the OECD itself, more than 75 percent of funded fellowships lead to ongoing collaboration between fellows and their host institutions well after the fellowship period ends, reflecting the programme's emphasis on long-term relationship-building over a single short visit.

About the Opportunity

Fellows spend between six and 26 weeks working with a host laboratory in another participating CRP country, conducting research aligned with the programme's priority areas, which span sustainable productivity and food security, climate risk and emissions in agriculture, forest loss and land degradation, and the environmental impacts of livestock production. Because the CRP's objective is explicitly both scientific and policy-relevant, informing the OECD Committee for Agriculture and related bodies, proposals are expected to demonstrate real potential to influence future policy discussions, not purely academic interest.

Benefits

Fellows receive a travel lump sum for return economy-class airfare, a weekly subsistence allowance of roughly EUR 600 to 650 depending on the host country's cost of living, and a EUR 165 terminal allowance to help cover transport costs at the start and end of the fellowship; no other costs are covered.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need to be research scientists working in agriculture, forestry, fisheries, or sustainable food systems, resident in a participating CRP country, generally with a PhD and around four years of postdoctoral training, though exceptional non-PhD applicants with equivalent published expertise may be considered; applicants must have a continuing employment contract ensuring their job is secure after the fellowship, and cannot apply to remain in a laboratory where they already hold a position.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible research areas include sustainable agriculture, food security, climate change, biodiversity, forestry, fisheries, aquaculture, soil health, livestock systems, and related natural resource management topics.

Required Documents

Applicants need the completed online CRP application form, a research proposal explaining the project and its relevance to CRP action areas, a CV, and confirmation or an invitation letter from the proposed host institution.

Application Process

Applicants first review the CRP Guidelines, Selection Criteria, and Action Areas, then identify and confirm a host institution in another participating country before submitting the online application, addressing every published selection criterion explicitly since the programme's Governing Body makes final decisions based on written applications alone each December.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 10 September 2026 at midnight Paris time, for fellowships to be funded and undertaken in 2027.

Why You Should Apply

For experienced agricultural, forestry, or fisheries researchers who want to build genuine international collaborations rather than a one-off research trip, the CRP fellowship offers meaningful travel and subsistence support alongside strong odds of a lasting partnership, backed by the OECD's own data showing most fellowships lead to continued collaboration.

For the full list of participating countries and current guidelines, visit the official OECD CRP fellowships page: https://www.oecd.org/en/about/programmes/co-operative-research-programme/crp-applications-for-fellowships.html
