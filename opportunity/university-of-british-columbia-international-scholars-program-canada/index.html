University of British Columbia International Scholars Program

## Introduction The University of British Columbia International Scholars Program recognises outstanding international students entering UBC undergraduate programmes with renewable tuition awards that can significantly reduce the cost of studying at one of Canada's top research universities. Most awards within the programme are considered automatically based on your admission application, though a small number of specific awards — such as the International Major Entrance Scholarship — require nomination or an additional application step. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** University of British Columbia - **Country of Study:** Canada (Vancouver or Okanagan campus) - **Opportunity Type:** Scholarship - **Funding Type:** Partially Funded - **Degree Level:** Undergraduate - **Duration:** Renewable for up to four years - **Official Website:** you.ubc.ca ## Benefits - Renewable tuition awards ranging from partial funding to full tuition coverage - Awards can be renewed for up to four years of undergraduate study, depending on the specific award tier ## Eligibility Criteria - Be an outstanding international student admitted to a UBC undergraduate programme - Be selected based on academic excellence and personal achievement - Some specific awards, such as the International Major Entrance Scholarship (IMES), require a nomination or a separate application beyond standard admission ## Required Documents - UBC undergraduate admission application - Academic transcripts - A personal profile/essay - English proficiency evidence ## Application Process **Step 1.** Apply for undergraduate admission to UBC, ensuring your personal profile/essay reflects your academic and personal achievements clearly. **Step 2.** Most International Scholars Program awards are automatically considered based on this application alone. **Step 3.** For specific awards like IMES that require nomination, check whether your high school or UBC itself handles this step, and complete any additional forms required. **Step 4.** UBC reviews eligible applicants against the programme's academic and personal achievement criteria. **Step 5.** Successful students receive an award offer specifying the tuition benefit and renewal terms. ## Important Dates Please refer to the official website for current UBC undergraduate admission deadlines, since scholarship consideration is generally tied to when you submit your admission application. ## Selection Criteria UBC selects International Scholars Program recipients based on academic excellence shown in your transcripts and the strength of your personal profile/essay, which is assessed for evidence of personal achievement and potential contribution to the UBC community. ## Tips for Applicants - Submit a strong, specific personal profile/essay, since it is assessed directly for most awards within this programme - Apply for UBC admission as early as possible, since scholarship consideration is generally linked to your admission timeline - Check specifically whether IMES or another nominated award applies to your situation, since it has its own separate process - Maintain the academic performance required to renew your award across your four years of study - Confirm your English proficiency evidence meets UBC's specific requirements ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the UBC International Scholars Program?** Outstanding international students admitted to UBC undergraduate programmes. **Is it fully funded?** No, it is partially funded, ranging from partial tuition support to full tuition coverage depending on the award. **Do I need to apply separately?** Most awards are automatic based on your admission application; a few, like IMES, require nomination or extra steps. **How long can the award last?** It is renewable for up to four years of undergraduate study. **Does it cover graduate study?** No, this programme is specifically for undergraduate students. **What campus does this apply to?** UBC's Vancouver or Okanagan campus, depending on your admission. ## Apply Now Apply for UBC undergraduate admission and review award details on the official UBC scholarships page: https://you.ubc.ca/financial-planning/scholarships-awards-international-students/international-scholars/
