Fellowship - Austria

Richard Plaschka Fellowship

## Introduction

The Richard Plaschka Fellowship is OeAD's dedicated award for young historians whose research focuses on Austrian history, open to postdocs and, notably, to pre-doc researchers from countries without a formal Habilitation system, which broadens access beyond the narrower postdoctoral requirement common in comparable German-language academic fellowships. It funds a genuine research stay inside Austrian academic infrastructure, giving historians direct access to archives and departments central to Austrian historiography.

## Opportunity Overview

- **Organization:** OeAD (Austria's Agency for Education and Internationalisation) - **Country of Study:** Austria - **Opportunity Type:** Fellowship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Postdoctoral / University Lecturer - **Duration:** Up to 12 months initially, extendable to 18 months after positive evaluation - **Official Website:** oead.at

## Benefits

- An initial fellowship award of up to 12 months, extendable to 18 months following a positive evaluation - Placement as a visiting researcher at Austrian university departments, libraries, archives, or research institutions - Financial support for your research stay in Austria

## Eligibility Criteria

- Be a young university teacher or researcher in the historical sciences, with a focus on Austrian history - Postdocs are eligible, as are pre-doc researchers from countries without a Habilitation system - Hold strong academic qualifications appropriate to historical research at this level

## Required Documents

- OeAD application form - A research proposal focused on Austrian history - CV - Academic transcripts and degree certificates - Recommendation letters

## Application Process

**Step 1.** Develop a research proposal specifically centred on Austrian history.

**Step 2.** Register and apply through the OeAD online application tool (grants.at).

**Step 3.** Submit your application form, proposal, CV, transcripts, and recommendation letters by the published deadline, typically 15 September.

**Step 4.** OeAD reviews applications for academic quality and fit with the fellowship's historical-research focus.

**Step 5.** If awarded, you begin an initial 12-month fellowship at a matched Austrian host institution.

**Step 6.** A positive evaluation partway through can extend your fellowship to a total of 18 months.

## Important Dates

- **Application deadline:** See the deadline listed for this opportunity (typically 15 September) - **Fellowship period:** Up to 12 months initially, with a possible extension to 18 months

Confirm the exact current-cycle deadline on the official OeAD page each year.

## Selection Criteria

OeAD evaluates the academic strength and specificity of your proposed Austrian history research, your existing qualifications as a historian, and, for extension purposes partway through the fellowship, the quality of your ongoing research progress.

## Tips for Applicants

- If you are a pre-doc researcher from a country without a Habilitation system, note explicitly in your application that you are eligible under this specific provision - Focus your proposal tightly on Austrian history rather than broader European or general historical topics - Plan for the possibility of extension: keep your research well-documented from the start, since a positive evaluation is required to move from 12 to 18 months - Identify a specific Austrian archive, library, or department relevant to your topic and mention it directly in your proposal - Apply well ahead of the 15 September deadline to allow time for complete documentation

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Who can apply for the Richard Plaschka Fellowship?** Young historians researching Austrian history, including postdocs and pre-docs from countries without a Habilitation system.

**Is the Richard Plaschka Fellowship fully funded?** Yes — it covers your research stay in Austria for up to 12-18 months.

**How long does the fellowship last?** An initial 12 months, extendable to 18 months after positive evaluation.

**Can pre-doc researchers apply?** Yes, specifically those from countries without a Habilitation system.

**What is the application deadline?** Typically 15 September each year.

**Where will I be based?** At an Austrian university department, library, archive, or research institution relevant to your topic.

## Apply Now

Submit your application through the official OeAD grants portal: https://oead.at/en/to-austria/grants-and-scholarships/richard-plaschka-grant
