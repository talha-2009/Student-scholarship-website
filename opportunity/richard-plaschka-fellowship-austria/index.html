Fellowship - Austria

Richard Plaschka Fellowship

Introduction

Austria's national agency for education and internationalisation, OeAD, runs the Richard Plaschka Fellowship specifically for young historians whose research focuses on Austrian history, with a notably inclusive eligibility rule: pre-doc researchers from countries without a formal Habilitation system are welcome alongside postdocs, broadening access beyond the narrower postdoctoral requirement common in many comparable German-language academic fellowships.

About the Opportunity

Fellows work as visiting researchers at Austrian university departments, libraries, archives, or research institutions, gaining direct access to primary sources and academic communities central to Austrian historiography. The fellowship begins with an initial award period, extendable to a longer stay following a positive evaluation partway through, giving fellows a genuine incentive to demonstrate strong research progress during their initial months.

Benefits

Fellows receive an initial award of up to 12 months, extendable to 18 months after positive evaluation, to work as a visiting researcher at Austrian institutions relevant to their research.

Who Can Apply?

Young university teachers or researchers in the historical sciences focused on Austrian history can apply, including both postdocs and pre-doc researchers from countries without a Habilitation system.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The fellowship is narrowly focused on historical sciences with an Austrian history emphasis, distinguishing it from broader European history fellowships.

Required Documents

Applicants need an application form, a research proposal, a CV, academic transcripts or degree certificates, and recommendation letters.

Application Process

Applicants apply through the OeAD online application tool, known as grants.at, with a research proposal focused specifically on Austrian history by the published deadline.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 15 September 2026.

Why You Should Apply

For historians whose work centers specifically on Austrian history, few fellowships offer this level of direct access to Austrian archives and institutions, with an eligibility structure that meaningfully includes pre-doc researchers often excluded from comparable postdoctoral fellowships.

For full application details, visit the official OeAD Richard Plaschka Fellowship page: https://oead.at/en/to-austria/grants-and-scholarships/richard-plaschka-grant
