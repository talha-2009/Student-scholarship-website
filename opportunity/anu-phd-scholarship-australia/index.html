Scholarship - Australia

ANU PhD Scholarship

Introduction

The Australian National University sits at the heart of Canberra's policy and research community, and its PhD Scholarship is the university's main vehicle for funding doctoral researchers across every field it offers. For prospective PhD candidates weighing where to pursue advanced research in Australia, ANU's scholarship stands out for combining a strong living stipend with, in many rounds, a fee offset for international students, all built directly into the university's standard PhD admission process.

About the Opportunity

Rather than running as a separate competition after admission, the ANU PhD Scholarship is woven into the university's Higher Degree by Research application itself. Once admitted to a PhD program, scholars typically receive a stipend for the full expected duration of their doctorate, generally three to three and a half years, giving them the financial stability to focus on research without needing separate part-time work to cover living costs.

Benefits

The scholarship provides a living stipend for the scholarship period, generally three to three and a half years, covering day-to-day costs while a scholar completes their doctoral research. Depending on the specific admission round, many international scholars also receive a fee offset, reducing or eliminating the tuition costs that would otherwise apply, though the exact terms can vary from round to round.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need to be admitted, or in the process of applying, to a Higher Degree by Research at ANU, with a strong academic record supporting that admission. International scholars are eligible but must commit to full-time study for the duration of the scholarship. ANU specifically recommends that prospective applicants contact a potential supervisor before submitting a formal application, since supervisor availability and fit play a real role in both admission and scholarship outcomes.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The scholarship covers doctoral research across ANU's full range of academic fields, from the sciences and engineering through the humanities, social sciences, and policy studies, reflecting the university's broad research profile.

Required Documents

Applicants typically need academic transcripts, a research proposal outlining their intended doctoral project, a CV, references, and, for international applicants, evidence of English language proficiency.

Application Process

Prospective candidates are encouraged to identify and contact a prospective ANU supervisor before formally applying, since this early conversation shapes both the feasibility of the proposed research and the strength of the eventual application. Formal applications go through the ANU Scholarships and Graduate Research Admission system, where candidates indicate interest in the scholarship as part of their broader PhD application rather than through a separate scholarship-only process. Because ANU runs multiple scholarship rounds through the year rather than one single annual deadline, applicants should confirm which round fits their intended start date.

Deadline

The current application deadline for this scholarship is 31 August 2026. Because ANU offers multiple rounds throughout the year, prospective applicants who miss this date should check the university's scholarships page for the next available round rather than assuming a full year's wait.

Why You Should Apply

For doctoral candidates who want strong financial support built directly into their admission rather than a separate competitive scholarship application, ANU's PhD Scholarship offers a relatively straightforward path to funded research at one of Australia's leading universities. Its integration with the standard PhD admissions process, combined with ANU's emphasis on early supervisor contact, rewards candidates who take the time to build a genuine research relationship before applying, making early outreach to a prospective supervisor the single most useful step for anyone seriously considering this opportunity.

For full details on current scholarship rounds, visit the official ANU Scholarships page: https://study.anu.edu.au/scholarships/find-scholarship/anu-phd-scholarship
