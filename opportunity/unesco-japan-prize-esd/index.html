UNESCO-Japan Prize on Education for Sustainable Development

Introduction

The UNESCO-Japan Prize on Education for Sustainable Development recognises outstanding projects worldwide that advance education for sustainable development, funded by the Government of Japan and awarded annually at a UNESCO ceremony. Unlike individually-applied competitions, this prize works entirely through nomination, organizations, institutions, or individuals must be put forward by a National Commission for UNESCO in their country, making that national body the essential gateway to the prize.

About the Opportunity

Nominated ESD projects are reviewed by an international jury for demonstrated impact and innovation, with three laureates selected each year to receive both prize money and international recognition at a formal UNESCO ceremony, giving winning projects meaningful visibility beyond their home country.

Benefits

Three laureates each receive USD 50,000 and international recognition at a UNESCO award ceremony.

Who Can Apply?

Organizations, institutions, or individuals implementing ESD projects can be considered, provided they are nominated through a National Commission for UNESCO in their country.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The prize covers education and sustainability broadly, welcoming any project genuinely advancing education for sustainable development.

Required Documents

Nominees need a project description, a nomination letter from their National Commission, and supporting evidence of impact.

Application Process

Nominations must be submitted through a National Commission for UNESCO in the applicant's country; there is no direct individual application route.

Deadline

Deadlines vary by nomination cycle; interested organizations should contact their National Commission for the current timeline.

Why You Should Apply

For organizations and individuals with a genuinely impactful ESD project, this prize offers significant international recognition and prize money, though nomination through a National Commission is the essential first step.

For full details, visit the official UNESCO ESD Prize page: https://www.unesco.org/en/prizes/esd
