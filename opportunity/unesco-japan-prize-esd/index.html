UNESCO-Japan Prize on Education for Sustainable Development

## Introduction

The UNESCO-Japan Prize on Education for Sustainable Development recognises outstanding projects worldwide that advance education for sustainable development (ESD), funded by the Government of Japan and awarded annually at a UNESCO ceremony. Unlike individually-applied competitions, this prize works entirely through nomination — organizations, institutions, or individuals must be put forward by a National Commission for UNESCO in their country, making that national body the essential gateway to the prize.

## Opportunity Overview

- **Organization:** UNESCO - **Country of Study:** Global - **Opportunity Type:** Competition - **Funding Type:** Unpaid - **Degree Level:** Open to all - **Duration:** A single annual award cycle - **Official Website:** unesco.org/en/prizes/esd

## Benefits

- Three laureates each receive USD 50,000 - International recognition at a UNESCO award ceremony

## Eligibility Criteria

- Be an organization, institution, or individual implementing education for sustainable development (ESD) projects - Be nominated through a National Commission for UNESCO in your country

## Required Documents

- A project description - A nomination letter from your National Commission - Supporting evidence of the project's impact

## Application Process

**Step 1.** Identify the National Commission for UNESCO in your country, since nomination through this body is required.

**Step 2.** Prepare a clear project description and supporting evidence documenting your ESD project's impact.

**Step 3.** Submit your materials to your National Commission for review and potential nomination.

**Step 4.** If nominated, your National Commission forwards your submission to UNESCO for international judging.

**Step 5.** An international jury selects three laureates each year, who are honoured at a UNESCO award ceremony.

## Important Dates

Please refer to the official website for the current cycle's nomination deadline, since timing runs through National Commissions and can vary.

## Selection Criteria

The international jury evaluates the demonstrated impact, innovation, and sustainability of the nominated ESD project, alongside its potential to be replicated or scaled in other contexts, based on the evidence provided in the nomination.

## Tips for Applicants

- Contact your National Commission for UNESCO early, since nomination — not individual application — is the only path into this prize - Document your project's impact with concrete evidence, such as data, testimonials, or measurable outcomes - Explain clearly how your project could be replicated or adapted elsewhere, since this strengthens the case for international recognition - Build a relationship with your National Commission well ahead of the nomination deadline, since they need time to review and forward your materials - Highlight genuine innovation in your ESD approach, not just general sustainability education activity

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Who can win the UNESCO-Japan Prize on ESD?** Organizations, institutions, or individuals implementing ESD projects, nominated through a National Commission for UNESCO.

**Can I apply directly to UNESCO?** No, nomination through your country's National Commission for UNESCO is required.

**Is there prize money?** Yes, three laureates each receive USD 50,000.

**How many winners are selected each year?** Three laureates.

**What kind of projects qualify?** Projects that advance education for sustainable development.

**Where is the award given?** At an annual UNESCO award ceremony.

## Apply Now

Contact your National Commission for UNESCO to explore nomination, and review full details on the official UNESCO ESD Prize page: https://www.unesco.org/en/prizes/esd
