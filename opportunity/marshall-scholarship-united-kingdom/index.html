Marshall Scholarship

## Introduction

Created by the British Parliament in 1953 as a living gesture of thanks for American assistance under the Marshall Plan after the Second World War, the Marshall Scholarship funds American citizens to pursue postgraduate study at any UK university, in virtually any subject. Unlike scholarships tied to a single institution, such as Rhodes' historical connection to Oxford, Marshall's genuine flexibility in choice of university and course, combined with its openness to applicants of any gender since its founding, has made it one of the most respected postgraduate awards available to US citizens specifically.

## About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue one to three years of postgraduate study at a UK university of their own choosing, selected through a national competition that begins with campus nomination at their home institution rather than a direct national application. Because Marshall does not restrict scholars to a single university, cohorts of scholars end up spread across the UK's higher education system, studying everything from the sciences through law, humanities, and public policy, though they remain connected through the Marshall Scholarship community and its associated events and programming throughout their time in Britain.

## Benefits

Scholars receive university fees, a living stipend, an annual book grant, a thesis grant, research and travel grants, and airfare to and from the United States, for one to three years of study, a benefits package designed to support not just tuition but the full range of costs associated with serious postgraduate research or coursework.

## Who Can Apply?

US citizens who are current undergraduates or recent graduates, endorsed by their home university through its internal nomination process, can apply, with the scholarship open to applicants of any gender, a deliberate departure from some historic postgraduate scholarships that originally restricted eligibility.

## Fields of Study or Available Areas

Marshall funds study across nearly any subject offered by UK universities, giving scholars genuine freedom in choosing both their field and their institution, rather than steering candidates toward a narrower set of pre-approved courses.

## Required Documents

Applicants need their campus nomination, a personal statement, academic transcripts, recommendation letters, and a proposed UK university and course plan demonstrating genuine research into where they intend to study.

## Application Process

Applicants apply through their home university's campus nomination process first, since Marshall does not accept unendorsed applications directly from students; nominees then submit the national Marshall application by the fall deadline for study beginning the following autumn, following a two-stage process that rewards early engagement with campus advisors.

## Deadline

The current application deadline is 29 September 2026. Because campus nomination has to happen before the national submission, students should engage their university's Marshall committee well ahead of this date, ideally months in advance rather than waiting until the final weeks of the nomination window.

## Why You Should Apply

For American citizens who want genuine flexibility in choosing both their UK university and field of study, Marshall offers comprehensive funding and a historic reputation matched by few peer scholarships open specifically to US applicants, making it a strong option for students who have a clear sense of what they want to study but no single obvious university to pursue it at.

For full details, visit the official Marshall Scholarship website: https://www.marshallscholarship.org/apply/
