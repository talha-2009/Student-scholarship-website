Marshall Scholarship

## Introduction Created by the British Parliament in 1953 as a living gesture of thanks for American assistance under the Marshall Plan after the Second World War, the Marshall Scholarship funds American citizens to pursue postgraduate study at any UK university, in virtually any subject. Unlike scholarships tied to a single institution, such as Rhodes' historical connection to Oxford, Marshall's genuine flexibility in choice of university and course, combined with its openness to applicants of any gender since its founding, has made it one of the most respected postgraduate awards available to US citizens specifically. ## About the Opportunity Scholars pursue one to three years of postgraduate study at a UK university of their own choosing, selected through a national competition that begins with campus nomination at their home institution rather than a direct national application. Because Marshall does not restrict scholars to a single university, cohorts of scholars end up spread across the UK's higher education system, studying everything from the sciences through law, humanities, and public policy, though they remain connected through the Marshall Scholarship community and its associated events and programming throughout their time in Britain. ## Benefits Scholars receive university fees, a living stipend, an annual book grant, a thesis grant, research and travel grants, and airfare to and from the United States, for one to three years of study, a benefits package designed to support not just tuition but the full range of costs associated with serious postgraduate research or coursework. ## Who Can Apply? US citizens who are current undergraduates or recent graduates, endorsed by their home university through its internal nomination process, can apply, with the scholarship open to applicants of any gender, a deliberate departure from some historic postgraduate scholarships that originally restricted eligibility. ## Fields of Study or Available Areas Marshall funds study across nearly any subject offered by UK universities, giving scholars genuine freedom in choosing both their field and their institution, rather than steering candidates toward a narrower set of pre-approved courses. ## Required Documents Applicants need their campus nomination, a personal statement, academic transcripts, recommendation letters, and a proposed UK university and course plan demonstrating genuine research into where they intend to study. ## Application Process Applicants apply through their home university's campus nomination process first, since Marshall does not accept unendorsed applications directly from students; nominees then submit the national Marshall application by the fall deadline for study beginning the following autumn, following a two-stage process that rewards early engagement with campus advisors. ## Deadline The current application deadline is 29 September 2026. Because campus nomination has to happen before the national submission, students should engage their university's Marshall committee well ahead of this date, ideally months in advance rather than waiting until the final weeks of the nomination window. ## Why You Should Apply For American citizens who want genuine flexibility in choosing both their UK university and field of study, Marshall offers comprehensive funding and a historic reputation matched by few peer scholarships open specifically to US applicants, making it a strong option for students who have a clear sense of what they want to study but no single obvious university to pursue it at. For full details, visit the official Marshall Scholarship website: https://www.marshallscholarship.org/apply/

Marshall Scholarship Eligibility

The Marshall Scholarship is exclusively open to US citizens who hold their first undergraduate degree from an accredited four-year US college or university. Applicants must have a minimum GPA of 3.7 and must have graduated after April 2024. You must not have previously studied for a degree at a UK university or sat UK school examinations such as A-Levels or GCSEs. Dual US-UK nationals are not eligible. There are no restrictions on the field of study — candidates from all academic disciplines are welcome to apply.

Marshall Scholarship Benefits and Funding

The Marshall Scholarship is fully funded and covers university and college tuition fees, a monthly living stipend, arrival and departure travel costs to the UK, and visa support. Funding is available for one to three years depending on the chosen pathway: a single one-year Master's degree, two consecutive one-year Master's degrees, a two-year Master's programme, or up to three years of doctoral study. Scholars also gain access to an influential transatlantic network of Marshall alumni working across government, academia, science, and the arts.

Marshall Scholarship Deadline and Timeline

For the 2026 cycle, all application materials — including reference letters — must be submitted by 29 September 2026 at 5:00 PM in the endorsing institution's time zone. Your undergraduate institution must then complete its endorsement and forward your application by 1 October 2026. The application portal typically opens in June. Finalists are invited to interviews in November, and awards are announced in January. Always confirm exact dates on the official Marshall Scholarship website.

Required Documents for the Marshall Scholarship

  • Online application form — submitted through the Marshall Scholarship portal
  • Academic transcripts — from all post-secondary institutions attended
  • Personal statement — describing your academic interests, leadership experience, and reasons for studying in the UK
  • Study proposal — outlining your intended programme of study at a UK university
  • Three reference letters — from academic referees who can assess your scholarly potential and leadership qualities
  • Institutional endorsement — your undergraduate university must endorse your application before submission
  • Passport or birth certificate — to verify US citizenship

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Starting the endorsement process too late — each university has its own internal deadline, which is often weeks before the national deadline. Contact your campus liaison immediately.
  2. Writing a generic personal statement — the selection committee looks for specific academic goals, leadership evidence, and ambassadorial potential. Tailor your essay to show why the UK is essential to your plans.
  3. Choosing a vague UK study plan — name a specific programme and university, explain why it fits your goals, and show you've researched the department.
  4. Underestimating the GPA threshold — while 3.7 is the minimum, successful candidates typically have GPAs well above this. Focus on demonstrating academic distinction beyond grades alone.
