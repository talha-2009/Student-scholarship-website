IMU-Simons Research Fellowship Program for Developing Countries

Introduction

Mathematicians working in developing countries often face a specific, practical barrier to genuine international collaboration: the cost of traveling abroad for sustained, in-person research time with peers elsewhere, a barrier that can quietly limit career progression regardless of research talent. The IMU-Simons Research Fellowship Program, jointly run by the International Mathematical Union and the Simons Foundation, exists specifically to remove that barrier, funding short collaborative research visits for faculty mathematicians based in IMU-designated developing countries, with a thoughtful provision for childcare that few peer research grants offer explicitly.

About the Opportunity

Fellows spend one to three months at a host institution abroad, working intensively with a mathematical collaborator they have already identified and connected with before applying, rather than being matched with a host through the programme itself. The programme's design assumes real collaborative infrastructure is already in place, an established host contact, a definite research plan, and approved leave from the applicant's home institution, so the grant itself functions as the funding that makes an already-planned collaboration financially possible rather than as seed funding for an idea still in early formation or a networking opportunity to find a collaborator from scratch.

Benefits

Grants range from USD 5,000 to 15,000, covering airfare, accommodation, and living expenses during the research visit. Distinctively, the programme includes childcare support specifically for primary caregivers, a provision that directly addresses a real practical barrier many academic parents face when considering extended research travel, and one that many comparable international research grants overlook entirely.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need a PhD in mathematics and a permanent faculty position at a university or similar institution in an IMU-designated developing country, along with an established host contact, a definite research plan, and approved leave of absence from their home institution secured before applying.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The grant supports mathematics research broadly, open to any subfield where a genuine collaborative research plan with an international host has already been established between the applicant and their prospective collaborator.

Required Documents

Applicants need a CV for both themselves and their host, each limited to three pages, a detailed research plan describing the intended collaboration, a statement from the host confirming their commitment, and a formal invitation letter specifying the visit period and institutional support offered.

Application Process

Applicants apply directly via the IMU-Simons fellowship application, submitting all required documents by the relevant round deadline, since the programme typically runs several rounds through the year tied to different intended visit timing, giving applicants some flexibility in when they schedule their research stay.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 15 July 2026, with IMU-Simons typically also running additional rounds in the months before and after, depending on the applicant's intended visit dates and how far in advance they are planning.

Why You Should Apply

For mathematicians at institutions in developing countries who have already identified a genuine international collaborator, this grant removes what is often the single biggest practical obstacle to sustained joint research, the cost of travel and living abroad, with a thoughtfulness around caregiving responsibilities that sets it apart from many comparable research grants and makes genuine international collaboration realistically achievable rather than a theoretical possibility.

For full application details, visit the official IMU-Simons fellowship page: https://www.mathunion.org/cdc/grants/SRF
