ETH Zurich Excellence Scholarship and Opportunity Programme

Introduction

ETH Zurich consistently ranks among the world's strongest science and engineering universities, and its Excellence Scholarship and Opportunity Programme funds a small, highly selective group of top bachelor's graduates to pursue their first master's degree there. Because ETH restricts eligibility to students finishing in roughly the top 10 percent of their cohort, this scholarship functions less as a broad access programme and more as recognition, paired with generous funding, for genuinely exceptional academic performance.

About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue a master's programme at ETH Zurich as their first postgraduate degree, and the scholarship application runs in parallel with, not after, the master's admission process itself. A distinguishing feature of ETH's approach is the required research pre-proposal, which asks applicants to sketch out an intended thesis or research direction even before formally starting their studies, giving the university a clearer sense of each candidate's academic seriousness and direction.

Benefits

Scholars receive a study and living cost scholarship of approximately CHF 12,000 per semester, alongside a full tuition fee waiver for the entire master's programme, typically three to four semesters. Beyond the direct financial support, scholars gain mentorship and access to the ETH Foundation's wider network, connecting them with resources and contacts extending beyond their immediate academic department.

Who Can Apply?

Both national and international students are eligible, provided they completed their bachelor's degree with excellent results, broadly defined as being in the top 10 percent of their cohort or achieving grade-A-equivalent performance. Applicants must be applying for their first postgraduate degree, since the scholarship is not available to those who have already completed another master's programme.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The scholarship covers all fields available at ETH Zurich at the master's level, reflecting the university's broad strength across engineering, natural sciences, mathematics, and related technical disciplines.

Required Documents

Applicants need their ETH master's programme application, bachelor's transcripts, a pre-proposal for their intended master's thesis or research direction, a CV, and a motivation letter.

Application Process

Applicants apply during ETH's specific first application window, running from 1 to 30 November, submitting their scholarship application via the ETH student portal alongside their master's programme application. The required research pre-proposal is a genuine differentiator in this process, and applicants may also be asked to complete a phone or video interview in February as part of the selection process.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 30 November 2026. Because ETH does not appear to run a second scholarship window later in the year, applicants who miss this narrow November period should expect to wait for the following year's cycle.

Why You Should Apply

For top bachelor's graduates who want to pursue their first master's at one of the world's leading technical universities, ETH's Excellence Scholarship offers substantial funding, generous living support, and a mentorship network extending beyond graduation. Among fully funded scholarships for master's study in Switzerland, its selectivity is precisely what makes it valuable: a genuine signal of academic distinction attached to a scholarship that removes financial barriers entirely for those who qualify.

For full eligibility and application details, visit the official ETH Zurich Excellence Scholarship page: https://ethz.ch/en/studies/master/financials/scholarships/excellence-scholarship.html
