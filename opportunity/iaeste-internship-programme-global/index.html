IAESTE Internship Programme

Introduction

For engineering and science students who want paid international work experience without navigating employer contacts entirely on their own, IAESTE offers one of the longest-running structured paths available, operating through a network of national committees rather than a single centralized application system. Because your application is registered and reviewed by your own country's IAESTE committee before ever reaching an employer, the process carries more built-in support and vetting than applying to international internships independently, a genuine advantage for students without existing overseas contacts.

About the Opportunity

IAESTE connects students with paid internship placements across a wide range of technical fields, arranged through committees in member countries worldwide. A student's home committee handles the initial registration and application review, and once approved, forwards the candidate's documents to a specific host employer for final selection. This structure means the practical work of the placement, arranging visas, local housing, and other logistics, is substantially supported by the network rather than left entirely to the individual student.

Benefits

Interns receive a cost-of-living stipend covering accommodation, food, and local travel in their host country, with housing typically arranged locally by the host committee or employer. Visa and work-authorization assistance is provided as part of the placement, and interns gain lasting access to the global IAESTE alumni network, a genuinely useful resource for building international professional connections well beyond the internship itself.

Who Can Apply?

Full-time bachelor's, master's, or PhD students, or recent graduates within one year of completing their degree, in engineering, science, computer science, architecture, agriculture, and some non-STEM fields can apply, provided they register through their home country's IAESTE national committee. Good English proficiency is required, since placements often involve working alongside international teams communicating primarily in English.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields center on engineering, science, computer science, architecture, and agriculture, reflecting IAESTE's historical roots in technical exchange, though committees occasionally list positions in adjacent non-STEM fields as well.

Required Documents

Applicants need an online registration and application, a CV, a cover letter, academic transcripts, proof of enrollment, a passport copy, and proof of insurance coverage.

Application Process

Students register on the IAESTE Exchange Platform through their country's national committee, then apply to specific Exchange or Global Internship listings matching their field and location preferences. Applications go through committee approval before being forwarded to the host employer, who makes the final selection decision, meaning success depends both on committee approval and on standing out to the specific employer reviewing candidates for that placement.

Deadline

IAESTE placements are listed on a rolling basis rather than following one fixed global deadline, so interested students should register with their national committee and begin browsing listings as early as possible, since strong placements in popular locations or fields can fill before a formal deadline would otherwise apply.

Why You Should Apply

For technical students who want genuine paid international work experience with structured support around visas, housing, and insurance, IAESTE's decades-long network offers a level of institutional backing that independently-arranged international internships typically cannot match. Among paid internships and global opportunities for engineering and science students specifically, its national-committee structure and lasting alumni network make it worth registering for even before a specific placement catches your eye.

For full registration details, visit the official IAESTE website: https://www.iaeste.org/students/
