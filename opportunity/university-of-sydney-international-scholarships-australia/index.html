University of Sydney International Scholarships

## Introduction The University of Sydney International Scholarships programme covers a range of merit-based awards for high-achieving students entering undergraduate, postgraduate coursework, or research programmes at one of Australia's oldest and most prestigious universities. Rather than a single scholarship, "International Scholarships" is best understood as a portfolio of separate awards with different tiers of funding, which means the specific benefits and requirements you encounter depend heavily on which award within the portfolio you apply for. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** University of Sydney - **Country of Study:** Australia (Sydney) - **Opportunity Type:** Scholarship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Bachelor's or Master's - **Duration:** Length of the chosen degree programme - **Official Website:** sydney.edu.au ## Benefits - Tuition fee coverage and/or a living allowance, depending on the specific scholarship tier - Research scholarships within the portfolio may include a stipend of around AUD 40,000 per year - Exact benefits vary by which specific award within the International Scholarships portfolio you are granted ## Eligibility Criteria - Be a high-achieving domestic or international student - Be admitted, or in the process of applying, to an eligible undergraduate, postgraduate coursework, or research programme at the University of Sydney - Selection is academic merit-based, with specific thresholds varying by the individual award ## Required Documents - Academic transcripts - A personal statement - CV - References - English proficiency evidence, for international applicants ## Application Process **Step 1.** Apply for admission to your intended programme at the University of Sydney. **Step 2.** Separately browse the University's scholarships portal to identify which specific International Scholarship award matches your programme and background. **Step 3.** Submit a separate scholarship application, including your personal statement, CV, transcripts, and references, by the published deadline for that award. **Step 4.** The University reviews your scholarship application alongside your admission status. **Step 5.** Successful applicants receive an award offer specifying the exact benefits granted under their particular scholarship tier. ## Important Dates - **Application deadline:** See the deadline listed for this opportunity (exact dates vary by specific award within the portfolio) - **Programme start:** Aligned with your University of Sydney admission offer Because this covers multiple distinct awards, always check the specific deadline for the award you are targeting on the official scholarships portal. ## Selection Criteria Each award within the International Scholarships portfolio sets its own precise criteria, but broadly, selection is based on strong academic transcripts, a compelling personal statement, and — for research-track awards — the quality of any proposed research direction. ## Tips for Applicants - Treat "International Scholarships" as a portfolio, not a single award — identify the specific scholarship that matches your programme and apply to that one directly - Apply for University admission and the specific scholarship in parallel, since both processes typically run separately - Tailor your personal statement to the specific award's stated priorities, since different awards within the portfolio can value different qualities - Confirm your English proficiency evidence meets the University's specific requirements if applying as an international student - Check deadlines carefully, since different awards in the portfolio may close at different times ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for University of Sydney International Scholarships?** High-achieving domestic and international students admitted to eligible programmes. **Is it fully funded?** Coverage varies by specific award — some cover full tuition and a stipend, others partial funding; check the specific award's terms. **Do I apply separately for admission and the scholarship?** Yes, they are generally separate application processes. **Can research students apply?** Yes, some awards within the portfolio are specifically for research programmes and may include a stipend around AUD 40,000 per year. **What degree levels are covered?** Both bachelor's and master's level programmes, depending on the specific award. **How do I know which specific scholarship to apply for?** Review the University's scholarships portal to match your programme and background to the relevant award. ## Apply Now Browse the current scholarship portfolio and apply through the official University of Sydney scholarships page: https://www.sydney.edu.au/scholarships.html
