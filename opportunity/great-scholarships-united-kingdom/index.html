GREAT Scholarships

Introduction

GREAT Scholarships form part of the UK government's wider GREAT Britain Campaign, but rather than being a single award, they operate as a coordinated national scheme delivered through the British Council in partnership with individual UK universities, each offering its own version of the discount to a specific, rotating list of eligible countries. For students from one of those countries considering a one-year UK master's, GREAT Scholarships can meaningfully reduce the overall cost, though it is worth understanding upfront that this is a partial discount rather than full funding.

About the Opportunity

Each participating UK university offers its own GREAT Scholarship, tied to specific countries and sometimes specific subjects, meaning a prospective applicant's actual eligibility depends on which university, country, and subject combination is currently on offer in a given year. This structure means "applying for a GREAT Scholarship" really means identifying the specific university offering the scholarship relevant to your country and field, then applying to that institution directly.

Benefits

Successful applicants receive a tuition-fee award of at least GBP 10,000 toward their one-year postgraduate course, a significant discount though not full funding, meaning recipients remain responsible for the remaining tuition and all living costs during their year of study.

Who Can Apply?

Eligibility depends on citizenship in one of the currently eligible countries, which has previously included Bangladesh, China, Egypt, Ghana, India, Indonesia, Kenya, Malaysia, Mexico, Nepal, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Thailand, and Turkey, though the exact list changes by year and by participating university. Applicants also need an undergraduate degree and must meet the specific English language requirement of their chosen UK university.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

GREAT Scholarships cover one-year taught postgraduate courses across a wide range of subjects, though the specific fields available depend on which university and scholarship an applicant is pursuing.

Required Documents

Applicants need a standard university postgraduate application, academic transcripts, English proficiency proof, and, as required by the specific university, a personal statement.

Application Process

Applicants first check the current GREAT Scholarships country and university list on the British Council's Study UK page, since eligibility changes from year to year. Once a relevant university and scholarship combination is identified, applicants apply directly to that university's postgraduate programme, following its own admissions process, and complete that specific institution's GREAT Scholarship application separately from the general British Council information.

Deadline

Because GREAT Scholarships run through many individual participating universities, deadlines vary considerably. Applicants should confirm the specific deadline for their chosen university's scholarship application directly rather than assuming one date applies universally.

Why You Should Apply

For students from an eligible country who want to reduce the cost of a UK master's without needing to win a fully competitive, fully-funded scholarship, GREAT Scholarships offer a genuinely useful partial discount backed by British Council credibility. Among partially funded scholarships for UK postgraduate study, the key practical step is checking the current eligible-country and university list early, since it changes yearly and directly determines whether a given applicant qualifies at all.

For the current eligible countries and participating universities, visit the official British Council Study UK page: https://study-uk.britishcouncil.org/scholarships-funding
