UNESCO Associate Expert Programme

## Introduction

The UNESCO Associate Expert Programme places young professionals inside UNESCO for one to two years, but with a distinctive funding model: rather than UNESCO itself covering the placement, your own national government sponsors it, positioning this as much a diplomatic exchange arrangement as an individual career opportunity. Because government sponsorship is central to eligibility, the practical starting point for this programme is your country's foreign ministry or UNESCO National Commission, not UNESCO's careers page directly.

## Opportunity Overview

- **Organization:** UNESCO - **Country of Study:** Global — placement locations vary - **Opportunity Type:** Fellowship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Graduate - **Duration:** One to two years - **Official Website:** en.unesco.org/careers/associateexpert

## Benefits

- A monthly stipend - Professional experience at UNESCO - International diplomatic exposure

## Eligibility Criteria

- Be a national of a UNESCO member state - Have your home government agree to sponsor your placement - Hold relevant graduate qualifications

## Required Documents

- Government sponsorship confirmation - CV - Degree certificates

## Application Process

**Step 1.** Contact your country's foreign ministry or UNESCO National Commission to inquire about government sponsorship for the Associate Expert Programme.

**Step 2.** Work with your government to secure formal sponsorship confirmation, since this is a required condition of eligibility.

**Step 3.** Prepare your CV and degree certificates to support your government's nomination.

**Step 4.** Your government submits your nomination and sponsorship confirmation through the appropriate diplomatic channel to UNESCO.

**Step 5.** UNESCO reviews sponsored candidates and matches successful nominees with a suitable placement.

**Step 6.** Begin your one-to-two-year assignment within UNESCO, working in your area of expertise.

## Important Dates

Because this programme is coordinated through individual national governments rather than a single centralized application, please refer to your country's foreign ministry or UNESCO National Commission for the current process and any relevant timelines.

## Selection Criteria

Selection depends first on securing genuine government sponsorship, then on UNESCO's review of your graduate qualifications and relevant expertise against available placement needs across the organization's various divisions and priorities.

## Tips for Applicants

- Start with your government's foreign ministry or UNESCO National Commission, not UNESCO's careers page directly, since sponsorship is the essential first step - Build a relationship with the relevant government office well in advance, since securing sponsorship can take time and coordination - Make sure your graduate qualifications and CV clearly align with UNESCO's areas of work (education, culture, science, communication) - Be patient with the process, since it depends on both your government's internal procedures and UNESCO's own placement matching - Ask your National Commission directly about the current status and any upcoming opportunities under this programme

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Who can apply for the UNESCO Associate Expert Programme?** Nationals of a UNESCO member state whose government agrees to sponsor the placement.

**Is it fully funded?** Yes — a monthly stipend is provided, funded through your government's sponsorship arrangement.

**Can I apply directly to UNESCO?** No, application is coordinated through your national government and UNESCO National Commission.

**How long does the placement last?** One to two years.

**What qualifications do I need?** Relevant graduate qualifications matching UNESCO's areas of work.

**Where will I be placed?** This varies, depending on UNESCO's needs and your area of expertise.

## Apply Now

Contact your country's foreign ministry or UNESCO National Commission to begin the sponsorship process: https://en.unesco.org/careers/associateexpert
