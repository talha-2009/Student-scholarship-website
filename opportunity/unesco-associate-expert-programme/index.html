UNESCO Associate Expert Programme

Introduction

The UNESCO Associate Expert Programme places young professionals inside UNESCO for one to two years, but with a distinctive funding model: rather than UNESCO itself covering the placement, the applicant's own national government sponsors it, positioning this as much a diplomatic exchange arrangement as an individual career opportunity.

About the Opportunity

Sponsored Associate Experts work within a UNESCO division matching their qualifications and their government's priorities, gaining direct professional experience at UNESCO alongside genuine international diplomatic exposure. Because government sponsorship is central to eligibility, the practical starting point for this programme is a national foreign ministry or UNESCO National Commission, not UNESCO's careers page directly.

Benefits

Associate Experts receive a monthly stipend, professional experience at UNESCO, and international diplomatic exposure.

Who Can Apply?

Nationals of a UNESCO member state whose government agrees to sponsor the placement, holding relevant graduate qualifications, can apply.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Relevant fields include education, culture, and science, reflecting UNESCO's core mandate areas.

Required Documents

Applicants need government sponsorship confirmation, a CV, and degree certificates.

Application Process

Application is coordinated through the applicant's national government and UNESCO National Commission rather than a direct individual application.

Deadline

No deadline has been officially announced; interested candidates should contact their national UNESCO Commission directly.

Why You Should Apply

For graduates whose government is willing to sponsor them, this programme offers genuine UNESCO experience and international diplomatic exposure not available through standard internship routes.

For full details, visit the official UNESCO Associate Expert page: https://en.unesco.org/careers/associateexpert
