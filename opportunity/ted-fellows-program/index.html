TED Fellows Program

## Introduction The TED Fellows Program identifies innovators and change-makers around the world and gives them a genuine platform at TED conferences, alongside media training and a lasting professional network. Unlike an academic fellowship, it is built around individual impact and public communication skills — TED is specifically looking for people who already have a compelling project or body of work and can present it effectively on a global stage, and applications only open periodically rather than on a fixed annual schedule. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** TED - **Country of Study:** Global - **Opportunity Type:** Fellowship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Open to all - **Duration:** A fellowship term connected to TED conference cycles - **Official Website:** ted.com/participate/ted-fellows-program ## Benefits - A speaking opportunity at a TED conference - Mentorship - Media training - Access to the global TED Fellows network ## Eligibility Criteria - Be an innovator, entrepreneur, or change-maker with an impactful project or body of work - Depending on the specific application cycle, you may need to be nominated, or you may be able to self-apply ## Required Documents - Application form - A project description - Supporting media illustrating your work ## Application Process **Step 1.** Watch the official TED Fellows website for open call announcements, since applications open periodically rather than on a continuous or fixed schedule. **Step 2.** When a call is open, complete the application form, describing your project or body of work clearly. **Step 3.** Submit supporting media that illustrates your work's impact. **Step 4.** TED reviews applications (and nominations, depending on the cycle) for genuine innovation and impact. **Step 5.** Selected Fellows receive an invitation connected to an upcoming TED conference, along with mentorship and media training. ## Important Dates TED Fellows applications are not open on a continuous or fixed annual basis. Please refer to the official website for the current status of open calls, since timing varies by cycle. ## Selection Criteria TED looks for candidates with a genuinely impactful, well-documented project or body of work, strong potential as a public communicator, and a story that would resonate with a broad global audience at a TED conference. ## Tips for Applicants - Check the official TED Fellows website regularly, since open calls are periodic rather than always available - Make your project description specific and evidence-based, showing real impact rather than potential alone - Prepare supporting media (video, images, press coverage) that clearly illustrates your work in action - Think about how your work would translate into a compelling talk, since public communication is central to being a TED Fellow - Be patient with timing, since this fellowship does not run on a predictable annual cycle ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the TED Fellows Program?** Innovators, entrepreneurs, and change-makers with an impactful project or body of work. **Is it fully funded?** Yes, it includes a TED conference speaking opportunity, mentorship, and media training. **When can I apply?** Only during periodic open calls; there is no fixed annual application window. **Do I need to be nominated?** This depends on the specific cycle — some calls accept self-applications, others rely on nomination. **What do I need to submit?** An application form, project description, and supporting media. **What happens after selection?** You receive a speaking opportunity at a TED conference along with mentorship and media training. ## Apply Now Check for current open calls on the official TED Fellows Program website: https://www.ted.com/participate/ted-fellows-program
