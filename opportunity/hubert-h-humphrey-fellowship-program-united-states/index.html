Hubert H. Humphrey Fellowship Program

## Introduction

Most Fulbright programmes are built for students. The Hubert H. Humphrey Fellowship Program is deliberately different: it is designed for established mid-career professionals who already have real leadership experience behind them, offering ten months at a US university focused on tailored professional development rather than a formal degree. For professionals in public policy, law, public health, economic development, media, or natural resources policy who have already built a career and want to sharpen it through US exposure, Humphrey offers a fellowship built specifically around that stage of professional life.

## About the Opportunity

Humphrey Fellows spend ten months engaged in non-degree coursework, structured professional development, and practical field experience connected to their specific area of expertise, rather than pursuing a traditional master's or PhD. Because Fellows arrive with substantial professional experience already, the programme is built around deepening and internationalizing existing expertise rather than introducing entirely new material, which shapes both the coursework and the practical placements Fellows undertake during their time in the US.

## Benefits

Fellows receive tuition and fees, a monthly maintenance allowance, a book and supply allowance, round-trip international travel, health insurance through ASPE, and a professional development allowance supporting fieldwork or additional training connected to their fellowship focus.

## Who Can Apply?

Applicants need to be mid-career professionals from eligible countries with a university degree and at least five years of full-time, relevant professional experience, along with demonstrated leadership and a commitment to public service. English proficiency is required, and the fellowship generally excludes university teachers or researchers without management experience, as well as recent graduates, since the programme is built specifically around candidates already established in a professional leadership track.

## Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields include public policy, law, public health, economic development, media, and natural resources policy, reflecting the fellowship's focus on sectors where mid-career leadership expertise can be meaningfully deepened.

## Required Documents

Applicants need the online application through apply.iie.org/huberthhumphrey, official transcripts, two recommendation letters with one required from their current employer, a CV, and an English proficiency test score.

## Application Process

Applicants apply through the U.S. Embassy Public Affairs Section or the Binational Fulbright Commission in their home country, which reviews national applications and nominates candidates to the Fulbright Foreign Scholarship Board for final approval. Because the fellowship specifically requires an employer recommendation letter, applicants need to secure that support from their current workplace as part of preparing a complete application.

## Deadline

Deadlines vary by country, set through the local U.S. Embassy or Binational Fulbright Commission rather than a single global date. Applicants should confirm the current cycle's specific deadline directly with their local Fulbright office.

## Why You Should Apply

For mid-career professionals with a genuine leadership track record who want to deepen and internationalize their expertise, few fellowships offer this level of tailored, fully funded professional development built specifically around their career stage. Among fellowships aimed at established professionals rather than students, Humphrey stands out for combining Fulbright's credibility with a structure genuinely designed around mid-career needs rather than adapted from a student-focused programme.

For country-specific application details, visit the official Hubert Humphrey Fellowship website: https://www.humphreyfellowship.org/
