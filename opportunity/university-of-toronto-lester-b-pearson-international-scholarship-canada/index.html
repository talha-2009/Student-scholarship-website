University of Toronto Lester B. Pearson International Scholarship

## Introduction The University of Toronto's Lester B. Pearson International Scholarship is one of Canada's most prestigious undergraduate awards, named after the former Canadian prime minister and Nobel laureate, and designed for incoming international students who show exceptional academic and personal achievement. Unlike scholarships requiring a separate application form, Pearson works through high school nomination: your school puts you forward, which means building the right relationship with your school's scholarship coordinator is often the real first step. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** University of Toronto - **Country of Study:** Canada (Toronto) - **Opportunity Type:** Scholarship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Undergraduate - **Duration:** Four years - **Official Website:** future.utoronto.ca ## Benefits - Full tuition - Books and incidental fees - A living allowance for the full four years of undergraduate study ## Eligibility Criteria - Be an incoming international undergraduate student - Demonstrate exceptional academic and personal achievement - Be nominated by your high school in most cases — this is the standard route into the scholarship ## Required Documents - University of Toronto undergraduate admission application - High school nomination - Transcripts - Personal statement/essays ## Application Process **Step 1.** Apply for undergraduate admission to the University of Toronto. **Step 2.** Confirm with your high school's scholarship or guidance coordinator whether they will nominate you for the Pearson Scholarship — nomination, not a separate application form, is the standard entry point. **Step 3.** Ensure your transcripts and personal statement/essays submitted as part of your University of Toronto application are strong, since there is no additional Pearson-specific form to complete. **Step 4.** The University reviews nominated students against the scholarship's academic and personal achievement criteria. **Step 5.** Successful nominees receive a combined offer covering admission and the full scholarship. ## Important Dates - **Application deadline:** See the deadline listed for this opportunity (typically early November) - **Programme start:** The following autumn at the University of Toronto Since nomination timing depends on your high school's own internal process, start the conversation with your school well ahead of the university's deadline. ## Selection Criteria The University of Toronto evaluates nominated students on demonstrated exceptional academic achievement and evidence of personal impact or leadership, reviewed through transcripts and personal statement essays submitted as part of the standard admission application. ## Tips for Applicants - Talk to your high school's scholarship coordinator early, since nomination — not a separate form — is the standard way into this scholarship - Make sure your University of Toronto personal statement and essays clearly demonstrate both academic and personal achievement, since there is no separate Pearson application to elaborate further - Apply for admission well ahead of the deadline to give your school time to complete its nomination process - If your school does not typically nominate students for Pearson, ask directly whether they are able to do so for your application - Highlight leadership, community impact, or other personal achievements clearly in your admission essays ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the Lester B. Pearson Scholarship?** Incoming international undergraduate students with exceptional academic and personal achievement. **Is it fully funded?** Yes — tuition, books, incidental fees, and a living allowance are covered for four years. **Do I need a separate application form?** No, nomination by your high school is the standard route, alongside your regular University of Toronto application. **What if my school doesn't usually nominate students?** Contact your school's scholarship coordinator directly to ask about the nomination process. **How long does the scholarship last?** Four years, covering the full undergraduate degree. **When is the deadline?** Typically early November; confirm the current cycle's exact date. ## Apply Now Apply for University of Toronto admission and confirm your nomination process on the official Pearson Scholarship page: https://future.utoronto.ca/pearson/
