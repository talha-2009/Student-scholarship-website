Peace Corps Volunteer Program

Introduction

For more than six decades, the Peace Corps has sent American volunteers into communities around the world for roughly two years of service, longer and more immersive than nearly any other international volunteering programme available to US citizens. Unlike short-term volunteer trips, Peace Corps service is a genuine multi-year commitment, and the programme provides correspondingly substantial support, a living stipend, housing, full medical and dental coverage, and student loan deferment, reflecting its status as a formal US government programme rather than an independently arranged placement.

About the Opportunity

Volunteers are placed in communities across dozens of countries, working in sectors including education, health, and community development based on their skills, interests, and the specific needs of the host community they are assigned to. Service typically begins with an intensive in-country training period covering language, culture, and technical skills relevant to the volunteer's assigned role, before transitioning into the main service period embedded within a specific community for the majority of the two years.

Benefits

Volunteers receive a living stipend, housing, full medical and dental coverage throughout their service, student loan deferment for the duration of their commitment, and a readjustment allowance paid upon completing service to support the transition back to life in the United States.

Who Can Apply?

US citizens aged 18 and older in good health can apply, with some specific programme assignments requiring relevant degrees or professional experience depending on the sector and country.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Peace Corps assignments span education, health, and community development broadly, with specific roles varying by country and community need.

Required Documents

Applicants need an application form, a resume, references, and medical and legal clearance documents as part of the screening process.

Application Process

Applicants apply online via the Peace Corps website, followed by interviews and a medical and legal clearance process that verifies fitness for extended overseas service before a final country and assignment placement is confirmed.

Deadline

Peace Corps accepts applications on a rolling basis rather than a fixed annual deadline; interested applicants should apply well ahead of their intended departure, since the clearance process can take several months.

Why You Should Apply

For US citizens who want a genuinely immersive, multi-year international service experience with substantial government support behind it, few volunteer programmes match the Peace Corps' combination of depth, structure, and comprehensive benefits throughout service.

For full application details, visit the official Peace Corps website: https://www.peacecorps.gov/how-to-apply/
