Peace Corps Volunteer Program

## Introduction The Peace Corps Volunteer Program is one of the longest-running and most recognisable international volunteer initiatives, placing U.S. citizens into community-based assignments abroad for roughly two years, spanning education, health, and community development work. Unlike many volunteering options that require significant self-funding, Peace Corps provides substantial support — living costs, full medical coverage, and student loan deferment — reflecting its status as a formal U.S. government programme rather than an independent volunteer placement service. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** U.S. Peace Corps - **Country of Study:** Global — country placements vary by programme and current openings - **Opportunity Type:** Volunteer Program - **Funding Type:** Paid - **Degree Level:** Open to all - **Duration:** Typically around two years - **Official Website:** peacecorps.gov ## Benefits - A living stipend - Housing - Full medical and dental coverage - Student loan deferment - A readjustment allowance upon completing service ## Eligibility Criteria - Be a U.S. citizen - Be at least 18 years old - Be in good health - Some specific programmes may require relevant degrees or professional experience ## Required Documents - Application form - Resume - References - Medical and legal clearance documents ## Application Process **Step 1.** Apply online through the Peace Corps website, indicating any programme or region preferences. **Step 2.** Submit your resume and references as part of the application. **Step 3.** Participate in interviews as part of the selection process. **Step 4.** Complete the required medical and legal clearance process, which verifies your fitness for overseas service. **Step 5.** Once cleared, you receive a country and programme assignment along with a departure date. **Step 6.** Begin your roughly two-year service, typically starting with an initial training period in-country. ## Important Dates Peace Corps accepts applications on a rolling basis rather than a single annual deadline. Please refer to the official website for current application windows and the specific timeline for medical/legal clearance, which can take several months. ## Selection Criteria Peace Corps assesses applicants on adaptability, relevant skills or experience for specific programme sectors (education, health, or community development), and successful completion of the medical and legal clearance process required for overseas service. ## Tips for Applicants - Start your application well ahead of your intended departure date, since medical and legal clearance can take several months - Be flexible about country and programme placement, since this can affect how quickly you are matched with an assignment - Highlight any relevant skills or prior experience clearly, especially for specific technical sectors like health or education - Prepare for interviews that assess your genuine readiness for extended overseas community-based service - Research the general realities of Peace Corps service in advance, since it involves significant lifestyle adjustment over roughly two years ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the Peace Corps?** U.S. citizens aged 18 or older who are in good health. **Is Peace Corps service paid?** Yes, it includes a living stipend, housing, and full medical and dental coverage. **How long does service last?** Typically around two years. **Do I need a specific degree?** Some specific programmes require relevant degrees or experience; others are more general. **What happens to my student loans during service?** Student loan deferment is available as part of the benefits. **How long does the clearance process take?** Medical and legal clearance can take several months as part of the application process. ## Apply Now Start your application through the official Peace Corps website: https://www.peacecorps.gov/how-to-apply/
