Salzburg Global Seminar Fellowship

## Introduction The Salzburg Global Seminar Fellowship convenes professionals and emerging leaders from many sectors at multi-day seminars in Salzburg, Austria, addressing global policy challenges through cross-sector dialogue. Rather than a single standing fellowship, it operates as a rotating series of specific seminar programmes, each with its own topic, eligibility profile, and application or nomination process, which means "applying" really means finding the specific seminar that matches your background and interests. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** Salzburg Global Seminar - **Country of Study:** Austria (Salzburg) - **Opportunity Type:** Fellowship - **Funding Type:** Partially Funded - **Degree Level:** Open to all - **Duration:** Multi-day seminar programmes - **Official Website:** salzburgglobal.org ## Benefits - Full or partial funding for travel and accommodation, depending on the specific seminar - Access to a global, multi-sector network of fellows ## Eligibility Criteria - Be a professional or emerging leader relevant to the specific seminar's topic - Selection criteria vary by individual programme, since each seminar targets a different sector or theme ## Required Documents - Application form - CV - A letter of motivation ## Application Process **Step 1.** Browse the current list of seminar programmes on the Salzburg Global website to find one matching your sector and interests. **Step 2.** Review that specific seminar's eligibility criteria and whether it accepts open applications or works by nomination. **Step 3.** Submit your application form, CV, and letter of motivation for the specific seminar, or accept nomination if that is the relevant route. **Step 4.** Salzburg Global reviews applications against that seminar's specific goals and desired participant mix. **Step 5.** Successful participants receive confirmation of funding coverage and travel to Salzburg for the seminar dates. ## Important Dates Because each seminar programme runs on its own schedule, please refer to the official website for current and upcoming seminars and their specific application deadlines. ## Selection Criteria Selection varies by programme, but generally looks for professional relevance to the seminar's theme, evidence of leadership or influence in your field, and a clear motivation for engaging with the specific cross-sector dialogue the seminar is designed to foster. ## Tips for Applicants - Browse multiple current seminar listings rather than assuming only one fits — Salzburg Global runs a genuinely varied set of programmes - Tailor your letter of motivation specifically to the seminar's theme, not a generic interest in international dialogue - Check whether your target seminar accepts open applications or works primarily through nomination - Highlight your specific professional relevance to the seminar's sector clearly in your CV - Apply or seek nomination well ahead of the seminar dates, since funding and logistics need to be arranged in advance ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for a Salzburg Global Seminar Fellowship?** Professionals and emerging leaders relevant to a specific seminar's topic; criteria vary by programme. **Is it fully funded?** Funding is full or partial depending on the seminar; check the specific programme's terms. **How do I find seminars to apply to?** By browsing the current list of programmes on the Salzburg Global website. **Do all seminars accept open applications?** No, some work by nomination; check each specific programme. **Where are seminars held?** In Salzburg, Austria. **How long do seminars last?** Multi-day programmes; exact length varies by seminar. ## Apply Now Browse current seminar programmes and apply through the official Salzburg Global "Get Involved" page: https://www.salzburgglobal.org/get-involved
