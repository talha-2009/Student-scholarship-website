The Geneva Challenge: Advancing Development Goals Contest

Introduction

The Geneva Challenge, run by the Geneva Graduate Institute, is built around a deliberately unusual requirement: teams must span at least two different academic disciplines, ruling out single-field teams no matter how strong, in order to compete for a share of the CHF 25,000 prize pool by proposing genuine solutions to global development goals.

About the Opportunity

Teams of three to five master's students develop a full written proposal addressing a development challenge over roughly eleven weeks following registration, with finalist teams, one selected per continent, invited to Geneva to present and defend their proposal before an international jury. The interdisciplinary requirement forces genuinely different perspectives into each team's proposal, distinguishing the competition from single-discipline case competitions common elsewhere.

Benefits

Winning teams share a CHF 25,000 total prize pool across five continental category winners, and the five finalist teams receive fully covered travel to present in Geneva.

Who Can Apply?

Teams of three to five master's-level students from at least two different academic disciplines, currently enrolled at any university worldwide, can enter, with proof of enrollment required for all team members at registration.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The contest centers on international development broadly, welcoming interdisciplinary teams combining any two or more academic fields relevant to their chosen development challenge.

Required Documents

Teams need a registration form, proof of enrollment for all team members, and a full written proposal.

Application Process

Teams register online with proof of enrollment by the registration deadline, then submit a full proposal within roughly eleven weeks; finalists present and defend their proposals in Geneva before a jury.

Deadline

The current submission deadline is 11 July 2026.

Why You Should Apply

For master's students who want to test genuinely interdisciplinary thinking against a real development challenge, the Geneva Challenge offers meaningful prize money and, for finalists, a funded trip to present their work in Geneva before an international jury.

For full details, visit the official Geneva Challenge website: https://www.graduateinstitute.ch/TheGenevaChallenge
