Fulbright Canada All Disciplines Scholar Award

## Introduction Fulbright Canada's All Disciplines Scholar Award funds Canadian PhD-level scholars and established professionals to spend an academic year teaching or researching at a U.S. host institution, spanning virtually any academic field. Unlike Fulbright programmes aimed at students, this award is built for people already established in their careers, and it requires candidates to arrange their own U.S. host affiliation as part of the application itself, making early outreach genuinely central to a strong submission. ## About the Opportunity Scholars spend an eight-month academic year at a U.S. institution they have identified and secured affiliation with themselves, either teaching or conducting research depending on the track they choose. Because the award is open across virtually all disciplines, the specific shape of a scholar's time in the US depends heavily on their own field and the host institution's particular strengths and needs. ## Benefits Scholars receive a US$25,000 award covering the full eight-month academic year, along with basic health insurance and visa and professional support services to help manage the practical logistics of an extended stay in the United States. ## Who Can Apply? Applicants need to be Canadian citizens, or dual US-Canadian citizens with at least five years' residence abroad, holding a PhD or an equivalent terminal degree or professional standing. Strong English proficiency is required, and applicants need to comply with Fulbright Foreign Scholarship Board guidelines throughout the application and award period. ## Fields of Study or Available Areas The award is open across all academic disciplines, reflecting its broad "All Disciplines" framing rather than any specific subject-area restriction. ## Required Documents Applicants need a project statement of up to five pages, a CV or resume, two letters of recommendation, and, depending on their specific track, a bibliography for research awards or syllabi for teaching awards, along with proof of their degree. ## Application Process The defining early step is securing an affiliation with a U.S. host institution willing to support the scholar's proposed research or teaching for the academic year, since this affiliation underpins the entire application. Once secured, applicants submit their online application via Fulbright Canada, including their project statement, CV, and two recommendation letters, along with a bibliography or syllabi depending on whether they are pursuing a research or teaching-focused award. ## Deadline The current application deadline is 15 September 2026. Because arranging a confirmed U.S. host affiliation typically takes real time and coordination, applicants should begin that outreach well ahead of the deadline rather than treating it as a final formality. ## Why You Should Apply For established Canadian scholars and professionals who want a funded academic year in the United States, this award offers substantial support across essentially any field, provided a candidate can secure the necessary host institution relationship. Among exchange programs for established academics, Fulbright Canada's All Disciplines Scholar Award stands out for its genuine breadth and the credibility that comes with the broader Fulbright name. For full application details, visit the official Fulbright Canada page: https://www.fulbright.ca/programs/american-scholars/traditional-all-disciplines
