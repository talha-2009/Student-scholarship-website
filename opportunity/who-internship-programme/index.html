WHO Internship Programme

## Introduction The WHO Internship Programme places graduate students and recent graduates inside the World Health Organization, giving direct exposure to how the UN's lead health agency designs and delivers global public health programmes from its Geneva headquarters or regional and country offices. Like several other UN-family placements, it is unpaid, positioning it as a valuable credential-building experience for students specifically pursuing careers in global public health, epidemiology, or health policy. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** World Health Organization - **Country of Study:** Switzerland (Geneva headquarters, with some regional/country office postings) - **Opportunity Type:** Internship - **Funding Type:** Unpaid - **Degree Level:** Graduate - **Duration:** Varies by posting - **Official Website:** who.int/careers ## Benefits - Practical experience in global public health work - Mentorship from WHO staff - Networking within international health institutions ## Eligibility Criteria - Be currently enrolled in, or have completed, a graduate degree relevant to WHO's work - Meet any additional language or field-specific requirements listed on the specific posting ## Required Documents - CV - A motivation letter - Proof of enrollment or graduation - Language proficiency proof ## Application Process **Step 1.** Visit the WHO Careers internship portal and search for open postings by division and location. **Step 2.** Review each posting's specific requirements, since WHO's work spans many distinct public health domains. **Step 3.** Submit your CV, motivation letter, proof of enrollment/graduation, and language proficiency evidence through the WHO Careers system. **Step 4.** If shortlisted, you may be interviewed by the hiring division. **Step 5.** Successful candidates receive an offer specifying division, location, and duration. ## Important Dates WHO internship postings open on a rolling basis rather than a single fixed annual cycle. Please refer to the official WHO Careers portal for current openings. ## Selection Criteria Hiring divisions assess how well your graduate-level academic background matches the specific health domain of the posting, such as epidemiology, health policy, or emergency response, along with your language proficiency and motivation. ## Tips for Applicants - Check the WHO Careers portal regularly, since postings across many divisions open on a rolling basis - Tailor your motivation letter to the specific division's public health focus, rather than a generic statement of interest in global health - Plan your finances in advance, since the internship is unpaid and Geneva has a relatively high cost of living - Highlight any relevant coursework, research, or fieldwork in public health explicitly - Consider regional or country office postings as well as Geneva headquarters, since they may offer different opportunities ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the WHO Internship Programme?** Graduate students or recent graduates in a field relevant to WHO's work. **Is the WHO internship paid?** No, it is unpaid; interns cover their own living and travel costs. **Where are internships based?** Primarily WHO headquarters in Geneva, with some regional and country office postings. **What languages do I need?** Language proficiency proof is required, matching the specific posting's needs. **How long does the internship last?** Duration varies by specific posting. **What kind of work will I do?** Support for public health programmes, research, or policy work, depending on the division. ## Apply Now Search current openings and apply through the official WHO Careers internship portal: https://www.who.int/careers/internship-programme
