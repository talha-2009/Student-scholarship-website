Internship - Switzerland

WHO Internship Programme

Introduction

The WHO Internship Programme places graduate students and recent graduates inside the World Health Organization, giving direct exposure to how the UN's lead health agency designs and delivers global public health programmes. Like several other UN-family placements, it is unpaid, positioning it as a valuable credential-building experience for students specifically pursuing careers in global public health.

About the Opportunity

Interns support work within a specific WHO division at headquarters in Geneva or a regional or country office, gaining direct insight into how global health policy and programmes are actually developed and implemented, rather than a purely observational placement.

Benefits

Interns gain practical experience in global health, mentorship from WHO staff, and networking within international health institutions.

Who Can Apply?

Graduate students or recent graduates enrolled in or having completed a degree relevant to WHO's work can apply.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Relevant fields include public health, epidemiology, health policy, and related disciplines connected to WHO's global health mandate.

Required Documents

Applicants need a CV, a motivation letter, proof of enrollment or graduation, and language proficiency proof.

Application Process

Applicants apply through the WHO Careers internship portal by submitting an online application and motivation letter.

Deadline

WHO internship postings open on a rolling basis; applicants should check the official WHO Careers portal regularly for current openings.

Why You Should Apply

For students pursuing a career in global public health, WHO's internship offers direct exposure to policy and programme work at the UN's lead health agency, a credential few comparable placements can match.

For current openings, visit the official WHO Careers internship portal: https://www.who.int/careers/internship-programme
