OIST Research Internship Program

Introduction

On a subtropical campus surrounded by ocean and forest, the Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology runs a research internship deliberately designed as a stepping stone toward its own PhD Program, giving undergraduate and master's students a genuine taste of OIST's department-free, cross-disciplinary research environment before committing to doctoral study. With more than 1,100 staff from over 70 countries on campus, interns join a genuinely international scientific community from day one, working directly under OIST faculty rather than in a separate, lower-tier internship track.

About the Opportunity

Interns spend three to six months embedded in a specific OIST research unit matching their academic background, gaining access to the institute's cutting-edge shared facilities, from high-performance computing to advanced imaging and fabrication labs, alongside mentorship from internationally renowned faculty. Because OIST explicitly positions this internship as preparation for its PhD Program, students already leaning toward doctoral study in the sciences are especially well suited to the experience, and internships of four months or longer can even count toward a lab rotation requirement if a student later joins the PhD programme.

Benefits

Interns receive one direct round-trip flight to Okinawa, furnished accommodation on or off campus funded by OIST or the host research unit, a daily living allowance, a commuting allowance, and administrative support including help securing a Certificate of Eligibility for their visa; these benefits apply to the intern only and do not extend to dependents or family members.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need to be in the final two years of a bachelor's degree, enrolled in any year of a master's degree, or recent bachelor's or master's graduates from any institution worldwide, with currently enrolled students needing prior approval from their home institution; current or graduated PhD students are explicitly not eligible for this internship.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields span OIST's full research scope: physics, chemistry, mathematical and computational sciences, marine science, neuroscience, molecular and cellular biology, environmental and ecological science, and quantum science.

Required Documents

Applicants need the online application form, passport details and a photo page scan (or alternative government ID), a recent ID photo, academic transcripts and diploma or enrollment certificate, a personal statement explaining fit with the desired research unit, and at least one academic or research reference letter, without which the application cannot be submitted.

Application Process

Applicants complete the online OIST application system, ensuring at least one recommendation letter has been submitted before final submission is possible; applications are reviewed twice yearly on a competitive basis considering academic fit, research suitability, and available space and funding in the desired unit, with results posted roughly three months after the deadline.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 15 October 2026, for the internship period running from April through September 2027; OIST typically runs two application rounds per year, so a separate deadline exists for internships starting later in the year.

Why You Should Apply

For STEM students considering a future PhD and wanting genuine hands-on research experience in a highly international setting, OIST's internship offers comprehensive funding, access to elite research infrastructure, and a direct on-ramp into the institute's own doctoral programme, all set against Okinawa's distinctive natural and cultural backdrop.

For current openings and full application details, visit the official OIST Research Internship page: https://www.oist.jp/admissions/research-internship
