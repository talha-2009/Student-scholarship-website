DAAD Research Grants for Doctoral Candidates and Young Academics

## Introduction

Doctoral research often reaches a point where a scholar needs sustained time in a specific lab, archive, or academic environment abroad, and DAAD's Research Grants for Doctoral Candidates and Young Academics exist to fund exactly that. Unlike DAAD's degree-focused scholarships, this grant supports research itself, funding doctoral candidates and early-career academics from any country to work with a confirmed German host supervisor on their own research project.

## About the Opportunity

Rather than funding a full degree programme, this grant supports a research stay built around a specific supervised project at a German host institution. The relationship with that host supervisor is central to the whole arrangement, since applicants need a confirmed acceptance from a German professor or institution before they can apply, meaning the intellectual match between the applicant's research and the host supervisor's work has to be established well before any formal application begins.

## Benefits

Grant recipients receive a monthly stipend of €1,300, rising to €1,400 from February 2026, along with health, accident, and liability insurance and a travel allowance covering their journey to Germany. Depending on individual circumstances, additional research or family allowances may also be available, providing further support during the research stay.

## Who Can Apply?

Doctoral candidates and young academics from any country are eligible, provided they hold an above-average degree and have developed a research proposal that has secured supervision from an eligible German host institution. There is no nationality restriction, but the requirement for confirmed German supervision means applicants need to have already built a genuine research relationship before applying, not simply identified a potential institution.

## Fields of Study or Available Areas

The grant is open across all academic fields, supporting doctoral and early-career research wherever a qualified German host supervisor is willing to take on the collaboration.

## Required Documents

Applicants need the DAAD application form, a detailed research proposal, a letter of acceptance from their German host supervisor, a CV, academic transcripts, recommendation letters, and a language certificate.

## Application Process

The essential first step is identifying and contacting a German host institution or professor whose work aligns with the applicant's own research, and securing their agreement to supervise the project. Only once that relationship is confirmed can an applicant submit their full application directly via the DAAD Portal, including the letter of acceptance from their host supervisor alongside their research proposal and other required documents.

## Deadline

The current application deadline is 30 October 2026. Because securing confirmed German host supervision is a prerequisite for applying at all, prospective applicants should begin reaching out to potential hosts well before this date, since that outreach and relationship-building often takes considerably longer than completing the application paperwork itself.

## Why You Should Apply

For doctoral candidates and young academics whose research would genuinely benefit from time at a specific German institution, this grant offers substantial monthly funding without requiring a full degree programme commitment, making it a flexible option compared with degree-focused scholarships. Among research grants for PhD candidates and young academics internationally, DAAD's programme stands out for its openness to any nationality and any field, provided the essential supervisory relationship with a German host is genuinely in place.

For full application details, visit the official DAAD research grants page: https://www.daad.org/en/find-funding/graduate-opportunities/research-grants/
