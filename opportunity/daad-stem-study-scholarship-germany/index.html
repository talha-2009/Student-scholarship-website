Scholarship - Germany

DAAD STEM Study Scholarship

Introduction

Germany's engineering and technical universities rank among the strongest in the world, and DAAD's STEM Study Scholarship exists specifically to bring international engineering and natural science graduates into that system for a fully funded master's degree. Unlike DAAD's broader Study Scholarship open to any discipline, this programme is built exclusively for STEM graduates, and that narrower focus shapes how DAAD evaluates applications, weighing technical academic strength and a clear STEM career trajectory more heavily than general academic versatility.

About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue a full-time master's degree at a German university in an engineering or natural science field, joining programmes that are frequently taught in English as well as German, reflecting the strong international orientation of German technical education. The scholarship covers the full duration of the chosen master's programme, giving scholars the financial stability to focus on coursework and any associated research without needing part-time work to cover living costs.

Benefits

Scholars receive a monthly payment of EUR 992, along with health, accident, and liability insurance and a travel allowance for the journey to Germany. An annual study allowance of EUR 460 helps cover books, materials, and other academic expenses throughout the programme.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need an above-average bachelor's degree in engineering or a natural science field, and generally no more than six years should have passed since completing that first degree. Applicants need to be applying for a full-time STEM master's programme at a German university, with no separate professional experience requirement beyond the underlying academic qualification.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields cover engineering and the natural sciences broadly, giving applicants flexibility to pursue master's study across a wide range of technical disciplines available at German universities.

Required Documents

Applicants need the DAAD application form, a CV, a motivation letter, academic transcripts, a language certificate in either German or English depending on their chosen programme's language of instruction, and recommendation letters.

Application Process

Applicants identify a STEM master's programme in Germany matching their academic background, then apply directly via the DAAD scholarship database and Portal. Because most DAAD scholarships, including this one, are pre-admission, applicants can apply for university admission and DAAD funding at the same time rather than needing a university offer in hand before starting the scholarship application.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 15 November 2026. Given that language certification and recommendation letters can take time to arrange, applicants should begin preparing their materials well ahead of this date.

Why You Should Apply

For engineering and natural science graduates who want a fully funded master's degree at a technically strong German university, this scholarship offers a clear, well-defined path with generous financial support and no requirement for prior professional experience. Among fully funded scholarships for STEM graduates, DAAD's programme stands out for the strength and international orientation of German technical education, plus the flexibility of pre-admission application that lets scholars apply for university and funding together.

For full application details, visit the official DAAD scholarship database listing: https://www2.daad.de/deutschland/stipendium/datenbank/en/21148-scholarship-database/?detail=57742130
