AIESEC Global Volunteer

## Introduction AIESEC Global Volunteer channels the same worldwide network behind AIESEC's paid internship platform into short-term volunteering placements connected to real community projects, many aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals. Open to any young person aged 18 to 30, it offers an accessible entry point into international volunteering for those who want hands-on cross-cultural experience without the longer time commitment of a full internship or degree program abroad. ## About the Opportunity Unlike Global Talent's paid corporate internships, Global Volunteer is self-funded and centers on project-based community work, typically addressing social impact or education-related goals in the host community. Volunteers join a specific project listed on the AIESEC platform, working alongside local organizations and often alongside other international volunteers, gaining direct exposure to community development work in a country and context different from their own. ## Benefits Participants gain genuine cross-cultural volunteering experience and hands-on involvement in project-based impact work, rather than a passive observational role. The program is explicitly framed around leadership development, since volunteers often take on real responsibility within their assigned project, and participants join AIESEC's global network, which many use as a stepping stone toward future international opportunities, including AIESEC's own Global Talent internships. ## Who Can Apply? Eligibility is broad and straightforward: any young person aged 18 to 30 can apply through open application on the AIESEC Global Volunteer platform. There is no specific academic or professional background required, making this one of the more accessible entry points into AIESEC's network compared with programs aimed at students in a specific field or graduates with particular qualifications. ## Fields of Study or Available Areas Projects generally fall under social impact and education themes, though the specific focus of any individual project depends on the local partner organization running it. Volunteers interested in community development, education access, or broader social impact work are likely to find projects aligned with their interests. ## Required Documents Applicants need a completed application profile on the AIESEC platform and a valid passport copy to support travel arrangements. Because the program is self-funded, applicants also need to pay a program fee as part of confirming their placement. ## Application Process Candidates create a profile on the AIESEC Global Volunteer platform and apply directly to specific listed projects that interest them. Selection is generally more accessible than AIESEC's paid internship listings, though candidates should still expect some level of screening by the local organization hosting the project. Once accepted, volunteers pay the program fee and receive guidance on travel and preparation ahead of their placement. ## Deadline Global Volunteer projects are listed on a rolling basis rather than following one fixed annual deadline, so interested applicants should check the platform regularly for current openings in locations and themes that interest them. ## Why You Should Apply For young people who want a genuine taste of international volunteering without committing to a full academic year or a formal internship contract, Global Volunteer offers a manageable, self-funded way to gain cross-cultural experience and contribute to real community projects. As a youth program with a low barrier to entry and a global reach, it works well both as a standalone experience and as a first step toward AIESEC's broader network of international opportunities for young people. To browse current project listings and start an application, visit the official AIESEC Global Volunteer platform: https://aiesec.org/global-volunteer
