Chevening Scholarship

## Introduction Every August, the UK Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office opens applications for Chevening, Britain's flagship scholarship for future global leaders. Since its founding, Chevening has sent tens of thousands of scholars to UK universities for a fully funded year of master's study, building a worldwide network of alumni who go on to shape policy, business, and civil society in their home countries. For early-to-mid-career professionals who already have a clear sense of the impact they want to make, Chevening offers not just funding but genuine entry into that global network. ## About the Opportunity Chevening operates through UK embassies and high commissions in more than 160 countries and territories, each running its own local application and interview process feeding into a single global scholarship. Unlike scholarships aimed at recent graduates, Chevening is explicitly designed for people who already have real professional experience and a demonstrated pattern of leadership, values that shape both the eligibility criteria and how applications are assessed. Scholars spend one academic year at a UK university of their choosing, studying any subject available. ## Benefits Chevening covers full tuition fees for one eligible master's programme, along with a monthly living stipend intended to cover day-to-day costs throughout the academic year. The scholarship includes economy-class return airfare, arrival and departure allowances, and coverage of the UK student visa application cost. Scholars also receive a travel grant specifically for attending official Chevening events during their year in the UK, reinforcing the network-building dimension that runs through the whole program. ## Who Can Apply? Applicants need to be citizens of a Chevening-eligible country, hold an undergraduate degree broadly equivalent to a UK 2:1 honours degree, and have at least two years of work experience, calculated as 2,800 hours, which can combine full-time, part-time, and voluntary work. Chevening does not set a fixed age limit, focusing instead on leadership potential and professional achievement. A firm condition of the award is returning to one's home country for at least two years after the scholarship ends, reflecting Chevening's goal of building impact at home rather than facilitating permanent emigration. ## Fields of Study or Available Areas Chevening funds study in essentially any subject offered by a UK university at master's level, giving scholars genuine flexibility to pursue whatever field best serves their career goals, with only a small number of restricted subjects excluded. ## Required Documents Applicants need completed education documents and transcripts, an English language qualification meeting their chosen course's requirements, up to three conditional offers from eligible UK master's courses, two references, and personal statement essays addressing Chevening's specific themes of leadership, networking, and career planning. ## Application Process The Chevening application portal opens in early August and closes in early October, and applicants complete essays addressing leadership and influence, networking, and their career plan, which together form the core of how candidates are assessed. Separately from the Chevening application itself, candidates need to apply directly to their chosen UK universities to secure a conditional offer, since Chevening requires proof of admission before finalizing scholarships. Shortlisted candidates are interviewed at their local British embassy or high commission, typically between February and March. ## Deadline The current application deadline is 7 October 2026. Given how competitive Chevening is and how much coordination is required between the Chevening application and separate university applications, candidates should begin preparing well before the portal opens in August rather than waiting until closer to the October deadline. ## Why You Should Apply Few scholarships combine full funding with Chevening's emphasis on leadership and its genuinely global alumni network spanning government, business, and civil society. For professionals with a track record of leadership and a specific plan for using UK study to create change back home, Chevening remains one of the most respected fully funded scholarships available anywhere, and its selection process rewards candidates who can articulate that plan clearly and specifically rather than in general terms. For full eligibility details and to begin an application, visit the official Chevening Scholarships website: https://www.chevening.org/scholarships/
