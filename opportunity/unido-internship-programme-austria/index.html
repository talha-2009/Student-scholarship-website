UNIDO Internship Programme

Introduction

Headquartered in Vienna, UNIDO, the United Nations Industrial Development Organization, runs an internship programme giving students exposure to how the UN approaches industrialization and sustainable development as an economic engine, distinct from the broader development mandates of agencies like UNDP. For students genuinely interested in the intersection of industrial policy, engineering, and development economics, UNIDO offers a focused, specialized entry point into UN Secretariat-adjacent work.

About the Opportunity

Interns support technical and policy projects connected to industrialization, technology transfer, and sustainable economic development, based either at UNIDO headquarters in Vienna or at one of its field offices worldwide, depending on the specific posting. Because UNIDO's mandate is more narrowly technical than many general development agencies, interns typically gain deeper, more specialized exposure to industrial policy work rather than the broader generalist experience some UN internships offer.

Benefits

Interns receive hands-on training on industrialization and development projects, and practical exposure to UNIDO's specific technical and policy work; the internship is unpaid, with travel, accommodation, and insurance costs the intern's own responsibility.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants enrolled in, or having completed within the past year, a graduate programme or the final year of a bachelor's degree in a field relevant to industrial development can apply, with a strong command of English or French required.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Relevant fields include industrial development, engineering, economics, and environmental studies, reflecting UNIDO's specific mandate around industrialization and sustainable economic development.

Required Documents

Applicants need a CV or resume, a cover letter, proof of enrollment or graduation, and proof of health insurance.

Application Process

Applicants apply directly via the UNIDO Careers portal to specific internship postings at either Vienna headquarters or a field office.

Deadline

UNIDO internship postings open on a rolling basis rather than a single fixed annual cycle; applicants should check the official UNIDO Careers portal regularly for current openings.

Why You Should Apply

For students with a genuine interest in industrial policy and development economics specifically, UNIDO offers more specialized, technically-focused exposure than broader UN development internships, making it a strong fit for students whose academic background already leans toward engineering or industrial economics.

For current openings, visit the official UNIDO Careers portal: https://careers.unido.org/content/Internship/?locale=en_US
