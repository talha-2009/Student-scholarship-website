UNIDO Internship Programme

## Introduction The UNIDO Internship Programme places students and recent graduates inside the United Nations Industrial Development Organization, headquartered in Vienna, which works on industrialization, technology transfer, and sustainable economic development in developing and transition economies. It offers hands-on exposure to how the UN approaches industrial and economic development specifically, distinct from the more general development mandates of agencies like UNDP, and is well suited to students with backgrounds in engineering, economics, or industrial policy. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** United Nations Industrial Development Organization (UNIDO) - **Country of Study:** Austria (Vienna headquarters, with some field-office postings) - **Opportunity Type:** Internship - **Funding Type:** Unpaid - **Degree Level:** Bachelor's (final year) or Master's - **Duration:** Varies by posting, generally a few months - **Official Website:** careers.unido.org ## Benefits - Hands-on training on real industrialization and development projects - Practical exposure to UNIDO's technical and policy work - Professional experience within a specialised UN agency - Unpaid — travel, accommodation, and insurance costs are the intern's own responsibility ## Eligibility Criteria - Be currently enrolled in a graduate programme, or in the final year of a bachelor's degree, in a field relevant to industrial development, or have completed either within the past year - Have a strong command of English or French - Meet any additional requirements specified by the particular division or field office you apply to ## Required Documents - CV/resume - Cover letter tailored to the specific posting - Proof of enrollment or graduation - Proof of health insurance ## Application Process **Step 1.** Visit the UNIDO Careers portal and search open internship postings, either at Vienna headquarters or in field offices. **Step 2.** Review each posting's specific division and requirements, since UNIDO's work spans a wide range of technical and policy areas. **Step 3.** Submit your CV, cover letter, proof of enrollment, and proof of health insurance through the UNIDO Careers system. **Step 4.** If shortlisted, you may be interviewed by the hiring division. **Step 5.** Successful candidates receive an offer specifying location, division, and duration. ## Important Dates UNIDO internship postings open on a rolling basis rather than a single fixed annual cycle. Please refer to the official UNIDO Careers portal for current openings and their individual deadlines. ## Selection Criteria UNIDO hiring divisions assess how well your academic background — often engineering, economics, environmental science, or industrial policy — matches the specific technical area of the posting, along with your written communication skills in English or French. ## Tips for Applicants - Check the UNIDO Careers portal regularly, since postings open across many divisions and field offices on a rolling basis - Tailor your cover letter to the specific technical area of the posting, since UNIDO's mandate is more specialised than general development work - Plan your finances in advance, since the internship is unpaid and typically based in Vienna - Highlight any coursework or project experience in industrial policy, engineering, or sustainable development explicitly - Consider field-office postings as well as Vienna headquarters, since they may offer different or less competitive opportunities ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the UNIDO Internship Programme?** Final-year bachelor's students, master's students, or recent graduates in a relevant field. **Is the UNIDO internship paid?** No, it is unpaid, and interns cover their own travel, accommodation, and insurance. **Where is the internship based?** Primarily UNIDO headquarters in Vienna, with some field-office postings. **What languages do I need?** A strong command of English or French. **What kind of work will I do?** Support for technical and policy projects related to industrialization and sustainable development, depending on the division. **Can I apply to field offices instead of Vienna?** Yes, some UNIDO internship postings are based in field offices rather than headquarters. ## Apply Now Search current openings and apply directly through the official UNIDO Careers portal: https://careers.unido.org/content/Internship/?locale=en_US
