Churchill Scholarship at Cambridge

Introduction

Established at Sir Winston Churchill's own request as part of founding Churchill College, Cambridge, the Churchill Scholarship funds one year of intensive Master's study in engineering, mathematics, or the sciences for exceptional American students, deliberately built around advancing US-UK scientific collaboration. Unlike scholarships that weigh leadership potential or broad extracurricular involvement, Churchill selects almost entirely on academic and research achievement, seeking what the Foundation itself calls candidates with "interesting jagged edges" rather than conventionally well-rounded profiles.

About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue an MPhil or MASt at Churchill College, the only Cambridge college with a dedicated STEM focus, typically through full-time lab-based research culminating in a thesis and oral examination, or through a small number of eligible part-taught courses in areas like Advanced Computer Science or specific engineering tracks. The programme funds up to 18 scholarships annually, 16 in science, mathematics, and engineering plus two Kanders Churchill Scholarships specifically in science policy, with each participating US institution permitted to nominate only two candidates each year.

Benefits

Scholars receive full University and Churchill College tuition and fees, round-trip airfare to the UK, visa fees and the NHS health surcharge, a living stipend set higher than the standard UK Research Council rate, and eligibility to apply for an additional USD 4,000 special research grant, bringing the total award value to roughly USD 65,000 to USD 80,000.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need to be US citizens, either native-born or naturalized, who are graduating seniors or graduated within the past 12 months from one of the roughly 138 participating institutions, holding a bachelor's degree or equivalent and not yet having attained a doctorate; there is no official minimum GPA, though recent Scholars have typically had a GPA of 3.7 or above.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields span engineering, mathematics, and the sciences broadly, including subjects like chemistry, computer science, genetics, and various engineering disciplines, with the Kanders track specifically covering science policy.

Required Documents

Applicants need the online application form, a one-page Proposed Program of Study, a two-page Academic and Research History, official transcripts, four academic letters of recommendation, and, for most research-based courses, correspondence with a prospective Cambridge lab supervisor indicating willingness to accept the student.

Application Process

Applicants must first secure nomination from a participating US institution, since Churchill does not accept unendorsed applications; nominated candidates then complete the national online application while separately applying to the University of Cambridge itself, listing Churchill College as their first choice, by Cambridge's own mid-October admissions deadline.

Deadline

The national Churchill Scholarship application deadline typically falls in early November, following individual institutional nomination deadlines that are generally set in September or October; applicants should confirm both their home institution's internal deadline and the national date well in advance.

Why You Should Apply

For outstanding US STEM students who want intensive, well-funded research experience at one of the world's leading scientific institutions, Churchill offers substantial funding and access to Cambridge's non-hierarchical, research-focused academic culture, though its selectivity and reliance on institutional nomination mean strong applicants should engage their campus fellowships office early.

For full eligibility and application details, visit the official Churchill Scholarship website: https://www.churchillscholarship.org/
