Harvard Model United Nations (HMUN)

Introduction

Each year, Harvard undergraduates organize one of the world's most established Model United Nations conferences for high school students, simulating the negotiation, procedure, and diplomacy of real UN committees. Harvard Model United Nations, HMUN, brings school delegations from around the world to debate global issues under committee rules modeled closely on actual UN proceedings, giving participants a genuinely rigorous introduction to formal diplomatic negotiation years before they might encounter it professionally.

About the Opportunity

Delegations, organized by school, are assigned specific countries or roles within simulated UN committees, and students spend the conference researching, negotiating, and drafting resolutions in character as their assigned country's representative. Because participation runs through school delegations rather than individual applications, the actual experience of attending HMUN depends heavily on how active and organized a student's own school Model UN programme is.

Benefits

Participants gain genuine diplomatic simulation experience, meaningful leadership skill-building through committee negotiation and public speaking, and exposure to a conference organized and run by Harvard-affiliated students, giving younger participants early contact with the kind of academic environment they may aspire to join later.

Who Can Apply?

High school students typically register as part of a school delegation rather than applying individually, meaning eligibility in practice depends on a student's school having an active Model UN programme willing to register a delegation for HMUN specifically.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

HMUN centers on international relations and diplomacy, giving students practical exposure to global policy debate, negotiation, and formal parliamentary procedure.

Required Documents

Participation requires school delegation registration, payment of the relevant conference fee, and faculty advisor confirmation supporting the delegation's attendance.

Application Process

Students interested in attending need to work through their school's Model UN programme, since registration happens at the delegation level via the official HMUN website rather than through individual student applications. Schools without an existing Model UN club would need to establish one and register a delegation to participate, making early engagement with a school's Model UN faculty advisor the practical first step for any interested student.

Deadline

Deadlines vary by conference year and are set through the official HMUN registration process. Prospective delegations should check the official HMUN website directly for the current year's registration timeline.

Why You Should Apply

For high school students serious about international relations, diplomacy, or debate, HMUN offers one of the more rigorous and well-established Model UN conferences available, with the added credibility that comes from Harvard student organization. Among conferences aimed at high schoolers with an interest in global affairs, HMUN's scale and reputation make it a strong goal for any student whose school is building or already running an active Model UN programme.

For registration details, visit the official HMUN website: https://hmun.org/
