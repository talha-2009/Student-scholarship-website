UNDP Internship Programme

## Introduction The UNDP Internship Programme places graduate-level students inside the United Nations Development Programme, the UN's lead agency for international development work spanning poverty reduction, governance, climate action, and crisis response. Unlike the general UN Secretariat internship, UNDP is explicitly graduate-focused and requires you to return to your studies afterward, positioning it as a substantive mid-degree experience rather than a stopgap between degrees. Its paid stipend, while modest and location-dependent, sets it apart from several unpaid UN-family placements. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) - **Country of Study:** Global — UNDP country offices and headquarters worldwide - **Opportunity Type:** Internship - **Funding Type:** Paid - **Degree Level:** Graduate (enrolled master's or PhD) - **Duration:** Minimum 3 consecutive months, minimum 24 hours per week - **Official Website:** undp.org/careers ## Benefits - A monthly stipend, with the exact rate varying by duty station and whether the internship is remote or in-person - Direct professional exposure to UNDP's global development programming - No guarantee of future employment is implied or promised by the internship itself ## Eligibility Criteria - Be currently enrolled in a graduate-level degree programme in a development-related field - Commit to returning to your studies after the internship ends — UNDP internships are explicitly designed around this condition - Be available to work at least three consecutive months, at a minimum of 24 hours per week - Maintain valid health insurance coverage for the duration of the internship ## Required Documents - CV/resume - Proof of current graduate enrollment - Proof of health insurance enrollment - A cover letter tailored to the specific posting ## Application Process **Step 1.** Visit the UNDP Jobs page and search for open internship vacancies by country office or theme. **Step 2.** Some offices require applications to be sent directly by email to a designated internship coordinator rather than through the central jobs portal — check each posting's specific instructions. **Step 3.** Submit your CV, proof of enrollment, proof of health insurance, and a tailored cover letter as required. **Step 4.** If shortlisted, you may be contacted for an interview by the hiring office. **Step 5.** Successful candidates receive an offer specifying duty station, stipend, and duration, contingent on maintaining your graduate enrollment throughout. ## Important Dates UNDP internship postings open on a rolling basis across many country offices rather than following one global annual cycle. Please refer to the official UNDP Jobs page for currently open postings and their specific deadlines. ## Selection Criteria UNDP hiring teams look for a clear match between your graduate field of study and the specific development theme of the team you're applying to, along with your practical availability to commit the required minimum hours and duration. ## Tips for Applicants - Check the UNDP Jobs page regularly and note that some offices want direct email applications rather than portal submissions — read each posting carefully - Make sure you can genuinely commit to the minimum three-month, 24-hour-per-week requirement before applying, since this is strictly enforced - Arrange health insurance coverage in advance, since proof is a mandatory document - Target postings that clearly connect to your specific graduate specialization, since UNDP's work spans many distinct development themes - Be clear in your cover letter that you will return to your studies afterward, since this is an explicit eligibility condition ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the UNDP Internship Programme?** Students currently enrolled in a graduate-level degree in a development-related field. **Is the UNDP internship paid?** Yes, a monthly stipend is provided, though the exact amount depends on duty station and remote/in-person status. **How long does the internship last?** A minimum of three consecutive months, at least 24 hours per week. **Do I need health insurance?** Yes, proof of health insurance enrollment is a required document. **Does the internship guarantee a job afterward?** No, UNDP explicitly states there is no guarantee of future employment. **Can undergraduate students apply?** No, this internship is specifically for students enrolled in a graduate-level programme. ## Apply Now Browse current openings and application instructions on the official UNDP Careers page: https://www.undp.org/careers
