UNDP Internship Programme

## Introduction

UNDP, the United Nations Development Programme, runs an internship explicitly built for graduate-level students in development-related fields, distinguishing it from the general UN Secretariat internship by requiring candidates to return to their studies afterward, positioning it as a substantive mid-degree experience rather than a stopgap between programmes. Its paid stipend, while modest and location-dependent, sets it apart from several unpaid UN-family placements.

## About the Opportunity

Interns support UNDP's global development programming, working within a specific country office or headquarters division for a minimum of three consecutive months at least 24 hours per week, gaining hands-on exposure to how the UN's lead development agency operates on the ground and centrally.

## Benefits

Interns receive a monthly stipend, with the rate varying by duty station and remote or in-person status; there is no guarantee of future employment following the internship.

## Who Can Apply?

Applicants enrolled in a graduate-level degree programme in a development-related field can apply, provided they will return to their studies after the internship and can commit at least three consecutive months at a minimum of 24 hours per week.

## Fields of Study or Available Areas

The internship spans development-related fields broadly, matching UNDP's wide-ranging work across poverty reduction, governance, climate, and crisis response.

## Required Documents

Applicants need a CV or resume, proof of graduate enrollment, proof of health insurance enrollment, and a cover letter.

## Application Process

Applicants apply directly to internship vacancies posted on the UNDP Jobs page, with some offices requiring applications by email to a designated internship coordinator instead.

## Deadline

Postings open on a rolling basis rather than a single annual cycle; check the UNDP Jobs page regularly.

## Why You Should Apply

For graduate students in development-related fields, UNDP's paid internship offers meaningful, hands-on exposure to the UN's lead development agency, with a stipend that sets it apart from several unpaid comparable placements.

For full details, visit the official UNDP Careers page: https://www.undp.org/careers
