DAAD EPOS (Development-Related Postgraduate Courses)

DAAD scholarship for professionals from developing countries with 2+ years of relevant work experience, funding a postgraduate degree in a development-related field at a German university. Covers stipend, travel, and insurance. EPOS deadlines are typically university-specific (roughly Jul-Dec 2026 depending on program) — confirm the exact date for your chosen course on the official DAAD database.

DAAD EPOS Eligibility

The DAAD EPOS Scholarship is open to professionals from developing and threshold countries listed on the DAC ODA Recipients list. Applicants must hold a relevant undergraduate degree (typically a four-year programme) and have completed at least two years of professional work experience in a development-related field. Applicants must not have resided in Germany for more than 15 months at the time of application. Language requirements vary by programme — some courses are taught in English, while others require German proficiency (typically DSH-2 or TestDaF). Check the specific programme's requirements before applying.

DAAD EPOS Benefits and Funding

The DAAD EPOS Scholarship is fully funded and provides a monthly stipend of €934 for Master's students or €1,300 for doctoral candidates, a travel allowance, comprehensive health, accident, and personal liability insurance, and tuition fee waivers where applicable. Additional benefits include a one-time research allowance, rent subsidies, and allowances for accompanying family members. The scholarship is designed to remove financial barriers for professionals from developing countries seeking advanced training in development-relevant fields.

DAAD EPOS Deadline and Timeline

Unlike many DAAD programmes with a single deadline, EPOS deadlines are programme-specific and typically fall between July and December 2026 depending on the university and course. Some programmes with early deadlines may close as early as July. Always confirm the exact deadline for your chosen EPOS course on the official DAAD database. It is advisable to begin your application at least three months before the deadline.

Required Documents for the DAAD EPOS Scholarship

  • DAAD application form — completed via the DAAD online portal
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV) — in tabular format, detailing education and professional experience
  • Motivation letter — explaining your development goals and why you chose this specific programme
  • Academic transcripts and degree certificates — certified copies of your undergraduate degree and transcripts
  • Proof of professional experience — employment certificates or reference letters confirming at least two years of relevant work
  • Language proficiency certificate — English (IELTS/TOEFL) or German (DSH/TestDaF) as required by the programme
  • Two academic or professional references — letters of recommendation
  • Development-related statement — a brief essay on how your studies will contribute to development in your home country

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Not meeting the work experience requirement — you must have at least two years of relevant professional experience. If you haven't reached this threshold yet, consider gaining more experience before applying.
  2. Applying to a non-EPOS programme — not all German university programmes are eligible. Verify your chosen course appears on the DAAD EPOS list before applying.
  3. Writing a weak motivation letter — the selection committee looks for a clear connection between your professional background, the programme, and your future development impact. Be specific about your goals.
  4. Missing the programme-specific deadline — deadlines vary significantly between courses. Don't assume all EPOS deadlines are the same; check each programme individually.
