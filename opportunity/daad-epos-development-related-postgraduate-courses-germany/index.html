DAAD EPOS (Development-Related Postgraduate Courses)

## Introduction Most DAAD scholarships fund study at any German university a candidate is admitted to. EPOS, short for development-related postgraduate courses, works differently: it funds a curated list of specific master's and PhD programmes chosen because they build skills directly relevant to development challenges, and it is aimed squarely at professionals from developing and newly industrialising countries who already have real work experience behind them. For someone already working on development issues who wants targeted further qualification rather than open-ended study, EPOS is one of DAAD's more precisely aimed scholarships. ## About the Opportunity EPOS candidates do not choose freely from Germany's full range of master's programmes. Instead, they select from a specific, pre-approved list of courses covering fields like public policy, engineering, economics, and environmental management, each chosen because DAAD considers it directly relevant to addressing development challenges. This structure means the scholarship and the academic programme are tied together from the start, with university admission and DAAD funding nomination happening through the same initial application. ## Benefits Master's-level EPOS scholars receive a monthly payment of €992, while doctoral scholars receive between €1,300 and €1,400. All scholars receive health, accident, and liability insurance, a travel allowance covering the journey to and from Germany, and a study allowance to help with books and materials. Depending on individual circumstances, family and language-course benefits may also apply, adding further support for scholars balancing study with family responsibilities. ## Who Can Apply? Applicants need to be graduates from a developing or newly industrialising country, as defined by DAAD's own country classifications, with at least two years of relevant professional experience since completing their first degree. An above-average academic record from that first degree is expected, and, critically, applicants need to be applying to, or already admitted into, a specific course listed on the official EPOS course list, since funding is not available for courses outside that list. ## Fields of Study or Available Areas EPOS-listed courses span public policy, engineering, economics, environmental management, and related development-relevant fields, though the exact list of eligible programmes changes periodically, so applicants need to consult the current EPOS course list directly rather than assuming a specific programme qualifies. ## Required Documents Applicants need the DAAD application form, a CV in Europass format that must be hand-signed, a hand-signed motivation letter explaining their development-related goals, academic transcripts and degree certificates, a language proficiency certificate matching the programme's language of instruction, and recommendation letters, ideally including one from a current or recent employer given the scholarship's professional-experience focus. ## Application Process The process starts with browsing the official EPOS course list to identify a specific programme matching both an applicant's professional background and development-related goals. Applicants then apply directly to that university and course, since EPOS applications run through the individual programme rather than a single central DAAD form. If the university nominates a candidate for EPOS funding, additional steps through the DAAD Portal finalize the scholarship itself, meaning strong performance in the university's own admissions review is a prerequisite for scholarship consideration. ## Deadline The current application deadline is 15 October 2026, though because EPOS deadlines are set at the level of individual listed programmes rather than centrally by DAAD, applicants should confirm the specific date for their chosen course directly. ## Why You Should Apply For development professionals who already know which specific field they want to deepen their expertise in, EPOS offers a scholarship built around exactly that kind of targeted, professionally-relevant study, rather than a generic funded degree. Among fully funded international scholarships, EPOS stands out for explicitly valuing prior work experience as much as academic record, making it a strong option for candidates who might otherwise feel a typical scholarship undervalues their professional background. For the current EPOS course list and full application details, visit the official DAAD scholarship database: https://www2.daad.de/deutschland/stipendium/datenbank/en/21148-scholarship-database/?detail=50076777

DAAD EPOS Eligibility

The DAAD EPOS Scholarship is open to professionals from developing and threshold countries listed on the DAC ODA Recipients list. Applicants must hold a relevant undergraduate degree (typically a four-year programme) and have completed at least two years of professional work experience in a development-related field. Applicants must not have resided in Germany for more than 15 months at the time of application. Language requirements vary by programme — some courses are taught in English, while others require German proficiency (typically DSH-2 or TestDaF). Check the specific programme's requirements before applying.

DAAD EPOS Benefits and Funding

The DAAD EPOS Scholarship is fully funded and provides a monthly stipend of €934 for Master's students or €1,300 for doctoral candidates, a travel allowance, comprehensive health, accident, and personal liability insurance, and tuition fee waivers where applicable. Additional benefits include a one-time research allowance, rent subsidies, and allowances for accompanying family members. The scholarship is designed to remove financial barriers for professionals from developing countries seeking advanced training in development-relevant fields.

DAAD EPOS Deadline and Timeline

Unlike many DAAD programmes with a single deadline, EPOS deadlines are programme-specific and typically fall between July and December 2026 depending on the university and course. Some programmes with early deadlines may close as early as July. Always confirm the exact deadline for your chosen EPOS course on the official DAAD database. It is advisable to begin your application at least three months before the deadline.

Required Documents for the DAAD EPOS Scholarship

  • DAAD application form — completed via the DAAD online portal
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV) — in tabular format, detailing education and professional experience
  • Motivation letter — explaining your development goals and why you chose this specific programme
  • Academic transcripts and degree certificates — certified copies of your undergraduate degree and transcripts
  • Proof of professional experience — employment certificates or reference letters confirming at least two years of relevant work
  • Language proficiency certificate — English (IELTS/TOEFL) or German (DSH/TestDaF) as required by the programme
  • Two academic or professional references — letters of recommendation
  • Development-related statement — a brief essay on how your studies will contribute to development in your home country

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Not meeting the work experience requirement — you must have at least two years of relevant professional experience. If you haven't reached this threshold yet, consider gaining more experience before applying.
  2. Applying to a non-EPOS programme — not all German university programmes are eligible. Verify your chosen course appears on the DAAD EPOS list before applying.
  3. Writing a weak motivation letter — the selection committee looks for a clear connection between your professional background, the programme, and your future development impact. Be specific about your goals.
  4. Missing the programme-specific deadline — deadlines vary significantly between courses. Don't assume all EPOS deadlines are the same; check each programme individually.
