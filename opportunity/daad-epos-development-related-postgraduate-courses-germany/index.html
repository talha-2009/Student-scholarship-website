Scholarship - Germany

DAAD EPOS (Development-Related Postgraduate Courses)

Introduction

Most DAAD scholarships fund study at any German university a candidate is admitted to. EPOS, short for development-related postgraduate courses, works differently: it funds a curated list of specific master's and PhD programmes chosen because they build skills directly relevant to development challenges, and it is aimed squarely at professionals from developing and newly industrialising countries who already have real work experience behind them. For someone already working on development issues who wants targeted further qualification rather than open-ended study, EPOS is one of DAAD's more precisely aimed scholarships.

About the Opportunity

EPOS candidates do not choose freely from Germany's full range of master's programmes. Instead, they select from a specific, pre-approved list of courses covering fields like public policy, engineering, economics, and environmental management, each chosen because DAAD considers it directly relevant to addressing development challenges. This structure means the scholarship and the academic programme are tied together from the start, with university admission and DAAD funding nomination happening through the same initial application.

Benefits

Master's-level EPOS scholars receive a monthly payment of EUR 992, while doctoral scholars receive between EUR 1,300 and EUR 1,400. All scholars receive health, accident, and liability insurance, a travel allowance covering the journey to and from Germany, and a study allowance to help with books and materials. Depending on individual circumstances, family and language-course benefits may also apply, adding further support for scholars balancing study with family responsibilities.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need to be graduates from a developing or newly industrialising country, as defined by DAAD's own country classifications, with at least two years of relevant professional experience since completing their first degree. An above-average academic record from that first degree is expected, and, critically, applicants need to be applying to, or already admitted into, a specific course listed on the official EPOS course list, since funding is not available for courses outside that list.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

EPOS-listed courses span public policy, engineering, economics, environmental management, and related development-relevant fields, though the exact list of eligible programmes changes periodically, so applicants need to consult the current EPOS course list directly rather than assuming a specific programme qualifies.

Required Documents

Applicants need the DAAD application form, a CV in Europass format that must be hand-signed, a hand-signed motivation letter explaining their development-related goals, academic transcripts and degree certificates, a language proficiency certificate matching the programme's language of instruction, and recommendation letters, ideally including one from a current or recent employer given the scholarship's professional-experience focus.

Application Process

The process starts with browsing the official EPOS course list to identify a specific programme matching both an applicant's professional background and development-related goals. Applicants then apply directly to that university and course, since EPOS applications run through the individual programme rather than a single central DAAD form. If the university nominates a candidate for EPOS funding, additional steps through the DAAD Portal finalize the scholarship itself, meaning strong performance in the university's own admissions review is a prerequisite for scholarship consideration.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 15 October 2026, though because EPOS deadlines are set at the level of individual listed programmes rather than centrally by DAAD, applicants should confirm the specific date for their chosen course directly.

Why You Should Apply

For development professionals who already know which specific field they want to deepen their expertise in, EPOS offers a scholarship built around exactly that kind of targeted, professionally-relevant study, rather than a generic funded degree. Among fully funded international scholarships, EPOS stands out for explicitly valuing prior work experience as much as academic record, making it a strong option for candidates who might otherwise feel a typical scholarship undervalues their professional background.

For the current EPOS course list and full application details, visit the official DAAD scholarship database: https://www2.daad.de/deutschland/stipendium/datenbank/en/21148-scholarship-database/?detail=50076777
