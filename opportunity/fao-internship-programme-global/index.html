FAO Internship Programme

The FAO Internship Programme is a verified United Nations internship route for students and recent graduates who want practical experience in food, agriculture and sustainable development. It is hosted by the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations and is designed to connect academic learning with real programme work. FAO's mandate spans food security, nutrition, agricultural development, fisheries, forestry, climate resilience, rural transformation, data, policy and emergency response, so internship assignments may involve a wide range of technical and support areas.

The programme is intended for applicants who can contribute to FAO's Strategic Framework and the Sustainable Development Goals while learning from an international, multicultural workplace. Interns may support research, technical analysis, communications, programme operations, data work or administrative tasks depending on the office and assignment. Because FAO works through headquarters, regional offices and country offices, applicants should read each Call for Expression of Interest carefully to understand the duty station and assignment requirements.

Eligibility is specific and should be checked before applying. Candidates must be nationals of FAO Member countries and must either be enrolled in an undergraduate or graduate programme at a recognized institution or be recent graduates. They need working knowledge of at least one FAO official language, with a second official language considered an asset. FAO also indicates an expected age range of 21 to 30 at the start of the internship, plus residence or visa readiness for the assignment country. Candidates with close family members employed by FAO are not eligible.

The application process runs through the official FAO careers portal. Applicants should prepare a careful motivation letter, evidence of university attendance or graduation, and any additional documents requested in the active call. The best applications explain the connection between the applicant's academic background and FAO's work, show multicultural communication ability, and demonstrate readiness to contribute to practical assignments. Since calls can change, applicants should verify the exact closing date and assignment details on the career portal before submitting.

Who should apply

Applicants must be nationals of FAO Member countries, be students in undergraduate or graduate programmes or recent graduates, have working knowledge of at least one FAO official language, normally be aged 21 to 30 at the start of the internship, and meet residence or visa requirements for the duty station.

Benefits explained

The programme gives selected students and recent graduates practical experience connected to FAO's mandate, Strategic Framework and the Sustainable Development Goals. Interns gain exposure to international development work, technical teams and multicultural collaboration while contributing skills relevant to food and agriculture.

Required documents

Online career portal application, motivation letter, evidence of attendance at a recognized university and any documents requested in the relevant Call for Expression of Interest.

Application process

Candidates apply online to the official FAO Call for Expression of Interest published on the FAO careers portal. The application should include a motivation letter and university attendance evidence. Only selected candidates are contacted, and candidates not selected before a call closes are asked to reapply to new calls if they want continued consideration.

Selection criteria

FAO considers educational background, relevance to FAO's mandate, language ability, age eligibility, adaptability, communication skills, basic computer skills and suitability for available assignments.

Important notes

The official FAO page explains that candidates apply through Calls for Expression of Interest on the career portal. Applicants should check the active call before submitting because duty stations, terms and closing dates can vary by call.
