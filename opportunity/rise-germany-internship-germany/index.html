Internship - Germany

RISE Germany Internship

## Introduction

Every summer, DAAD's RISE Germany programme connects undergraduate students from the US, Canada, UK, and Ireland with German university and research institute labs, letting motivated students choose their own research placement from a searchable database rather than being assigned one centrally. Because students apply directly to specific listed projects with tailored motivation letters, RISE functions more like a targeted job search than a single generic scholarship application, rewarding candidates willing to invest real effort in identifying the right fit.

## About the Opportunity

Interns spend a summer research placement, typically around two to three months, embedded in a German lab working alongside doctoral researchers on real ongoing projects in biology, chemistry, computer science, physics, earth sciences, or engineering. A distinctive feature of the RISE experience is the annual RISE meeting in Heidelberg, where interns from across Germany gather to connect with each other, building a peer network that spans the entire summer cohort rather than just their individual host lab.

## Benefits

Interns receive a monthly stipend of EUR 992, pro-rated to the length of their internship, health, accident, and liability insurance, an international travel subsidy, a EUR 160 travel stipend for the RISE meeting in Heidelberg, and housing assistance arranged by their host institute.

## Who Can Apply?

Undergraduate students who have completed at least two years of study, and who are citizens or students at a university in the US, Canada, UK, or Ireland, studying biology, chemistry, computer science, physics, earth sciences, or engineering, can apply.

## Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields include biology, chemistry, computer science, physics, earth sciences, and engineering, reflecting the STEM research focus of participating German host labs.

## Required Documents

Applicants need an online application form, an official transcript, a certificate of enrollment showing expected graduation date, a motivation letter tailored to each specific project, and a signed reference letter.

## Application Process

Applicants register for a DAAD ID, browse the RISE Germany internship database for specific host projects, and apply directly to those listings with individually tailored motivation letters, since selection is made by each host professor rather than a central committee.

## Deadline

The current application deadline is 30 November 2026.

## Why You Should Apply

For undergraduate STEM students who want genuine funded research experience in Germany with real choice over their specific lab and project, RISE Germany offers comprehensive funding and a built-in peer network through the Heidelberg meeting that few comparable undergraduate research internships provide.

For full details, visit the official RISE Germany database: https://www.daad.de/rise/en/rise-germany/find-an-internship/
