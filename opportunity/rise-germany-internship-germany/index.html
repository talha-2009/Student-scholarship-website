Internship - Germany

RISE Germany Internship

## Introduction

RISE Germany is a DAAD-run summer research internship connecting undergraduate students from the US, Canada, UK, and Ireland with German university and research institute labs in biology, chemistry, computer science, physics, earth sciences, and engineering. Unlike many research internships that require you to already have a contact in place, RISE runs its own matching database, letting you browse specific projects and apply directly with a motivation letter for each one — closer to a targeted job search than a single general scholarship application.

## Opportunity Overview

- **Organization:** DAAD (German Academic Exchange Service) - **Country of Study:** Germany - **Opportunity Type:** Internship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Undergraduate (after completing at least 2 years of study) - **Duration:** Typically a summer research placement (around 2-3 months) - **Official Website:** daad.de/rise

## Benefits

- A monthly stipend of €992 (pro-rated to the length of your internship) - Health, accident, and liability insurance - An international travel subsidy - A €160 travel stipend to attend the RISE meeting in Heidelberg, where interns from across Germany connect - Housing assistance arranged by your host institute

## Eligibility Criteria

- Be an undergraduate student who has completed at least two years of study - Be a citizen or student at a university in the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, or Ireland - Be studying biology, chemistry, computer science, physics, earth sciences, or engineering - Be available for a summer research placement matching your chosen project's timeline

## Required Documents

- Online application form - Official academic transcript - Certificate of enrollment showing your expected graduation date - A motivation letter tailored to each specific project you apply to - A signed reference letter

## Application Process

**Step 1.** Register for a DAAD ID through the RISE Germany portal.

**Step 2.** Browse the RISE Germany internship database and identify specific host projects matching your field of study.

**Step 3.** Write a tailored motivation letter for each project you apply to — RISE explicitly expects project-specific letters, not one generic version.

**Step 4.** Submit your transcript, certificate of enrollment, and reference letter alongside your applications.

**Step 5.** If a host professor selects you, you will receive a formal internship offer with dates and stipend details.

**Step 6.** Attend the RISE meeting in Heidelberg during your internship to connect with other RISE participants across Germany.

## Important Dates

- **Application deadline:** See the deadline listed for this opportunity (typically 30 November for the following summer) - **Host selection:** Decisions are communicated by host professors over the following months - **Internship period:** Summer, generally June through August

Always confirm exact dates for the current cycle, since host institutes set specific windows within the summer period.

## Selection Criteria

Individual host professors, not a central DAAD committee, select their own interns, so success depends heavily on how well your specific motivation letter and academic background match that professor's particular research project.

## Tips for Applicants

- Apply to multiple projects, since selection is made by individual host professors and success with one specific project isn't guaranteed - Genuinely tailor each motivation letter to the specific project's research focus — generic letters are a common reason applicants are overlooked - Apply as early as the portal allows, since popular projects and professors can receive many applications - Make sure your transcript clearly documents relevant coursework in your chosen field - Attend the RISE meeting in Heidelberg if selected, since it's a genuine opportunity to build a peer network across the programme

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Who can apply for RISE Germany?** Undergraduates from the US, Canada, UK, or Ireland who have completed at least two years of study in an eligible STEM field.

**Is RISE Germany fully funded?** Yes — it includes a monthly stipend, insurance, a travel subsidy, and housing assistance.

**How do I get matched with a host lab?** You apply directly to specific listed projects in the RISE database, and individual professors select their own interns.

**How long does the internship last?** Typically a summer placement of around two to three months.

**Do I need to speak German?** No, RISE projects are generally conducted in English within international research groups.

**Can graduate students apply?** No, RISE Germany is specifically for undergraduate students.

## Apply Now

Browse current host projects and apply through the official RISE Germany database: https://www.daad.de/rise/en/rise-germany/find-an-internship/
