Scholarship - Australia

Australia Awards Scholarships

Introduction

Every year, the Australian Government funds thousands of students from developing countries across the Indo-Pacific, Africa, and the Middle East to study at Australian universities, all in service of a specific policy goal: building skilled, connected leaders who return home to contribute to their own country's development. Administered by the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade, Australia Awards Scholarships are highly prestigious and genuinely competitive, but the process runs differently by country, since each participating nation has its own eligibility nuances, priority fields, and application timeline layered on top of the general programme rules.

About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue full-time undergraduate or postgraduate study at a participating Australian university or TAFE institution, in a field aligned with their home country's specific development priorities as defined in that country's official profile. A defining feature of the programme is its post-study expectation: scholars must leave Australia for a minimum of two years after finishing their studies, reflecting the programme's core purpose of building capacity in partner countries rather than facilitating migration to Australia.

Benefits

Scholars receive full tuition fees, one return economy-class airfare to and from Australia, a one-time establishment allowance to cover books and initial accommodation costs, a fortnightly contribution to living expenses, and Overseas Student Health Cover; depending on eligibility, pre-course English training, supplementary academic support, and fieldwork travel may also be covered.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need to be citizens of a participating country, residing in and applying from that country, at least 18 years old on 1 February of the year the scholarship begins, and must not hold Australian or New Zealand citizenship or permanent residency, nor be applying for a scholarship at an academic level lower than one already achieved.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields are tied to each participating country's specific development priorities, commonly including public policy, health, education, agriculture, engineering, and governance, though exact priority areas vary by country and are listed in each country profile.

Required Documents

Applicants need certified copies of their degree certificate, academic transcripts, and identification documents, two recommendation letters (one academic, one general), English proficiency test scores, and, for PhD or research master's applicants, evidence that a prospective supervisor has given preliminary approval to the proposed research.

Application Process

Applicants register and apply through the Online Australia Scholarships Information System, known as OASIS, except in Indonesia and the Philippines, which use separate country-specific portals; before applying, candidates should read the Australia Awards Scholarships Policy Handbook and their specific country profile, since eligibility and required documents differ by country.

Deadline

The current application round opened 1 February 2026 and closes 30 April 2026 at 14:00 AEST; applications are not assessed on a rolling basis, so the closing time is firm.

Why You Should Apply

For students from eligible developing countries who want a fully funded Australian education tied to a genuine commitment to their home country's development, Australia Awards offers comprehensive funding and a global alumni network built over decades, provided applicants carefully follow their specific country's eligibility and application requirements.

For full country-specific eligibility and application details, visit the official Australia Awards Scholarships page: https://www.dfat.gov.au/people-to-people/australia-awards/australia-awards-scholarships
