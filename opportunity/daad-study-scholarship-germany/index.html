Scholarship - Germany

DAAD Study Scholarship

Introduction

Among DAAD's many scholarship strands, the Study Scholarship is the most broadly accessible, open to graduates of any academic discipline rather than a specific field or professional background. For graduates who want a fully funded German master's degree but don't fit neatly into DAAD's more specialized programmes like EPOS or the STEM scholarship, this is often the most straightforward option to consider.

About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue a full master's degree at a German university in any field, with funding beginning the following academic year after a successful application. Because the scholarship is open to any discipline, DAAD's review focuses heavily on the strength of an applicant's academic record and the clarity of their reasons for choosing both their specific field and Germany as a study destination, rather than fit with a narrower professional or thematic criteria.

Benefits

Scholars receive a monthly payment of EUR 992, health, accident, and liability insurance, a travel allowance, and an annual study allowance of EUR 460. Depending on individual circumstances, a rent subsidy and family allowance may also be available, providing additional support for scholars with dependents or facing higher housing costs.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need an above-average first degree in any discipline, and generally no more than six years should have passed since that degree was completed. Applicants must be applying for a new, full-time master's programme at a German university, with funding intended to begin the following academic year, meaning the scholarship is not designed for students already partway through a German master's degree.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Unlike most other DAAD scholarships, the Study Scholarship places no restriction on academic field, making it accessible to graduates from the humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, and professional fields alike.

Required Documents

Applicants need the DAAD application form, a CV, a motivation letter, academic transcripts and degree certificates, a language proficiency certificate matching their chosen programme's language of instruction, and recommendation letters.

Application Process

Applicants identify an eligible master's programme in Germany matching their academic interests, then apply directly online via the DAAD Portal. As with most DAAD scholarships, this is a pre-admission process, meaning applicants can apply for their chosen university programme and DAAD funding simultaneously rather than needing to secure admission first.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 15 October 2026. Because this scholarship is open to graduates from any field, competition can be broad, so applicants should submit a complete, well-prepared application well ahead of the deadline rather than close to it.

Why You Should Apply

For graduates of any discipline who want to study in Germany but don't fit a narrower DAAD scholarship category, the Study Scholarship offers a genuinely open, fully funded path to a German master's degree. Among broad, discipline-agnostic scholarships for international students, it stands out for the comprehensive support DAAD provides, from the monthly stipend through possible family and rent allowances, making it worth serious consideration for any strong graduate weighing study abroad options in Germany.

For full application details, visit the official DAAD scholarship database listing: https://www2.daad.de/deutschland/stipendium/datenbank/en/21148-scholarship-database/?detail=50026200
