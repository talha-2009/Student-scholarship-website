World Economic Forum Global Shapers Community

## Introduction The World Economic Forum Global Shapers Community is a worldwide network of young leaders organized into locally run "hubs," each pursuing community and policy-level impact projects under the WEF umbrella. Rather than a single centralized application, Global Shapers works through these individual hubs, each of which runs its own recruitment and selection process when it opens membership — meaning your actual path into the community depends on finding and connecting with a hub near you. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** World Economic Forum - **Country of Study:** Global — organized through local hubs worldwide - **Opportunity Type:** Fellowship - **Funding Type:** Unpaid - **Degree Level:** Open to all (ages 20-30) - **Duration:** Ongoing hub membership - **Official Website:** globalshapers.org ## Benefits - Access to the World Economic Forum network and events - Hub-based mentorship - A platform to lead local impact initiatives ## Eligibility Criteria - Be aged 20-30 - Demonstrate leadership and community impact - Be nominated or selected via a specific local hub's application process ## Required Documents - Application form - CV - References from hub curators ## Application Process **Step 1.** Identify whether a Global Shapers Hub already exists in your city, and check if it is currently recruiting. **Step 2.** When a hub announces an open call, submit your application form, CV, and any required references. **Step 3.** The hub's curators (its elected leadership) review applications for leadership and community impact. **Step 4.** Successful applicants join the local hub and gain access to the wider Global Shapers and WEF network. **Step 5.** Participate in hub-led community and policy impact projects on an ongoing basis. ## Important Dates Each Global Shapers Hub sets its own recruitment timeline, and there is no single global application window. Please refer to the official website and your local hub's specific page for current recruitment status. ## Selection Criteria Hub curators assess demonstrated leadership and tangible community impact, alongside references that speak to your character and prior initiative — since Global Shapers is built around active, self-organized local projects rather than passive membership. ## Tips for Applicants - Find and follow your local Global Shapers Hub's social media or website, since recruitment calls are often announced there first - Prepare a CV and references that clearly demonstrate concrete leadership and community impact, not general potential - Reach out to current hub members if possible to understand the hub's specific focus and current projects - Be ready to actively participate in ongoing local projects, since membership is not passive - If no hub exists in your city, research whether the WEF process allows for establishing a new one ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can join the Global Shapers Community?** Young people aged 20-30 demonstrating leadership and community impact. **Is it fully funded?** No, it is unpaid; membership does not include a stipend or funding. **How do I apply?** Through an open call from an existing local Global Shapers Hub when recruitment is announced. **Is there a global application?** No, recruitment is managed at the hub level, not centrally by the WEF. **What do Global Shapers actually do?** Lead local community and policy impact projects, supported by hub-based mentorship and the wider WEF network. **What if there is no hub in my city?** Research whether you can explore establishing one through the WEF Global Shapers process. ## Apply Now Find your local Global Shapers Hub and review current recruitment status on the official Global Shapers website: https://www.globalshapers.org/
