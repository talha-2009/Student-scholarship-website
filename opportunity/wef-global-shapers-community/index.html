World Economic Forum Global Shapers Community

Introduction

The World Economic Forum Global Shapers Community is a worldwide network of young leaders organized into locally run hubs, each pursuing community and policy-level impact projects under the WEF umbrella. Rather than a single centralized application, Global Shapers works through individual hubs, each running its own recruitment when it opens membership.

About the Opportunity

Young people join a local Global Shapers Hub in their city, participating in ongoing community and policy impact projects while gaining access to the wider WEF network, with hub curators managing recruitment and member selection locally rather than through a central WEF process.

Benefits

Members gain access to the WEF network and events, hub-based mentorship, and a platform to lead local impact initiatives.

Who Can Apply?

Young people aged 20-30 demonstrating leadership and community impact can apply, nominated or selected via local hub applications when recruitment opens.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The community spans leadership and policy broadly, open to young people across any sector who can demonstrate genuine community impact.

Required Documents

Applicants need an application form, a CV, and references from hub curators.

Application Process

Applicants apply through an open call from an existing local Global Shapers Hub when recruitment is announced.

Deadline

No global deadline exists; each hub announces recruitment periodically, so interested applicants should follow their local hub directly.

Why You Should Apply

For young leaders wanting access to a genuinely global network and platform for local impact work, Global Shapers offers a unique blend of grassroots community action and international WEF connections.

For full details, visit the official Global Shapers website: https://www.globalshapers.org/
