Joint Japan World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program

## Introduction The Joint Japan World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program funds master's study specifically in development-related fields for professionals from World Bank member developing countries, jointly sponsored by the World Bank and the Government of Japan in a partnership that reflects both institutions' shared interest in building development expertise across the countries the World Bank serves. What sets this scholarship apart structurally is its sequencing: applicants must first independently secure admission to an eligible partner university programme before they can even apply for the scholarship funding itself, making university admission the genuine first hurdle rather than a parallel process running alongside the scholarship application. ## About the Opportunity Scholars pursue a master's degree in a development-related field at one of the programme's partner universities, bringing professional experience from their home country into their graduate study rather than approaching development topics as an entirely new subject. Because admission comes first and funding second, the programme effectively screens candidates twice, once through the partner university's own admissions standards, which vary by institution, and again through the scholarship's own review of professional relevance and development impact, meaning a genuinely strong application needs to satisfy two somewhat different sets of criteria rather than one unified standard. ## Benefits Scholars receive tuition, travel costs, a living allowance, and health insurance for the duration of their master's programme, a benefits package intended to remove financial barriers for professionals who might otherwise struggle to combine full-time graduate study with limited personal savings. ## Who Can Apply? Applicants need to be citizens of a World Bank member developing country with at least three years of relevant work experience, and, critically, must already be admitted to an eligible master's programme at a partner university before applying for scholarship funding, a sequencing requirement that surprises some applicants who assume they can apply for both simultaneously. ## Fields of Study or Available Areas The scholarship funds development-related fields specifically, supporting professionals looking to deepen expertise directly connected to development challenges in their home countries, spanning economics, public policy, environmental management, and related disciplines. ## Required Documents Applicants need their university admission letter, a CV, recommendation letters, and a statement of purpose connecting their professional background to their proposed studies and future development-related career plans. ## Application Process Applicants first secure admission to an eligible master's programme at a partner university independently, then apply for the scholarship separately once that admission is confirmed, meaning the two processes are genuinely sequential rather than parallel, and applicants should not expect to submit both applications at the same time. ## Deadline Because the scholarship deadline depends partly on the applicant's partner university's own admissions timeline, there is no single fixed global date; applicants should confirm the current cycle's date directly on the World Bank scholarships page well before they plan to apply for admission. ## Why You Should Apply For development professionals who have already secured, or are confident of securing, admission to a partner university programme, this scholarship offers comprehensive funding specifically tied to development-focused study, making it worth pursuing once the admission hurdle is cleared, and worth planning for well in advance given its sequential application structure. For full details, visit the official World Bank scholarships page: https://www.worldbank.org/en/programs/scholarships
