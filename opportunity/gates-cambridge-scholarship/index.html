Gates Cambridge Scholarship

## Introduction Established in 2000 with a major founding gift from the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, the Gates Cambridge Scholarship funds outstanding non-UK students pursuing any full-time postgraduate course at the University of Cambridge, from one-year taught master's programmes through multi-year PhDs. Because every scholar joins the same single university rather than choosing among many institutions, Gates Cambridge scholars form a tight-knit, genuinely international community, with dedicated leadership programming layered on top of their academic work that few university-specific scholarships attempt to replicate at this scale. ## About the Opportunity Scholars apply for admission to their intended Cambridge course through the university's standard graduate admissions system, and Gates Cambridge consideration happens within that same process rather than through a separate scholarship-only application submitted independently. Beyond funding, the Gates Cambridge Trust runs leadership development programming specifically for scholars, including workshops, speaker events, and structured opportunities to connect across the wide range of disciplines and roughly 100 countries represented across the scholar community at any given time, building a genuinely cross-disciplinary network that persists well beyond graduation through an active alumni association. ## Benefits Scholars receive full tuition and college fees, a maintenance allowance covering living costs for the entire duration of their course, and airfare to and from Cambridge. Additional discretionary funding is available for research-related costs such as fieldwork, conference travel, or other academic expenses that extend beyond the basic cost of study itself, giving scholars real flexibility to pursue their research ambitions without constant budget constraints. ## Who Can Apply? Applicants need to be citizens of any country other than the United Kingdom, applying for or already admitted to a full-time postgraduate course at Cambridge, with an outstanding academic record and demonstrated leadership potential. The Trust weighs a genuine, demonstrated commitment to improving the lives of others heavily in its selection criteria, alongside pure academic strength, reflecting the Gates Foundation's founding interest in cultivating leaders oriented toward positive impact rather than academic achievement in isolation. ## Fields of Study or Available Areas Any full-time postgraduate course offered at the University of Cambridge is eligible, giving scholars genuine flexibility across disciplines from the sciences and engineering through the humanities, social sciences, and professional fields like law and public policy. ## Required Documents Applicants need their Cambridge graduate application, a personal statement addressing motivation and future plans, academic references, and, where relevant, a financial need statement supporting their case for funding. ## Application Process Applicants apply for admission through the standard University of Cambridge graduate application system and select the Gates Cambridge option within that same process, meaning strong performance in Cambridge's own admissions review is essential to scholarship consideration and cannot be separated from it. ## Deadline The current application deadline is 3 December 2026. Because Gates Cambridge consideration happens within the standard Cambridge admissions timeline, applicants should confirm their specific course's deadline alongside this general date, since some Cambridge courses set earlier internal deadlines. ## Why You Should Apply For outstanding non-UK students who want to study at Cambridge without the financial burden that would otherwise accompany it, Gates Cambridge offers comprehensive funding alongside a genuinely close-knit international scholar community and leadership development programming that few university-specific scholarships replicate at this scale or with this level of ongoing alumni engagement. For full application details, visit the official Gates Cambridge Scholarship website: https://www.gatescambridge.org/apply/
