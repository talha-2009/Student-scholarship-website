Scholarship - Germany

DAAD Helmut-Schmidt-Programme (Public Policy and Good Governance)

Introduction

Named after the former German chancellor whose career spanned economics, defense, and international diplomacy, the DAAD Helmut-Schmidt-Programme funds a master's degree specifically for graduates and young professionals working in public policy, economics, law, or governance-related fields. It reflects a specific bet DAAD has made: that supporting the next generation of governance and policy leaders with firsthand experience of Germany creates lasting value both for those individuals and for Germany's own international relationships.

About the Opportunity

Like DAAD's EPOS scholarship, Helmut-Schmidt funding is tied to a defined list of partner master's courses rather than open study at any German university, though here the unifying theme across eligible programmes is public policy, governance, and related fields rather than development more broadly. Scholars pursue their master's at one of these partner institutions, gaining not just an academic credential but direct exposure to German approaches to public policy and governance during their time in the programme.

Benefits

Depending on their specific level and programme, scholars receive a monthly stipend ranging from EUR 992 to EUR 1,400. All scholars receive health, accident, and liability insurance, along with a travel allowance and a study allowance to support books, materials, and other academic costs throughout their programme.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants should be graduates or young professionals working in public policy, economics, law, or a governance-related field, typically holding a first degree and some relevant professional experience, though the exact requirements can vary depending on which specific partner course an applicant targets. Because eligibility details differ by programme, applicants should review their chosen course's specific admission criteria alongside DAAD's general scholarship requirements.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible programmes cluster around public policy, governance, law, economics, and development studies, reflecting the scholarship's focus on building expertise relevant to public-sector and governance careers.

Required Documents

Applicants need the DAAD application form, a CV, a motivation letter, academic transcripts or degree certificates, a language proficiency certificate, and recommendation letters supporting their candidacy.

Application Process

Applicants search the DAAD scholarship database for the specific Helmut-Schmidt-Programme-listed master's course that matches their governance-related background and goals. Once nominated by the university offering that course, applicants complete their scholarship application through the DAAD Portal, meaning the university admissions process and the DAAD funding process are closely linked rather than fully separate.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 31 July 2026. Because this scholarship works through a defined list of partner courses, applicants should confirm both the general DAAD deadline and any specific deadline set by their chosen programme well in advance.

Why You Should Apply

For graduates and professionals building a career in public policy, governance, or law who want direct exposure to German approaches in these fields, the Helmut-Schmidt-Programme offers a targeted, fully funded route into that experience through a set of partner institutions chosen specifically for their relevance to governance and public administration. Among scholarships for public policy and governance study, it stands out for its historical namesake and DAAD's explicit focus on developing future leaders in this specific space.

For the current list of partner courses and application details, visit the official DAAD Helmut-Schmidt-Programme page: https://www.daad.de/en/studying-in-germany/scholarships/daad-funding-programmes/helmut-schmidt-programme/
