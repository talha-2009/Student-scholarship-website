Leiden University Excellence Scholarship

Introduction

Leiden University's Excellence Scholarship, known as LExS, is one of the more genuinely competitive partial scholarships in the Netherlands, awarding roughly 25 scholarships each year against more than 1,000 applicants, a ratio that puts it among the more selective partial-funding awards available to international master's students in Europe. Rather than promising to cover a student's full cost of study, LExS is explicitly designed as a meaningful contribution across five different award levels, meaning successful applicants should plan for it to reduce rather than eliminate their overall study costs, a distinction worth understanding clearly before building a full financial plan around the scholarship.

About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue a master's degree at Leiden University, one of the Netherlands' oldest and most internationally respected research universities, applying for admission and LExS consideration through the same application portal rather than two entirely separate systems. The scholarship application itself is centered around a notably concise motivation letter, capped at 500 words, a length restriction that forces genuine clarity and specificity rather than allowing applicants to rely on volume to make their case. Because competition is intense relative to the small number of awards, Leiden's selection process weighs both academic excellence and the clarity of that short motivation letter heavily, treating concision itself as a signal of a candidate's ability to communicate effectively, a skill relevant to graduate study regardless of discipline.

Benefits

Scholars receive a partial scholarship at one of five award levels, though the award does not cover full study costs and cannot be combined with another full external scholarship, a restriction applicants should factor in if they are also pursuing other funding sources simultaneously.

Who Can Apply?

Non-EU/EEA students admitted to a Leiden University master's programme can apply, with two notable exceptions, the Master of Law Advanced Programme and MSc International Relations, both open to all nationalities regardless of EU status. Applicants need excellent academic standing given how competitive the scholarship genuinely is relative to the small number of awards granted each cycle.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

LExS covers master's programmes across Leiden University's full academic range, with eligibility tied to admission to an eligible programme rather than a specific field restriction, spanning the sciences, humanities, social sciences, and law.

Required Documents

Applicants need their master's programme application, a LExS motivation letter submitted as a PDF and capped strictly at 500 words, academic transcripts, and proof of nationality.

Application Process

Applicants apply for admission to their chosen Leiden master's programme, then complete the separate LExS scholarship application and motivation letter within the same application portal, ensuring both components are submitted before the LExS-specific deadline rather than only meeting the general admissions deadline.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 1 October 2026. Given the strict 500-word limit on the motivation letter, applicants should draft and revise this component carefully well ahead of the deadline, since a rushed 500-word statement is unlikely to stand out against a highly competitive applicant pool.

Why You Should Apply

For academically excellent non-EU students who want to study at Leiden, LExS offers a meaningful reduction in study costs, though applicants should approach it with realistic expectations about its partial nature and genuinely high competitiveness, treating any award received as a valuable contribution rather than assuming it will cover the full cost of a Dutch master's degree.

For full details, visit the official Leiden University LExS page: https://www.universiteitleiden.nl/en/education/scholarships/leiden-university-excellence-scholarships-lexs
