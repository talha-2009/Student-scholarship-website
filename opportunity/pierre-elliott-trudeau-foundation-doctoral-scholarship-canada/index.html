Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation Doctoral Scholarship

Introduction

Named after Canada's fifteenth prime minister, the Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation Doctoral Scholarship funds PhD students in the humanities and social sciences who want their research to connect meaningfully to public life, not remain confined to academic journals alone. Unlike scholarships that fund research purely for its own academic merit, the Trudeau Foundation explicitly looks for candidates demonstrating leadership and a genuine commitment to public engagement alongside strong scholarly work.

About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue doctoral research at a Canadian or foreign university in the humanities or social sciences, while also participating in the Foundation's mentorship network and events designed to connect scholars with public life beyond the academy, including policymakers, civil society leaders, and fellow scholars working across disciplines. This dual emphasis, on both rigorous doctoral research and genuine public engagement, shapes how the Foundation evaluates candidates from the very start of the application process.

Benefits

Scholars receive up to CAD 60,000 per year for up to three years, along with access to a mentorship network and Foundation events connecting them with leaders across public and academic life.

Who Can Apply?

Doctoral students in the humanities and social sciences enrolled at a Canadian or foreign university can apply, provided they can demonstrate genuine leadership and a commitment to public engagement alongside their scholarly work.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The scholarship covers PhD research across the humanities and social sciences broadly, open to any specific discipline within those fields where a candidate can articulate genuine public relevance.

Required Documents

Applicants need an application form, a research proposal, a CV, transcripts, and recommendation letters.

Application Process

Applicants apply online through the Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation's scholarship portal with required essays and references by the published deadline, with strong applications typically articulating a clear connection between the proposed doctoral research and broader public engagement.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 17 November 2026. Given the Foundation's emphasis on public engagement alongside academic merit, applicants should allow real time to develop essays that speak convincingly to both dimensions.

Why You Should Apply

For doctoral candidates in the humanities and social sciences who see their research as connected to real public and policy questions, this scholarship offers substantial funding alongside a genuine network of mentors and public figures that purely academic funding sources typically cannot provide.

For full application details, visit the official Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation scholarship page: https://www.trudeaufoundation.ca/scholarship/
