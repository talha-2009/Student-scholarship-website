Exchange Program - Canada

Killam Fellowships Program

Introduction

Jointly delivered by Fulbright Canada and the Killam Trusts, the Killam Fellowships Program funds a full academic year of undergraduate exchange between the United States and Canada, one of the few major exchange programmes running specifically in that direction. Most well-known US-Canada academic ties run through informal partnerships or study-abroad offices with limited funding attached; Killam is distinctive precisely because it comes with a fully funded package administered through the same trusted Fulbright network that runs graduate-level exchanges. Because it operates entirely through university nomination rather than open individual applications, a student's home institution's Fulbright Canada/Killam liaison office is the actual gateway into the programme, not a general online portal that a student could discover and apply to independently.

About the Opportunity

Nominated students spend a full academic year studying at a host institution across the border, US students in Canada or Canadian students in the US depending on their home country, immersing themselves in a different national academic culture while continuing their undergraduate studies without losing time toward their degree. Beyond coursework, the programme builds in enrichment activities specifically designed to deepen the cross-border cultural and academic experience, recognizing that simply attending classes at a foreign institution does not automatically produce meaningful cultural exchange without deliberate programming around it. Fellows join a small, tightly-knit cohort each year, since the number of Killam Fellowships awarded is limited, giving participants a genuine community of peers navigating the same transition simultaneously.

Benefits

Fellows receive a tuition waiver, a monthly stipend, travel funding to and from their host country, and access to enrichment activities throughout their exchange year. Together, these benefits are designed to remove essentially every major financial barrier to a full year of study abroad, letting fellows focus fully on their coursework and cultural immersion rather than budgeting around travel or living costs.

Who Can Apply?

Full-time undergraduate students who are citizens of the US or Canada can apply, provided they are nominated by their home institution, since Killam does not accept applications submitted directly by students without that nomination. Because nomination is the actual bottleneck in this process, students should find out early whether their institution has an active Fulbright Canada/Killam liaison office and how that office selects its nominees each year, since internal nomination criteria and competitiveness can vary considerably between universities.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The fellowship is open across all academic fields, giving nominated students flexibility to continue their existing course of undergraduate study at their host institution without needing to switch majors or academic focus to qualify.

Required Documents

Applicants need their university nomination, transcripts, essays, and recommendation letters, with the essays typically expected to explain both academic goals and genuine interest in the specific cross-border cultural experience the programme offers.

Application Process

Students apply through their home university's Fulbright Canada/Killam liaison office, which handles the nomination process; students interested in the fellowship should contact that office directly rather than searching for a general online application, since no such direct-application route exists for this particular exchange.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 6 October 2026. Because nomination requires coordination with a home institution's liaison office, students should reach out well ahead of this date, ideally at the very start of the academic year in which they hope to apply, since some universities run their own internal nomination timeline well before the national deadline.

Why You Should Apply

For US and Canadian undergraduates who want a genuine cross-border academic year without pausing their degree progress, Killam offers comprehensive funding through a well-established bilateral exchange, provided their home institution actively participates in the nomination process. Among exchange programs specifically connecting the US and Canada at the undergraduate level, few carry Killam's combination of full funding and the institutional credibility of the Fulbright name.

For full details, visit the official Fulbright Canada Killam Fellowships page: https://www.fulbright.ca/programs/killam-fellowships
