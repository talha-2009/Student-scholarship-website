Research Grant - Germany

DAAD Bi-nationally Supervised Doctoral Degrees

Introduction

Some of the strongest doctoral research happens at the intersection of two academic systems, and DAAD's Bi-nationally Supervised Doctoral Degrees programme, often known by the French term cotutelle, funds exactly that kind of collaboration. Rather than requiring a doctoral candidate to choose between their home institution and a German one, this DAAD grant supports PhD research jointly supervised in both countries, resulting in a degree recognized by both institutions at once.

About the Opportunity

A cotutelle arrangement requires genuine coordination between two universities, one in Germany and one in the candidate's home country, each contributing a qualified supervisor to the doctoral project. DAAD's grant funds the German portion of that arrangement, supporting the candidate's research stays in Germany while the dual-supervision structure ensures the resulting degree carries recognition and academic weight in both academic systems, not just one.

Benefits

DAAD provides a monthly stipend to cover living costs during research stays in Germany, along with a travel allowance to support movement between the two countries involved in the cotutelle arrangement. Health insurance is included, and a research or study allowance helps cover additional costs directly connected to the doctoral project itself.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need an above-average master's degree and, most importantly, a confirmed qualified supervisor at a German institution alongside a confirmed qualified supervisor at their home-country institution, since the entire structure depends on this dual-supervision arrangement being genuinely in place before applying, not simply proposed.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The grant is open across disciplines, supporting multidisciplinary doctoral research wherever a genuine cotutelle arrangement between a German and a home-country institution can be established.

Required Documents

Applicants need a detailed research proposal, confirmation letters from both supervisors, one in Germany and one at the home institution, academic transcripts, and a CV.

Application Process

The practical starting point for this grant is securing both supervisors' agreement to the cotutelle arrangement, since their confirmation letters are required documents, not optional supporting material. Once both supervisory relationships are confirmed, applicants develop a research proposal reflecting the genuinely joint nature of the project and submit their complete application, including both confirmation letters, transcripts, and CV, through the DAAD Portal.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 15 November 2026. Because arranging confirmed supervision at two separate institutions in two different countries takes real coordination and time, applicants should begin those conversations with prospective supervisors well before this date rather than trying to secure both confirmations in the final weeks.

Why You Should Apply

For doctoral candidates whose research genuinely benefits from expertise and resources spanning two academic systems, this DAAD grant offers a funded, structured way to formalize that collaboration rather than pursuing an informal international research stay. A cotutelle degree carries recognition in both countries involved, a meaningful advantage for candidates planning an academic career that might unfold across borders, and the dual-supervision structure often produces richer, more internationally connected doctoral research than a single-country PhD.

For full application details, visit the official DAAD scholarship database listing: https://www2.daad.de/deutschland/stipendium/datenbank/en/21148-scholarship-database/?detail=57507783
