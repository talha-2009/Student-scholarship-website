World Food Programme Internship Programme

The World Food Programme Internship Programme is a verified humanitarian internship pathway for students and recent graduates who want practical exposure to food assistance, emergency response and sustainable development. WFP is the United Nations food assistance agency, and its internships are designed to complement academic study with professional assignments that support programmes, operations and technical teams. Interns may work in country offices, regional bureaux or headquarters depending on available vacancies and organisational needs.

The programme is suitable for applicants interested in humanitarian action, nutrition, food security, logistics, data, programme management, communications, partnerships, finance or related professional areas. Interns may conduct research and data analysis, draft documents and presentations, support project teams, contribute technical knowledge and collaborate across multicultural teams. The experience is intended to help young professionals understand how humanitarian organisations operate while giving WFP fresh perspectives and practical support.

WFP publishes clear eligibility rules. Applicants must be current undergraduate or graduate students who have attended classes in the past year, or recent graduates within six months who also attended classes in the past year. They must have completed at least two years of undergraduate study. English fluency is required, while knowledge of other UN languages can be an asset. Candidates should be comfortable with MS Office, collaborative work and cross-cultural communication, and they cannot have immediate relatives working for WFP.

The programme includes meaningful benefits. WFP states that interns may receive a stipend contribution linked to the city and country of assignment, not exceeding US$1,000 per month, along with medical and accident insurance during the internship. Depending on eligibility and assignment context, interns may also receive travel-related support, official holidays, certified sick leave, visa support and access to training resources. Because postings vary, applicants should read the active vacancy carefully and prepare a tailored application that connects their academic background to WFP's mission of ending hunger.
