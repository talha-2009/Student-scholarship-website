U.S. Commercial Service Germany Internship

Introduction

The U.S. Commercial Service Germany Internship places university students inside the trade-promotion arm of the U.S. Department of Commerce, working out of the U.S. Embassy in Berlin or one of the Consulates General in Düsseldorf, Frankfurt, or Munich, offering a distinctly diplomatic-adjacent experience rather than a typical corporate or academic placement. A genuine U.S. government security check, including an interview, is required before an intern can start, reflecting the internship's position within an official government office.

About the Opportunity

Interns support trade-promotion work connecting American and German businesses, gaining direct exposure to how U.S. commercial diplomacy functions in practice at one of Germany's key embassy or consulate locations. Because placements are unpaid and security-vetted, interns tend to be genuinely motivated by the experience and access itself rather than compensation, and many use the placement as a meaningful credential for future government, trade, or international business careers.

Benefits

Interns gain hands-on trade-promotion experience, an official letter of recommendation, and academic credit of three to six credits at most universities; the internship is unpaid volunteer service with no federal compensation.

Who Can Apply?

Students enrolled at a university or institute of higher education beyond their second, sophomore, year can apply, provided they can pass a U.S. government background check; selection is made without regard to race, color, national origin, religion, sex, marital status, or disability.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Relevant fields include international trade, business, and economics, though the placement suits any student genuinely interested in commercial diplomacy.

Required Documents

Applicants need a cover letter or application, proof of student status, a letter of reference or reference contacts, and background-check information.

Application Process

Applicants email an application directly to the U.S. Commercial Service office at their desired location at least four months before their intended start date, since the required security check can itself take up to four months, including an interview.

Deadline

There is no fixed deadline; applicants should apply at least four months before their intended start date given the security check timeline.

Why You Should Apply

For students interested in trade, diplomacy, or international business, this internship offers genuine access to a U.S. Embassy or Consulate environment and a credential valuable for future government or international business careers, provided applicants plan around the lengthy security clearance process.

For full details, visit the official U.S. Commercial Service Germany page: https://www.trade.gov/germany-internships
