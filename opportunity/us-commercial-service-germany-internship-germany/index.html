Internship - Germany

U.S. Commercial Service Germany Internship

## Introduction

The U.S. Commercial Service Germany Internship places students inside the trade-promotion arm of the U.S. Department of Commerce, working out of the U.S. Embassy in Berlin or one of the Consulates General in Düsseldorf, Frankfurt, or Munich. It offers a distinctive government diplomatic-adjacent experience rather than academic research, and its process reflects that: applications go directly by email to a specific office, and a genuine U.S. government security check — including an interview — is required before you can start.

## Opportunity Overview

- **Organization:** U.S. Commercial Service Germany (U.S. Department of Commerce / U.S. Embassy Berlin) - **Country of Study:** Germany - **Opportunity Type:** Internship - **Funding Type:** Unpaid - **Degree Level:** Bachelor's or Master's - **Duration:** Varies by placement, set individually with the hosting office - **Official Website:** trade.gov

## Benefits

- Hands-on trade-promotion experience at the U.S. Embassy Berlin or a Consulate General - An official letter of recommendation - Academic credit (3-6 credits) at most universities - Unpaid volunteer service — no federal compensation is provided

## Eligibility Criteria

- Be enrolled at a university or institute of higher education beyond your second (sophomore) year - Be able to pass a U.S. government background check - Selection is made without regard to race, color, national origin, religion, sex, marital status, or disability

## Required Documents

- A cover letter/application - Proof of student status - A letter of reference or reference contacts - Background-check information

## Application Process

**Step 1.** Decide which office you want to apply to: the U.S. Embassy in Berlin, or a Consulate General in Düsseldorf, Frankfurt, or Munich.

**Step 2.** Email your application directly to that specific office at least four months before your intended start date.

**Step 3.** Provide your cover letter, proof of student status, and reference contacts as requested.

**Step 4.** Undergo the required U.S. government security check, which can take up to four months and includes an interview.

**Step 5.** Once cleared, you receive confirmation of your placement location and start date.

## Important Dates

There is no single fixed annual deadline; applications should be submitted by email at least four months before your intended start date, since the security check itself can take up to four months.

## Selection Criteria

Selection is based on meeting the academic year requirement (beyond sophomore year), successfully passing the U.S. government background check, and general fit with the specific office's trade-promotion work, applied without regard to protected characteristics.

## Tips for Applicants

- Apply at least four months ahead of your intended start date — the security check timeline alone can take that long - Choose the specific office (Berlin, Düsseldorf, Frankfurt, or Munich) that matches your location preference, since applications go directly to that office by email - Confirm with your university in advance whether this internship qualifies for academic credit, since that arrangement is handled on your end - Prepare thoroughly for the required security interview, since this is a genuine government clearance process - Remember this is unpaid; plan your finances for the internship period accordingly

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Who can apply for the U.S. Commercial Service Germany Internship?** Students beyond their second year of university who can pass a U.S. government background check.

**Is the internship paid?** No, it is unpaid volunteer service with no federal compensation.

**Where are placements based?** The U.S. Embassy in Berlin, or Consulates General in Düsseldorf, Frankfurt, or Munich.

**How do I apply?** By emailing an application directly to your desired office at least four months before your intended start date.

**How long does the security check take?** Up to four months, including an interview.

**Can I get academic credit?** Many universities offer 3-6 credits for this internship; confirm with your own institution.

## Apply Now

Email your application to your chosen office and review full details on the official U.S. Commercial Service Germany page: https://www.trade.gov/germany-internships
