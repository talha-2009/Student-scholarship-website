Fellowship - Austria

Franz Werfel Fellowship

## Introduction

Named after the Austrian-Bohemian writer Franz Werfel, this fellowship from OeAD, Austria's national agency for education and internationalisation, supports young Germanists, scholars and teachers of German language and Austrian literature, to spend extended time as visiting researchers inside Austria itself. For early-career academics whose work centers specifically on German-language and Austrian literary studies, it offers something a general research grant cannot: direct access to the archives, libraries, and university departments most relevant to that particular field.

## About the Opportunity

Fellows are placed as visiting researchers at Austrian university departments, libraries, archives, or research institutions for a period of four to eighteen months, depending on their specific project and needs. Beyond the fellowship period itself, OeAD provides material follow-up support afterward, reflecting an interest in sustaining the connections and research relationships fellows build during their time in Austria rather than treating the fellowship as a one-off engagement.

## Benefits

The fellowship covers a stay of four to eighteen months in Austria, with financial support for the researcher's time there, plus material follow-up support once the fellowship period concludes, helping fellows maintain the professional connections and research momentum they built during their time in the country.

## Who Can Apply?

Applicants should be young university teachers or researchers specializing in German language and Austrian literature, generally no older than 35, with above-average academic qualifications. Prior university teaching experience is preferred, though not always strictly required, and applicants without it can still be competitive with a sufficiently strong research proposal.

## Fields of Study or Available Areas

The fellowship is narrowly and deliberately focused on German language and Austrian literature specifically, distinguishing it from broader German studies fellowships that might not emphasize the specifically Austrian literary and linguistic tradition.

## Required Documents

Applicants need the OeAD application form, a research or teaching proposal, a CV, academic transcripts or degree certificates, and recommendation letters.

## Application Process

Applicants register and submit their materials through the OeAD online application tool, known as grants.at, including a research or teaching proposal specific to Austrian literature or German-language topics with a genuine Austrian connection. Because OeAD typically runs this fellowship across two annual deadlines, applicants should confirm which cycle fits their planned timeline.

## Deadline

The current application deadline is 15 September 2026, one of two typical annual cycles for this fellowship, alongside an earlier deadline around March. Applicants who miss one cycle should check for the next available deadline rather than assuming a full year's wait.

## Why You Should Apply

For young Germanists whose research or teaching specifically touches Austrian literature and German-language studies, few fellowships offer this level of direct, funded access to Austrian academic institutions and archives. Among specialized fellowships in the humanities, the Franz Werfel Fellowship stands out for its precise focus and for OeAD's continued material support even after the fellowship period itself concludes.

For full eligibility details and the current application cycle, visit the official OeAD Franz Werfel Fellowship page: https://oead.at/en/study-research-teaching/overview-grants-and-scholarships/franz-werfel-scholarship
