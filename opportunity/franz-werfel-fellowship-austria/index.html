Franz Werfel Fellowship

## Introduction Named after the Austrian-Bohemian writer Franz Werfel, this fellowship from OeAD, Austria's national agency for education and internationalisation, supports young Germanists, scholars and teachers of German language and Austrian literature, to spend extended time as visiting researchers inside Austria itself. For early-career academics whose work centers specifically on German-language and Austrian literary studies, it offers something a general research grant cannot: direct access to the archives, libraries, and university departments most relevant to that particular field. ## About the Opportunity Fellows are placed as visiting researchers at Austrian university departments, libraries, archives, or research institutions for a period of four to eighteen months, depending on their specific project and needs. Beyond the fellowship period itself, OeAD provides material follow-up support afterward, reflecting an interest in sustaining the connections and research relationships fellows build during their time in Austria rather than treating the fellowship as a one-off engagement. ## Benefits The fellowship covers a stay of four to eighteen months in Austria, with financial support for the researcher's time there, plus material follow-up support once the fellowship period concludes, helping fellows maintain the professional connections and research momentum they built during their time in the country. ## Who Can Apply? Applicants should be young university teachers or researchers specializing in German language and Austrian literature, generally no older than 35, with above-average academic qualifications. Prior university teaching experience is preferred, though not always strictly required, and applicants without it can still be competitive with a sufficiently strong research proposal. ## Fields of Study or Available Areas The fellowship is narrowly and deliberately focused on German language and Austrian literature specifically, distinguishing it from broader German studies fellowships that might not emphasize the specifically Austrian literary and linguistic tradition. ## Required Documents Applicants need the OeAD application form, a research or teaching proposal, a CV, academic transcripts or degree certificates, and recommendation letters. ## Application Process Applicants register and submit their materials through the OeAD online application tool, known as grants.at, including a research or teaching proposal specific to Austrian literature or German-language topics with a genuine Austrian connection. Because OeAD typically runs this fellowship across two annual deadlines, applicants should confirm which cycle fits their planned timeline. ## Deadline The current application deadline is 15 September 2026, one of two typical annual cycles for this fellowship, alongside an earlier deadline around March. Applicants who miss one cycle should check for the next available deadline rather than assuming a full year's wait. ## Why You Should Apply For young Germanists whose research or teaching specifically touches Austrian literature and German-language studies, few fellowships offer this level of direct, funded access to Austrian academic institutions and archives. Among specialized fellowships in the humanities, the Franz Werfel Fellowship stands out for its precise focus and for OeAD's continued material support even after the fellowship period itself concludes. For full eligibility details and the current application cycle, visit the official OeAD Franz Werfel Fellowship page: https://oead.at/en/study-research-teaching/overview-grants-and-scholarships/franz-werfel-scholarship

Franz Werfel Fellowship Eligibility

The Franz Werfel Fellowship targets non-Austrian university teachers and early-career academics who specialise in German-language literature with a specific connection to Austrian writers and literary history. Applicants must currently hold a teaching or research position in higher education outside Austria. Austrian nationals and those already based in Austria are not eligible. The fellowship is particularly suited to scholars working on Austrian literary figures such as Franz Werfel, Ingeborg Bachmann, Thomas Bernhard, or Elfriede Jelinek, though proposals on other Austrian literary topics are also welcome.

Franz Werfel Fellowship Benefits

The fellowship is fully funded and supports a 4- to 9-month residency at the University of Vienna or another Austrian host institution. Benefits include a monthly living stipend, access to university libraries and archives, integration into the host department's research activities, and networking opportunities within the Austrian Germanist community. The programme also provides follow-up financial and material support to sustain the fellow's teaching and scholarly output after returning to their home institution.

Franz Werfel Fellowship Deadline and Timeline

There are two annual deadlines: 1 March and 15 September. Applications are submitted through grants.at, Austria's national grants database. The fellowship cycle typically begins a few months after the deadline, allowing time for selection and logistical arrangements. Always confirm exact dates on the OeAD website, as timelines may vary between cycles.

Required Documents for the Franz Werfel Fellowship

  • Research proposal — detailing your planned research focus, its connection to Austrian literature, and intended outcomes
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV) — including your academic positions, publications, and teaching experience
  • Proof of academic employment — a letter from your current institution confirming your position
  • Letters of recommendation — typically two academic references who can attest to your research quality
  • Host institution agreement — confirmation from an Austrian university department willing to host your research
  • Language proficiency evidence — demonstrating advanced German-language capability for research purposes

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Proposing a topic unrelated to Austrian literature — the fellowship specifically supports research connected to Austrian writers and literary history. Ensure your proposal has a clear Austrian focus.
  2. Applying without a host institution — you need confirmation from an Austrian university department before applying. Contact potential hosts well in advance of the deadline.
  3. Submitting an incomplete grants.at application — the online portal requires multiple fields and uploads. Start early and ensure all required documents are attached.
  4. Overlooking the language requirement — research and daily academic life at the host institution are conducted in German. Demonstrate strong language skills in your application.
