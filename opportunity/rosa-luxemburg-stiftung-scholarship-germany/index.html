Scholarship - Germany

Rosa Luxemburg Stiftung Scholarship

Introduction

Germany's Rosa-Luxemburg-Stiftung, the political foundation associated with the country's Left Party, funds highly-qualified undergraduate and doctoral students who combine academic strength with genuine social or political engagement, with an explicit preference for women, students from non-academic or migrant backgrounds, and other underrepresented groups. This equity-focused mission distinguishes it clearly from many peer German political foundation scholarships and shapes how the foundation evaluates every application it receives.

About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue master's or doctoral study across the social sciences, humanities, political science, law, economics, or arts, while also gaining access to the foundation's scholar community and political education programming designed to connect their academic work with broader social engagement. Because the foundation runs two application cycles each year, spring and autumn, prospective scholars have more flexibility in timing their application than programmes with a single annual deadline.

Benefits

Scholars receive a monthly stipend, along with research and travel grants and access to the foundation's scholar community and political education programming.

Who Can Apply?

Highly-qualified undergraduate and doctoral students from Germany and abroad who demonstrate strong social or political engagement can apply, with preference given to women, students from non-academic or migrant backgrounds, and other underrepresented groups.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields include social sciences, humanities, political science, law, economics, and the arts, reflecting the foundation's broad interest in scholarship connected to social and political engagement.

Required Documents

Applicants need an application form, a CV, a personal or political engagement statement, academic transcripts, and a recommendation letter.

Application Process

Applicants apply directly to the Rosa-Luxemburg-Stiftung Scholarship Department via its online application system during either the spring or autumn cycle.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 1 October 2026.

Why You Should Apply

For students who combine academic ability with genuine social or political engagement, particularly those from underrepresented backgrounds, this scholarship offers funding alongside a foundation explicitly committed to supporting exactly that combination of qualities.

For full details, visit the official Rosa-Luxemburg-Stiftung scholarships page: https://www.rosalux.de/en/foundation/rosa-luxemburg-stiftung/scholarships
