Scholarship - Germany

Rosa Luxemburg Stiftung Scholarship

## Introduction

The Rosa Luxemburg Stiftung Scholarship comes from the political foundation associated with Germany's Left Party (Die Linke), funding highly-qualified undergraduate and doctoral students who combine academic strength with strong social or political engagement. What sets it apart from several peer German political-foundation scholarships is its explicit preference for women, students from non-academic or migrant backgrounds, and other underrepresented groups, reflecting the foundation's broader mission around social equity.

## Opportunity Overview

- **Organization:** Rosa-Luxemburg-Stiftung - **Country of Study:** Germany - **Opportunity Type:** Scholarship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Master's or PhD - **Duration:** Length of the funded degree programme - **Official Website:** rosalux.de

## Benefits

- A monthly stipend - Research and travel grants - Access to the foundation's scholar community and political education programming

## Eligibility Criteria

- Be a highly-qualified undergraduate or doctoral student from Germany or abroad - Demonstrate strong social or political engagement - Preference is given to women, students from non-academic or migrant backgrounds, and other underrepresented groups

## Required Documents

- Application form - CV - Personal/political engagement statement - Academic transcripts - A recommendation letter

## Application Process

**Step 1.** Confirm your eligibility, particularly the emphasis on social/political engagement and underrepresented backgrounds where relevant.

**Step 2.** Prepare your CV, personal/political engagement statement, and transcripts.

**Step 3.** Secure a recommendation letter that can speak to your academic and civic profile.

**Step 4.** Submit your complete application via the Rosa-Luxemburg-Stiftung's online application system during the spring or autumn cycle.

**Step 5.** The foundation's selection committee reviews applications for academic strength, social/political engagement, and fit with its mission around underrepresented groups.

**Step 6.** Successful applicants receive scholarship confirmation and join the foundation's scholar community.

## Important Dates

- **Application deadline:** See the deadline listed for this opportunity (typically around 1 October) - **Selection outcome:** Communicated following the review period for that cycle

Confirm exact dates on the official Rosa-Luxemburg-Stiftung website, since the foundation runs both spring and autumn cycles.

## Selection Criteria

Selection weighs academic qualification alongside demonstrated social or political engagement, with explicit preference given to women, students from non-academic or migrant backgrounds, and other underrepresented groups as part of the foundation's equity-focused mission.

## Tips for Applicants

- Be specific and genuine in your personal/political engagement statement, since this is central to the foundation's selection focus - If you come from a non-academic or migrant background, or are a woman applying, note this clearly, since it is an explicit preference in selection - Choose a recommender who can speak to both your academic ability and civic engagement - Apply during either the spring or autumn cycle, whichever fits your programme timeline - Research the foundation's political education programming in advance, since genuine interest in this aspect strengthens your fit

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Who can apply for the Rosa Luxemburg Stiftung Scholarship?** Highly-qualified undergraduate and doctoral students from Germany or abroad with strong social/political engagement.

**Is it fully funded?** Yes — it includes a monthly stipend plus research and travel grants.

**Is there a preference for certain groups?** Yes, women, students from non-academic or migrant backgrounds, and other underrepresented groups are explicitly prioritized.

**How many application cycles are there?** Two per year, in spring and autumn.

**Can PhD students apply?** Yes, both master's and PhD levels are covered.

**What kind of engagement is valued?** Demonstrated social or political engagement connected to the foundation's broader mission.

## Apply Now

Submit your application through the official Rosa-Luxemburg-Stiftung online application system: https://www.rosalux.de/en/foundation/rosa-luxemburg-stiftung/scholarships
