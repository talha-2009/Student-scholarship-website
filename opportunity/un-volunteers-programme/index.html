UN Volunteers Programme

## Introduction The UN Volunteers Programme, run by United Nations Volunteers (UNV), mobilizes people worldwide to support peace and development through assignments across UN agencies. Unlike unpaid internships elsewhere in the UN system, UNV assignments come with a monthly living allowance, positioning it as a genuine mid-career or early-career volunteering pathway rather than a purely academic placement — suited to people who want practical development or humanitarian field experience within the UN system. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** United Nations Volunteers (UNV) - **Country of Study:** Global — assignments span UN agencies worldwide - **Opportunity Type:** Volunteer Program - **Funding Type:** Paid - **Degree Level:** Open to all (18+) - **Duration:** Varies by specific assignment - **Official Website:** unv.org ## Benefits - A monthly volunteer living allowance - Travel costs - Insurance - Professional development opportunities ## Eligibility Criteria - Be aged 18 or older, though minimum age can vary by specific assignment - Have relevant qualifications or experience, depending on the specific role - Meet any additional requirements set by the particular UN agency or assignment ## Required Documents - CV/resume - Education certificates - A motivation statement ## Application Process **Step 1.** Create a profile on the UNV talent pool portal. **Step 2.** Browse open assignments across UN agencies worldwide. **Step 3.** Apply to specific assignments matching your qualifications and interests, submitting your CV, education certificates, and motivation statement. **Step 4.** If shortlisted, you may be contacted by the hiring UN agency for further review or interview. **Step 5.** Successful candidates receive an assignment offer specifying duty station, allowance, and duration. ## Important Dates UNV assignments open on a rolling basis across many UN agencies rather than a single annual cycle. Please refer to the official UNV talent pool portal for current openings. ## Selection Criteria Selection is made by the specific hiring UN agency, generally based on how well your qualifications and experience match the particular assignment's requirements, alongside your motivation statement's relevance to development or humanitarian work. ## Tips for Applicants - Build a complete UNV talent pool profile, since agencies search this pool directly when filling assignments - Apply to multiple assignments matching your background, since opportunities open continuously across different agencies - Write a motivation statement that connects your specific experience to development or humanitarian work, not general enthusiasm alone - Keep your education certificates and CV updated and ready to submit quickly, since strong assignments can be competitive - Research the specific UN agency behind an assignment before applying, since UNV spans a wide range of agencies with different focuses ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the UN Volunteers Programme?** Applicants aged 18 and older with relevant qualifications or experience, depending on the assignment. **Is it paid?** Yes, it includes a monthly living allowance, travel costs, and insurance. **Where are assignments based?** Across UN agencies worldwide, varying by specific assignment. **How do I find assignments?** By creating a profile on the UNV talent pool portal and applying to open listings. **How long do assignments last?** Duration varies significantly by specific assignment. **Do I need prior UN experience?** Not necessarily, though relevant qualifications or experience strengthen your application for specific roles. ## Apply Now Create your profile and browse current assignments on the official UNV talent pool portal: https://www.unv.org/become-volunteer
