UN Volunteers Programme

Introduction

United Nations Volunteers, known as UNV, mobilizes people worldwide to support peace and development through assignments spanning UN agencies globally, distinguishing itself from several unpaid UN internships by offering a genuine paid living allowance to its volunteers. For anyone seeking practical development or humanitarian field experience within the UN system, but who cannot afford an unpaid placement, UNV offers a genuinely accessible entry point.

About the Opportunity

Volunteers take on assignments across UN agencies, contributing skills relevant to peace and development work in roles that vary considerably by agency, duty station, and current need. Because UNV maintains a talent pool that agencies search directly when filling openings, building and maintaining a strong profile is itself an ongoing part of finding an assignment, rather than a one-time application event.

Benefits

Volunteers receive a monthly volunteer living allowance, travel costs, insurance, and professional development opportunities throughout their assignment.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants aged 18 and older can apply, though minimum age and specific qualification requirements vary by assignment, with relevant qualifications or experience expected depending on the particular role.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Assignments span development and humanitarian work broadly, reflecting the wide range of UN agencies and country offices that draw on the UNV talent pool.

Required Documents

Applicants need a CV or resume, education certificates, and a motivation statement.

Application Process

Applicants create a profile on the UNV talent pool portal and apply to open assignments as they become available across different UN agencies and duty stations.

Deadline

UNV assignments open on a rolling basis; interested applicants should build a profile and monitor the talent pool portal regularly rather than waiting for a fixed application window.

Why You Should Apply

For those seeking genuine UN system experience with a paid living allowance rather than an unpaid internship, UNV offers a practical, accessible path into development and humanitarian work across a wide range of agencies and locations.

For full details, visit the official UNV talent pool portal: https://www.unv.org/become-volunteer
