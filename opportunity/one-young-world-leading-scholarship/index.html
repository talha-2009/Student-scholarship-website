One Young World Leading Scholarship

## Introduction The One Young World Leading Scholarship offers young changemakers a fully funded place at the One Young World Summit, the organization's flagship annual gathering of young leaders from around the world, held in 2026 in Cape Town, South Africa. Unlike academic scholarships, this is a conference delegate scholarship — it funds your attendance at a multi-day summit rather than a degree programme, with particular priority given to applicants from underrepresented countries and those attending for the first time. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** One Young World - **Country of Study:** South Africa (Cape Town, 2026 Summit) - **Opportunity Type:** Conference - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Open to all - **Duration:** The multi-day Summit period - **Official Website:** oneyoungworld.com ## Benefits - Full delegate access to the Summit - Hotel accommodation - Catering - Round-trip travel to Cape Town ## Eligibility Criteria - Be aged 18-35 - Show evidenced leadership and a genuine commitment to positive change - Priority is given to applicants from underrepresented countries and first-time Summit attendees ## Required Documents - Application form - A personal statement on your leadership impact ## Application Process **Step 1.** Complete the online scholarship application form on the One Young World website. **Step 2.** Write a personal statement describing your leadership impact and commitment to positive change. **Step 3.** Submit your application; selection is conducted on a rolling basis rather than a single review date. **Step 4.** Successful applicants receive confirmation of their delegate scholarship, including travel and accommodation arrangements for the Cape Town Summit. ## Important Dates - **Application deadline:** See the deadline listed for this opportunity (31 October 2026) - **Selection:** Rolling, so earlier applications may hear back sooner - **Summit dates:** Held in Cape Town, South Africa, in 2026 Because selection is rolling, applying earlier can improve your chances relative to waiting until close to the deadline. ## Selection Criteria One Young World reviews personal statements for evidence of genuine leadership impact and commitment to positive change, with explicit priority given to candidates from underrepresented countries and those who have not previously attended a Summit. ## Tips for Applicants - Apply as early as possible, since selection is rolling rather than based on a single review after the deadline - Use your personal statement to describe specific, concrete examples of leadership impact, not general aspirations - If you are from an underrepresented country or applying for the first time, this works in your favor under the stated priority criteria - Research the Summit's typical themes and format in advance, so your statement reflects genuine understanding of what you're applying for - Be honest and specific about the scale of your impact — quality of evidence matters more than the size of the project described ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the One Young World Leading Scholarship?** Applicants aged 18-35 with evidenced leadership and commitment to positive change. **Is it fully funded?** Yes — delegate access, accommodation, catering, and travel to Cape Town are all covered. **Is there a priority group?** Yes, applicants from underrepresented countries and first-time attendees are prioritized. **How is selection made?** On a rolling basis, so earlier applications can be reviewed sooner. **Where is the 2026 Summit held?** Cape Town, South Africa. **What do I need to submit?** An application form and a personal statement on your leadership impact. ## Apply Now Submit your application through the official One Young World scholarship page: https://www.oneyoungworld.com/scholarship/leading-scholarship-2026
