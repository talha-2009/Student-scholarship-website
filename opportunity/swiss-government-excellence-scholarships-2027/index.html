Scholarship - Switzerland

Swiss Government Excellence Scholarships

Introduction

Each year the Swiss Confederation awards Government Excellence Scholarships to strengthen academic exchange and research cooperation between Switzerland and more than 180 partner countries, administered by the Federal Commission for Scholarships for Foreign Students, commonly known by its German acronym ESKAS. Among government-funded scholarships in Europe, it stands out for its genuinely unrestricted disciplinary scope, open to virtually any academic field, though a specific structural requirement sets it apart: no application can proceed without a confirmed Swiss academic supervisor willing to support the project before submission even begins.

About the Opportunity

The programme offers three main tracks: research/PhD scholarships for postgraduate researchers holding at least a master's degree, postdoctoral scholarships for those who have already completed a PhD, and a smaller art scholarship track for students pursuing an initial master's in an artistic field, available only to applicants from a limited set of eligible countries. Recipients can study or research at any Swiss cantonal university, either of Switzerland's two federal institutes of technology (ETH Zurich or EPFL), a university of applied sciences, or one of four ETH Domain research institutes, with country-specific deadlines and requirements varying depending on the applicant's nationality.

Benefits

As of the 2026-27 cycle, scholars receive a monthly stipend of CHF 2,450 for PhD and research scholars, or CHF 3,500 for postdoctoral researchers, plus a housing allowance of CHF 600, mandatory Swiss health insurance for non-EU/EFTA nationals, and a half-fare travel card for Swiss public transport; the research/PhD award runs an initial 12 months, renewable up to 36 months total based on progress, while postdoctoral awards are fixed at one year with no extension.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need to be citizens of one of the more than 180 eligible countries (Swiss nationals are not eligible), holding a master's degree or equivalent by the relevant deadline for research/PhD tracks, or a completed PhD for postdoctoral applicants; priority goes to candidates who have not previously studied or conducted research in Switzerland, and no one who has already received this scholarship once may apply again.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The scholarship covers virtually all academic disciplines for research and PhD tracks, from natural sciences and mathematics through life sciences, economics, and the humanities, plus a dedicated track for artistic study at Swiss conservatories or universities of the arts.

Required Documents

Applicants need the FCS application form, a motivation letter (maximum two pages), a signed research proposal with a timeline (maximum five pages, using the official FCS template), a support letter and short CV from their confirmed Swiss academic host, two confidential recommendation letters from different professors submitted via FCS reference forms, academic transcripts and degree certificates, a medical certificate, and passport copies.

Application Process

The essential first step is securing a formal letter of support from a Swiss academic supervisor at an eligible institution, since applications lacking this are automatically rejected; applicants then check their nationality's specific deadline and requirements, and submit their complete application online through the ESKAS portal, where it undergoes preliminary review by national authorities before final evaluation by the FCS.

Deadline

Applications for the next cycle open 20 August 2026, with deadlines varying by country of origin; applicants should confirm their specific national deadline on the official SBFI website well in advance, since finding a Swiss supervisor typically takes considerable lead time before the portal even opens.

Why You Should Apply

For postgraduate researchers and select artists from eligible countries seeking funded study in Switzerland, this scholarship offers strong monthly support and access to some of Europe's most respected research institutions, though success depends heavily on securing a genuine Swiss academic supervisor well before the application window opens, making early outreach the single most important step.

For country-specific eligibility, deadlines, and documents, visit the official Swiss Government Excellence Scholarships website: https://www.sbfi.admin.ch/en/swiss-government-excellence-scholarships
