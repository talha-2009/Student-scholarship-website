Exchange Program - Canada

Fulbright Canada Traditional Student Award

Introduction

While Fulbright Canada's All Disciplines Scholar Award targets established academics, the Traditional Student Award serves a different group entirely: Canadian bachelor's degree holders looking to pursue graduate study, research, or early-career professional development in the United States for a fully funded academic year. What distinguishes this track from many peer scholarships is a practical flexibility, applicants do not need a confirmed U.S. university acceptance before applying, only evidence that an application is underway.

About the Opportunity

Recipients spend an eight-month academic year at a U.S. institution pursuing graduate study, research, or professional development, depending on their specific goals and field. Because Fulbright Canada accepts applicants whose U.S. institution decision is still pending, candidates can move forward with their Fulbright application in parallel with their university applications rather than waiting for an admission decision first.

Benefits

The award provides a fixed USD 25,000 for the eight-month academic year, covering housing, travel, tuition, and academic expenses. Recipients are enrolled in the ASPE health benefit plan for the duration of their time in the US, and Fulbright Canada provides visa and professional services support throughout.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need to be Canadian citizens holding a bachelor's degree prior to the grant start date, with strong English proficiency and compliance with Fulbright Foreign Scholarship Board guidelines and relevant visa and immigration regulations.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The award covers master's, PhD, and early-career professional development pursuits across all fields, giving applicants flexibility to pursue graduate study or research wherever their academic interests lead.

Required Documents

Applicants need the online application form, transcripts, either a letter of invitation or acceptance from a U.S. host institution or proof that an application is currently in progress, recommendation letters, and English proficiency proof.

Application Process

Applicants identify their intended U.S. institution and programme and begin that application process, then submit their Fulbright Canada application in parallel, including transcripts, recommendation letters, and English proficiency evidence. Because a confirmed U.S. acceptance is not required at the time of the Fulbright application, proof that the university application is genuinely underway is sufficient, giving applicants meaningful flexibility in how they sequence the two processes.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 6 October 2026. Given the need to coordinate both a U.S. university application and the Fulbright Canada submission, applicants should start both processes well ahead of this date.

Why You Should Apply

For Canadian graduates who want a fully funded year of study or professional development in the United States, this award offers a genuinely accessible path that does not require securing university admission first, a meaningful advantage for candidates still finalizing their US university plans. Among exchange programs for early-career Canadians, the Traditional Student Award's flexibility around admission timing sets it apart from scholarships that require a confirmed acceptance before applying.

For full application details, visit the official Fulbright Canada page: https://www.fulbright.ca/programs/american-students/traditional-awards
