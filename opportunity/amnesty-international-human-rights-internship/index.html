Amnesty International Human Rights Internship

## Introduction

Amnesty International has spent more than six decades investigating and campaigning against human rights abuses worldwide, and its internship program gives graduate students and recent graduates a genuine seat inside that work rather than a peripheral administrative role. For anyone building a career in human rights, international law, or advocacy, interning with an organization of Amnesty's scale and reach offers exposure to real casework and campaigning that few other placements can match.

## About the Opportunity

Amnesty's internships are distributed across its many offices worldwide, and the specific focus of any given internship depends heavily on which office and team an intern joins, whether that is research into a particular region's human rights situation, legal advocacy work, or public campaigning. Interns work alongside experienced human rights professionals, contributing to ongoing projects rather than shadow-only observation, and gain direct insight into how a major international human rights organization operates day to day.

## Benefits

Unlike some human rights and advocacy internships that go entirely unpaid, Amnesty offers a stipend, though the exact amount varies by office and location. Interns gain hands-on advocacy experience working on live human rights issues, and receive mentorship directly from professionals with deep experience in the field, a benefit that often proves valuable well beyond the internship itself for those pursuing careers in this space.

## Who Can Apply?

Amnesty's internships are aimed at graduate students and recent graduates with a strong interest in human rights, law, or international relations. There is no single fixed academic requirement beyond that general profile, though candidates with coursework, research, or prior experience directly connected to human rights or legal advocacy are typically better positioned for competitive postings.

## Fields of Study or Available Areas

Human rights and law form the core focus areas, though the specific subject matter of any internship depends on the office and team, spanning international law, regional human rights research, campaigning and communications, and legal advocacy.

## Required Documents

Applicants generally need a CV and a cover letter tailored to the specific posting they are applying for. Some postings also request a writing sample, particularly for research or advocacy-focused roles where written analysis is central to the work.

## Application Process

Amnesty posts internship opportunities through its official careers portal as specific positions become available across its various offices. Because postings open individually rather than through one central annual cycle, prospective applicants need to check the careers portal directly and apply to specific listings that match their interests and location preferences. Given Amnesty's global reach, opportunities in different regions can vary considerably in focus and requirements, so reviewing each posting carefully before applying is worthwhile.

## Deadline

There is no single fixed deadline for Amnesty International internships, since postings open on a rolling basis throughout the year as specific offices identify a need. Interested candidates should check the official careers portal regularly rather than waiting for one annual application window.

## Why You Should Apply

For students and graduates serious about a career in human rights advocacy, law, or international relations, few internships offer the combination of scale, global reach, and hands-on advocacy work that Amnesty provides. The stipend, while variable by office, sets this apart from many comparable unpaid opportunities in the advocacy sector, and the mentorship from established human rights professionals offers a meaningful head start for anyone building a long-term career in this field.

To browse current internship postings, visit the official Amnesty International careers portal: https://www.amnesty.org/en/careers/
