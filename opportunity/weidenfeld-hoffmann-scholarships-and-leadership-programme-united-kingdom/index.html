Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarships and Leadership Programme

Introduction

The Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarships and Leadership Programme is one of Oxford's most generous graduate awards, explicitly designed for students who plan to return home and improve public life there, rather than pursue a long-term career abroad. It funds a new full-time graduate course at Oxford in full, alongside a structured leadership programme.

About the Opportunity

Applicants select the Weidenfeld-Hoffmann option within their standard Oxford graduate application and submit a dedicated Scholarship Statement explaining their intended return home and plan to improve public life there, which functions as the core differentiator in an otherwise standard Oxford admissions process.

Benefits

Scholars receive 100% of Oxford course fees plus a living cost grant of at least GBP 20,780 per year, along with a leadership development programme including mentoring and networking.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants to a new full-time graduate course at Oxford who intend to return to their country of ordinary residence and can show a clear connection between their study and improving public life there can apply; current Oxford students (unless already scholars) and deferred offer holders are not eligible.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The scholarship covers over 40 eligible Oxford graduate courses across a wide range of fields.

Required Documents

Applicants need the Oxford graduate application form, a Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarships Statement, academic transcripts, and references.

Application Process

Applicants select the Weidenfeld-Hoffmann option on the Oxford graduate application form, submit their Scholarship Statement, and apply by their specific course's December/January deadline.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 15 January 2027.

Why You Should Apply

For students genuinely committed to returning home and driving positive public change, this scholarship offers comprehensive Oxford funding tied directly to that mission, standing apart from scholarships that don't require this specific commitment.

For full details, visit the official Oxford Weidenfeld-Hoffmann page: https://www.ox.ac.uk/admissions/graduate/fees-and-funding/fees-funding-and-scholarship-search/weidenfeld-hoffmann-scholarships-and-leadership-programme
