Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarships and Leadership Programme

## Introduction The Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarships and Leadership Programme is one of Oxford's most generous graduate awards, explicitly designed for students who plan to return home and improve public life there, rather than pursue a long-term career abroad. It funds a new full-time graduate course at Oxford in full, alongside a structured leadership programme, and its eligibility rules are unusually specific about intent: applicants must show they plan to return to their country of ordinary residence and articulate a clear link between their studies and improving public life there. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** University of Oxford / Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Trust - **Country of Study:** United Kingdom (Oxford) - **Opportunity Type:** Scholarship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Master's (new full-time graduate course) - **Duration:** Length of the chosen Oxford graduate course - **Official Website:** ox.ac.uk ## Benefits - 100% of Oxford course fees - A living cost grant of at least £20,780 per year - A leadership development programme including mentoring and networking opportunities ## Eligibility Criteria - Be applying to a new full-time graduate course at Oxford (not already enrolled, unless already a scholar) - Intend to return to your country of ordinary residence after completing the course - Be able to show a clear connection between your proposed study and improving public life in your home country or region - Not be a current Oxford student (unless already a Weidenfeld-Hoffmann scholar) or a deferred offer holder ## Required Documents - The University of Oxford graduate application form - A Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarships Statement - Academic transcripts - References ## Application Process **Step 1.** Choose your intended Oxford graduate course and confirm its specific application deadline, since these fall in December or January depending on the course. **Step 2.** Complete the standard Oxford graduate application form, selecting the Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarship option. **Step 3.** Write and submit your Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarships Statement, focused specifically on your intended return home and your plan to improve public life there. **Step 4.** Submit your transcripts and references alongside your application. **Step 5.** Shortlisted candidates may be invited for further review or interview as part of the Trust's selection process. **Step 6.** Successful applicants receive both an Oxford admission offer and full scholarship funding. ## Important Dates - **Application deadline:** See the deadline listed for this opportunity (typically early January, aligned with your specific course's December/January Oxford deadline) - **Programme start:** The following autumn at Oxford Because deadlines are tied to your specific Oxford course's own timeline, confirm the exact date for your chosen programme. ## Selection Criteria Selection weighs strong academic potential for Oxford graduate study alongside a genuinely convincing Scholarship Statement that connects your studies to a specific, credible plan for improving public life in your home country or region after you return. ## Tips for Applicants - Write your Scholarship Statement around a specific, realistic plan for impact at home — vague statements about wanting to "make a difference" are less competitive than concrete plans - Apply well ahead of your specific course's December/January deadline, since Oxford course deadlines vary - Make sure you are not currently enrolled at Oxford or holding a deferred offer, since these disqualify most applicants under this scheme - Choose references who can speak to both your academic ability and your genuine commitment to public service in your home country - Research the leadership development programme's structure in advance so your application shows understanding of what the scholarship offers beyond funding ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarship?** Applicants to a new full-time Oxford graduate course who plan to return home and improve public life there. **Is it fully funded?** Yes — 100% of course fees plus a living cost grant of at least £20,780 per year. **Can current Oxford students apply?** No, except for those already holding a Weidenfeld-Hoffmann scholarship. **Do I need to prove I'll return home?** Yes, this is a core eligibility requirement, not just a preference. **What is the leadership programme like?** It includes structured mentoring and networking alongside your academic studies. **When is the deadline?** Typically early January, aligned with your specific Oxford course's deadline. ## Apply Now Review your specific course's deadline and apply through the official Oxford Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarships page: https://www.ox.ac.uk/admissions/graduate/fees-and-funding/fees-funding-and-scholarship-search/weidenfeld-hoffmann-scholarships-and-leadership-programme
