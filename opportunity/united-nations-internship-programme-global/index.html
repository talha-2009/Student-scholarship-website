United Nations Internship Programme

## Introduction

The United Nations Internship Programme places students and recent graduates directly inside UN Secretariat offices worldwide, from New York headquarters to regional commissions, giving a firsthand view of how multilateral diplomacy and international administration actually function day to day. It is unpaid by design, reflecting the UN's long-standing internship policy, which makes it best suited to students who can cover their own costs during the placement, whether through savings, family support, academic funding, or a home-institution stipend. For anyone building a career in international affairs, development, or diplomacy, it remains one of the most direct ways to gain UN Secretariat experience.

## Opportunity Overview

- **Organization:** United Nations Secretariat - **Country of Study:** Global — postings across UN Secretariat offices worldwide - **Opportunity Type:** Internship - **Funding Type:** Unpaid - **Degree Level:** Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD - **Duration:** Typically 2-6 months, depending on the specific posting - **Official Website:** careers.un.org

## Benefits

- Direct professional exposure to the UN's international work environment - Practical experience relevant to careers in diplomacy, development, and international administration - Possible academic credit, depending on your home institution's own policy - Access to the UN's professional network and, in many duty stations, UN premises and facilities - No salary is paid — interns are responsible for their own travel, accommodation, insurance, and living costs

## Eligibility Criteria

- Be currently enrolled in, or have graduated within the past year from, a bachelor's, master's, or PhD programme - Have a strong command of English or French, the UN Secretariat's working languages - Not be a child or sibling of a UN Secretariat staff member, per UN conflict-of-interest rules - Meet any additional requirements specified by the particular office or department you apply to, since eligibility can be refined at the posting level

## Required Documents

- A completed Inspira profile, including education, work experience, and language details - A tailored cover letter referencing the specific Job ID of the internship posting - Proof of current enrollment or recent graduation

## Application Process

**Step 1.** Create a profile on Inspira, the UN's official careers and recruitment platform, via careers.un.org.

**Step 2.** Search open Internship postings across UN Secretariat offices and departments.

**Step 3.** Prepare a cover letter tailored to the specific posting, referencing its Job ID directly.

**Step 4.** Submit your application through Inspira before the posting's stated deadline.

**Step 5.** If shortlisted, you may be contacted for an interview by the hiring office.

**Step 6.** Selected interns receive an offer letter specifying their duty station, start date, and duration.

## Important Dates

Because postings open on a rolling basis throughout the year across many offices, there is no single annual deadline. Please refer to the official Inspira portal for current open postings and their individual deadlines.

## Selection Criteria

Hiring offices generally select candidates based on the relevance of their academic background and language skills to the specific team's work, along with the strength and specificity of their cover letter — generic applications that don't address the particular office's mandate are less competitive.

## Tips for Applicants

- Search Inspira regularly, since UN internship postings open and close continuously rather than on a single fixed cycle - Write a cover letter specific to each posting's Job ID and stated responsibilities, rather than reusing one generic letter - Plan your finances before applying, since this internship is unpaid and you are responsible for all living and travel costs - Apply to postings in offices genuinely aligned with your academic background or career goals, since fit matters more than volume of applications - Keep your Inspira profile updated, since recruiters review it directly alongside your cover letter

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Who can apply for the UN Internship Programme?** Students enrolled in, or graduates within the past year of, a bachelor's, master's, or PhD programme.

**Is the UN Internship Programme paid?** No, it is unpaid; interns cover their own travel, accommodation, and living costs.

**Where are internships based?** UN Secretariat offices worldwide, including New York headquarters and regional commissions.

**How long does the internship last?** Typically 2-6 months, depending on the specific posting.

**Do I need to know French?** A strong command of English or French is required, since these are the UN Secretariat's working languages.

**Can recent graduates apply?** Yes, graduates within the past year of a relevant degree are eligible.
