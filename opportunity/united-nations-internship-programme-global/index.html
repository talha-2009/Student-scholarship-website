United Nations Internship Programme

Introduction

The United Nations Internship Programme places students and recent graduates directly inside UN Secretariat offices worldwide, from New York headquarters to regional commissions, giving a firsthand view of how multilateral diplomacy and international administration actually function day to day. It is unpaid by design, reflecting the UN's long-standing internship policy, which makes it best suited to students who can cover their own costs during the placement.

About the Opportunity

Interns join a specific UN Secretariat office or department, contributing to real ongoing work rather than a purely observational role, with the specific nature of the work depending heavily on which office and team an intern is placed with. Because postings span the full breadth of UN Secretariat functions, from political affairs through administration and communications, the actual day-to-day experience varies considerably by placement.

Benefits

Interns gain direct professional exposure to the UN's international work environment, practical experience relevant to careers in diplomacy and international administration, and possible academic credit depending on their home institution's policy; the internship is unpaid, with travel, accommodation, insurance, and living costs the intern's own responsibility.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants enrolled in or graduated within the past year from a bachelor's, master's, or PhD programme can apply, with a strong command of English or French required, and applicants must not be the child or sibling of a UN Secretariat staff member.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The internship spans all academic fields, reflecting the full breadth of functions across the UN Secretariat, from policy and diplomacy through administration and technical support roles.

Required Documents

Applicants need an Inspira profile covering education, work experience, and languages, a tailored cover letter, and proof of enrollment or graduation.

Application Process

Applicants create an Inspira profile via careers.un.org, browse open Internship postings, and submit a tailored application referencing the specific Job ID before the posting's deadline.

Deadline

Postings open on a rolling basis throughout the year across many offices; applicants should check the Inspira portal regularly for current listings.

Why You Should Apply

For students and graduates building a career in international affairs, diplomacy, or global administration, few internships offer this direct a view into UN Secretariat operations, making the unpaid nature of the role worthwhile for those genuinely committed to this career path.

For current openings, visit the official UN Careers portal: https://careers.un.org/
