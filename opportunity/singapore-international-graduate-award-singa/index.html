Singapore International Graduate Award

Introduction

The Singapore International Graduate Award, known as SINGA, is a joint initiative between A*STAR and four of Singapore's leading universities, NTU, NUS, SMU, and SUTD, funding international students to pursue a fully funded PhD in Science and Engineering. Receiving roughly 30,000 applications a year from more than 170 countries, SINGA is genuinely competitive, but it offers something distinctive: a single application that covers both scholarship funding and PhD placement together, rather than requiring separate applications to A*STAR and a Singapore university.

About the Opportunity

Scholars conduct doctoral research primarily at A*STAR Research Institutes, NTU, NUS, SMU, or SUTD, depending on their chosen research area, working within Singapore's genuinely multicultural research community alongside mentors and students from dozens of countries. Because SINGA is bond-free, unlike some Singapore government scholarships that require post-study service commitments, awardees are free to pursue their careers wherever they choose after completing their PhD.

Benefits

Scholars receive full tuition fees, a monthly stipend of SGD 2,700 that increases to SGD 3,200 after passing the Qualifying Examination, a one-time airfare grant of up to SGD 1,500, and a one-time settling-in allowance of SGD 1,000, for up to four years of PhD study.

Who Can Apply?

International graduates of any nationality with an excellent academic record and a genuine interest in Science and Engineering research can apply, provided they are not Singapore citizens or Permanent Residents and have not previously studied at a Singapore tertiary institution; strong written and spoken English and positive academic references are also expected.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

SINGA funds PhD study exclusively in Science and Engineering, spanning biomedical sciences and physical science and engineering; programmes in humanities, arts, social sciences, or economics are not eligible.

Required Documents

Applicants need a scanned passport copy, a recent photo, bachelor's and/or master's transcripts in English, a degree certificate or a university letter confirming pending conferral, and two online recommendation reports submitted directly by referees; standardized test scores like GRE or IELTS are optional at the SINGA stage.

Application Process

Applicants register on the SINGA portal, browse available research areas and projects, and submit their application alongside two referee recommendation reports; because the application is only processed once both references arrive, applicants are strongly advised to apply at least two weeks before the deadline to give referees adequate time to respond.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 1 December 2026, for the following intake; SINGA typically runs application windows tied to specific intake dates, so applicants should confirm the current cycle's exact window on the official portal.

Why You Should Apply

For international science and engineering graduates who want a fully funded, bond-free PhD with a genuinely streamlined single application covering both funding and university placement, SINGA offers comprehensive financial support and access to Singapore's leading research institutions, though the volume of global applicants makes early, complete submission essential.

For full application details, visit the official SINGA page on the A*STAR website: https://www.a-star.edu.sg/scholarships
