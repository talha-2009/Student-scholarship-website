Fellowship - China

Schwarzman Scholars Program

Introduction

Schwarzman Scholars was built on a specific bet: that the next generation of global leaders, whatever their own nationality, need firsthand understanding of China's role in the world. Founded in 2016 with a landing gift from Stephen A. Schwarzman and modeled explicitly on the Rhodes Scholarship, the programme funds a one-year Master's in Global Affairs at Tsinghua University in Beijing, bringing together scholars from dozens of countries who live together on campus while studying policy, economics, and international affairs.

About the Opportunity

Scholars live in a dedicated residential building on Tsinghua's campus, studying alongside a genuinely international cohort while participating in an intensive programme combining coursework, leadership training, mentorship, and travel across China. Because scholars are drawn from many countries and fields simultaneously, the programme functions as much as a leadership and cross-cultural immersion experience as a traditional academic degree, with the shared living arrangement specifically designed to build lasting relationships across the cohort.

Benefits

Scholars receive full tuition and fees, room and board, round-trip travel to Beijing, in-country study tours, course materials, a laptop and smartphone, health insurance, and a personal stipend for the one-year programme.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants of any nationality and field, aged 18 to 28 as of August 1 of the enrollment year, holding or on track to complete an undergraduate degree, with strong English proficiency, can apply, with a separate, earlier deadline applying specifically to Chinese-passport applicants.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The programme spans global affairs, economics, business, public policy, and international relations within its Master's in Global Affairs curriculum.

Required Documents

Applicants need an online application, a resume or CV capped at two pages, a Leadership Essay of 750 words, a Statement of Purpose of 500 words, transcripts, three letters of recommendation, and English proficiency scores where applicable.

Application Process

Applicants apply online via the Schwarzman Scholars portal with essays, resume, transcripts, and recommendations; shortlisted candidates are interviewed October through November, with results announced in December.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 9 September 2026.

Why You Should Apply

For ambitious young people of any field who want deep, structured exposure to China's global role alongside genuinely comprehensive funding, Schwarzman Scholars offers a residential, cross-cultural experience that few master's programmes anywhere can replicate.

For full application details, visit the official Schwarzman Scholars admissions page: https://www.schwarzmanscholars.org/admissions/
