Fellowship - China

Schwarzman Scholars Program

## Introduction

Schwarzman Scholars is a one-year master's programme in Global Affairs at Tsinghua University in Beijing, created in 2016 with a founding gift from Stephen A. Schwarzman and modeled explicitly on the Rhodes Scholarship. It was built on the premise that the next generation of global leaders needs firsthand understanding of China, regardless of their own nationality or field. Scholars come from dozens of countries each year and live together in a dedicated building on Tsinghua's campus, studying policy, economics, and international affairs alongside a heavy programme of leadership training, mentorship, and travel across China. It suits ambitious early-career professionals and strong recent graduates who want exposure to China's role in global affairs, rather than students seeking a narrow academic specialism.

## Opportunity Overview

- **Organization:** Schwarzman Scholars / Tsinghua University - **Country of Study:** China (Beijing) - **Opportunity Type:** Fellowship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Master's (Master of Global Affairs) - **Duration:** One academic year - **Official Website:** schwarzmanscholars.org

## Benefits

- Full tuition and fees at Tsinghua University - Room and board for the full academic year - Round-trip travel to and from Beijing - Funded study tours and travel within China during the programme - All course materials, plus a laptop and smartphone provided to each scholar - Comprehensive health insurance - A personal stipend for incidental expenses - Lifetime access to the Schwarzman Scholars alumni and mentor network

## Eligibility Criteria

- Be aged between 18 and 28 (not yet turned 29) by August 1 of the year you would enroll - Hold, or be on track to complete by August 1, a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution — open to any nationality and any undergraduate field of study - Demonstrate strong English proficiency, since the programme is taught in English (English test scores may be required depending on your academic background) - Show leadership potential and a record of achievement in academics, work, or extracurricular activities - Applicants holding Chinese passports follow a separate, earlier application deadline than the general international pool

## Required Documents

- Online application form - Resume/CV (maximum two pages) - Leadership Essay (750 words) - Statement of Purpose (500 words) - Academic transcripts from all post-secondary institutions attended - Three letters of recommendation - English proficiency test scores, if required for your academic background

## Application Process

**Step 1.** Register on the Schwarzman Scholars online application portal.

**Step 2.** Complete your personal and academic information and confirm you meet the age and degree requirements.

**Step 3.** Write your Leadership Essay and Statement of Purpose according to the word limits set by the programme.

**Step 4.** Upload your transcripts, CV, and arrange for your three recommenders to submit letters directly through the portal.

**Step 5.** Submit your complete application before the deadline (Chinese-passport applicants must meet an earlier date).

**Step 6.** Shortlisted candidates are invited to interview, typically between October and November, either in person or virtually depending on location.

**Step 7.** Final decisions are communicated in December, with the programme beginning the following August in Beijing.

## Important Dates

- **Application deadline:** See the deadline listed for this opportunity (Schwarzman Scholars typically closes in September, with an earlier date for Chinese-passport applicants) - **Interviews:** October-November - **Results announced:** December - **Programme start:** August of the following year in Beijing

Please refer to the official website for the current cycle's exact dates, as they can shift slightly year to year.

## Selection Criteria

Schwarzman Scholars selects for demonstrated leadership potential above pure academic achievement, looking for candidates who have already shown initiative and impact in their chosen field, strong intellectual curiosity about China's global role, and the interpersonal skills needed to thrive in a small, intensely collaborative, live-in cohort with people from dramatically different backgrounds.

## Tips for Applicants

- Use the Leadership Essay to describe a specific moment you led or influenced an outcome, rather than summarising your resume in prose form - Connect your Statement of Purpose explicitly to China's global role — panels want to see genuine curiosity about the region, not just an interest in an elite fellowship - Choose recommenders who can speak to leadership and character, not only academic performance, since the programme weighs this heavily - Prepare for interviews that may test how you think on your feet about global affairs, not just what you already know - If you hold a Chinese passport, double-check the earlier deadline that applies to you specifically

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Who can apply for Schwarzman Scholars?** Applicants aged 18-28 of any nationality with a bachelor's degree, from any academic field.

**Is Schwarzman Scholars fully funded?** Yes — tuition, accommodation, travel, insurance, and a personal stipend are all covered.

**Do I need to speak Chinese?** No, the programme is taught in English; Mandarin lessons are provided as part of the curriculum.

**Can international students apply?** Yes, the programme is explicitly designed for a globally diverse cohort, including US, European, and Asian applicants alongside Chinese nationals.

**Is there an age limit?** Yes, applicants must not have turned 29 by August 1 of their enrollment year.

**Where do scholars live?** All scholars live together in a dedicated residential building on the Tsinghua University campus in Beijing.

**What happens after graduation?** Scholars join a global alumni network and are expected to maintain long-term engagement with the Schwarzman Scholars community.

## Apply Now

Review full eligibility details and start your application on the official Schwarzman Scholars website: https://www.schwarzmanscholars.org/admissions/
