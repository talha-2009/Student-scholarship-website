Scholarship - Germany

Deutschlandstipendium

## Introduction

Germany's Deutschlandstipendium, literally the "Germany Scholarship," operates on a distinctive public-private funding model unlike most national scholarship programmes: for every euro a private donor, company, or foundation contributes toward a student's scholarship, the German government matches it. Launched in 2011, the scheme now runs through individual universities across the country, each managing its own version of the programme with its own pool of private sponsors and its own selection process.

## About the Opportunity

Because Deutschlandstipendium is delivered university by university rather than through one central body, the actual experience of applying, and the odds of success, depend heavily on which German university a student attends and how many private sponsors that particular institution has secured. Selected students receive a monthly stipend for up to two years, funded jointly by their university's private donors and matched government funds, with no expectation of repayment.

## Benefits

Scholars receive a monthly stipend of €300 for up to two years, a modest but meaningful contribution toward living costs during their studies. Because German public universities generally do not charge tuition fees for this level of study, the stipend functions primarily as living-cost support rather than a tuition offset.

## Who Can Apply?

Any high-achieving student of any nationality enrolled at a participating German university can apply, with selection based not solely on academic performance but also on extracurricular involvement and personal achievements or challenges a candidate has overcome. This broader selection criteria is a deliberate design choice, intended to widen access beyond students with the single strongest transcripts.

## Fields of Study or Available Areas

The scholarship covers bachelor's and master's students across all academic fields, with no restriction to a particular discipline, since eligibility depends on university enrollment and personal merit rather than field of study.

## Required Documents

Applicants need their university's application form, academic transcripts, a CV, a motivation letter, and proof of extracurricular or social engagement supporting their broader case for selection.

## Application Process

Because each university runs its own Deutschlandstipendium office and process, applicants need to apply directly through that office rather than a single national portal, and should expect deadlines and exact procedures to vary meaningfully from one institution to another. Students already enrolled at a participating university are generally best positioned to navigate this process, since they can consult their university's specific office directly for guidance.

## Deadline

Because deadlines are set independently by each participating university, there is no single national date. Prospective applicants should contact their specific German university's Deutschlandstipendium office directly to confirm the current cycle's deadline.

## Why You Should Apply

For students already studying, or planning to study, at a German university, Deutschlandstipendium offers accessible additional funding without requiring the kind of narrow academic excellence that some other scholarships demand, since extracurricular involvement and personal circumstances genuinely factor into selection. Among partially funded scholarships for students in Germany, its public-private matching structure and broad eligibility make it worth investigating regardless of field of study or nationality.

For general programme information and to find your university's specific office, visit the official Deutschlandstipendium website: https://www.deutschlandstipendium.de/
