UNICEF Internship Programme

UNICEF offers students and recent graduates practical experience in the humanitarian and development sector through internship opportunities across offices worldwide. Applicants should be at least 18 and enrolled in or recently graduated from an undergraduate, graduate, or PhD program.

UNICEF Internship Eligibility

The UNICEF Internship Programme is open to students currently enrolled in an undergraduate, master's, or PhD programme, as well as recent graduates who completed their studies within the past two years. Applicants must be at least 18 years old and proficient in at least one of UNICEF's working languages: English, French, or Spanish. There are no nationality restrictions, and interns may be placed at UNICEF offices, country programmes, or research centres anywhere in the world. Candidates should have a demonstrated interest in child rights, international development, public health, education, or a related humanitarian field.

UNICEF Internship Benefits and Stipend

Selected interns receive a monthly stipend to help cover living expenses during their placement. Subject to available funding, UNICEF may also provide a one-time contribution toward travel and visa costs. Beyond financial support, interns gain invaluable professional experience working alongside UNICEF's global team of child rights advocates, policy experts, and programme managers. The internship offers mentorship, exposure to UN systems and procedures, and a strong foundation for a career in international development and humanitarian work.

UNICEF Internship Deadline

UNICEF posts internship positions on a rolling basis throughout the year. There is no single application deadline — new opportunities appear regularly as offices worldwide identify staffing needs. Candidates are encouraged to check the UNICEF careers portal frequently and apply as soon as a suitable position is posted. Competition for placements is high, so early applications are strongly recommended.

Required Documents for the UNICEF Internship

  • Updated CV or resume — highlighting relevant coursework, volunteer work, and any previous internship or professional experience
  • Cover letter — explaining your interest in UNICEF's mission and the specific role you are applying for
  • Proof of enrolment or graduation — a letter from your university or a copy of your diploma if you graduated within the past two years
  • Copy of passport or national ID
  • Language proficiency evidence — if applicable, proof of proficiency in English, French, or Spanish

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Applying only once — since positions are posted on a rolling basis, check the portal regularly and apply to multiple openings that match your profile.
  2. Submitting a generic cover letter — tailor each application to the specific role and office. Mention why you are interested in that particular placement.
  3. Overlooking language requirements — some offices require French or Spanish. Verify the language needs before applying.
  4. Waiting until the last minute — popular positions close quickly. Submit your application as soon as you find a suitable opening.
