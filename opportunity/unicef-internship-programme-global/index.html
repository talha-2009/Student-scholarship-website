UNICEF Internship Programme

Introduction

UNICEF's Internship Programme places students and recent graduates inside one of the UN's most visible agencies, dedicated to child rights, education, health, and protection worldwide. Compared with several other UN-family internships, UNICEF stands out for offering a monthly stipend rather than running fully unpaid, along with support toward travel and visa costs where funding allows.

About the Opportunity

Interns support UNICEF's global programming within a specific office or thematic area, whether child protection, education, health, or another priority, gaining direct exposure to how one of the world's most recognized child-focused organizations designs and delivers its work.

Benefits

Interns receive a monthly stipend, a one-time lump sum contribution toward travel and visa costs subject to available funding, and visa support documentation.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants enrolled in an undergraduate, graduate, or PhD programme, or graduated within the past two years, proficient in English, French, or Spanish plus the working language of the applied office, can apply.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

UNICEF internships span all fields, reflecting the agency's wide-ranging work across child rights, education, health, and protection.

Required Documents

Applicants need a CV or resume, a cover letter, proof of enrollment or graduation, and evidence of language proficiency.

Application Process

Applicants apply directly through the UNICEF Careers internship portal to specific posted vacancies.

Deadline

Postings open on a rolling basis; check the UNICEF Careers portal regularly for current openings.

Why You Should Apply

For students and graduates interested in child-focused development work, UNICEF's paid internship offers meaningful experience with one of the UN's most recognized agencies.

For full details, visit the official UNICEF Careers internship portal: https://www.unicef.org/careers/internships
