UNICEF Internship Programme

## Introduction The UNICEF Internship Programme places students and recent graduates inside one of the UN's most visible agencies, dedicated to child rights, education, health, and protection worldwide. Compared with several other UN-family internships, UNICEF stands out for offering a monthly stipend rather than running fully unpaid, along with support toward travel and visa costs where funding allows — a meaningful difference for applicants weighing cost against experience. It suits students and graduates with a genuine interest in child-focused development, humanitarian response, or public health work. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** UNICEF - **Country of Study:** Global — UNICEF offices and country programmes worldwide - **Opportunity Type:** Internship - **Funding Type:** Paid - **Degree Level:** Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD - **Duration:** Varies by specific posting, generally a few months - **Official Website:** unicef.org/careers ## Benefits - A monthly stipend paid throughout the internship - A one-time lump-sum contribution toward travel and visa costs, subject to available funding - Visa support documentation from UNICEF - Direct exposure to UNICEF's global programming on child rights, education, health, and protection ## Eligibility Criteria - Be currently enrolled in an undergraduate, graduate, or PhD programme, or have graduated within the past two years - Be proficient in English, French, or Spanish, plus the working language of the specific office you apply to, if different - Meet any additional field-specific or office-specific requirements listed on individual posting pages ## Required Documents - CV/resume - Cover letter tailored to the specific posting - Proof of current enrollment or graduation within the past two years - Evidence of language proficiency where required ## Application Process **Step 1.** Visit the UNICEF Careers internship portal and browse currently open postings by office and field. **Step 2.** Review each posting's specific requirements, since language and background needs vary by office. **Step 3.** Submit your CV and a cover letter tailored to that particular posting through the UNICEF Careers system. **Step 4.** If shortlisted, participate in an interview with the hiring office. **Step 5.** Successful candidates receive an offer specifying duty station, stipend amount, and duration. ## Important Dates UNICEF internship postings open on a rolling basis throughout the year rather than on one fixed annual cycle. Please refer to the official UNICEF Careers portal for current openings and their individual deadlines. ## Selection Criteria Hiring managers assess how well your academic background and language skills match the specific team's mandate, alongside the clarity and relevance of your cover letter to that office's particular focus area, whether child protection, education, health, or another UNICEF priority. ## Tips for Applicants - Check the UNICEF Careers portal regularly, since postings across different country offices open and close continuously - Tailor each application to the specific office and thematic area, since UNICEF's work spans very different fields across its global network - Note that the stipend and travel contribution can vary by duty station and funding availability, so clarify financial details with the hiring office before accepting an offer - Highlight any prior experience with children, education, or humanitarian work explicitly, since it strengthens fit for UNICEF's specific mandate - Apply promptly once you find a strong-fit posting, since popular offices can receive high volumes of applications ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the UNICEF Internship Programme?** Students or recent graduates (within two years) proficient in English, French, or Spanish. **Is the UNICEF internship paid?** Yes, it includes a monthly stipend and, subject to funding, a travel/visa cost contribution. **Where are UNICEF internships based?** UNICEF offices and country programmes around the world. **Do I need a specific degree field?** Requirements vary by posting; UNICEF hires interns across development, health, education, communications, and other fields. **Can I apply straight after graduating?** Yes, graduates within the past two years remain eligible. **How long do UNICEF internships last?** Duration varies by posting, generally a few months. ## Apply Now Browse current openings and apply directly through the official UNICEF Careers internship portal: https://www.unicef.org/careers/internships
