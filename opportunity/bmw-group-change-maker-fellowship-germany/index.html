BMW Group Change Maker Fellowship

Introduction

BMW's engineering culture and ESMT Berlin's business education come together in the Change Maker Fellowship, a partnership that funds MBA and MSc students working at the intersection of technology, mobility, and digital innovation. Rather than a broad general-purpose scholarship, this fellowship is deliberately narrow: it targets STEM-background candidates from a specific list of countries tied to BMW's global operations, reflecting the company's interest in building a talent pipeline connected directly to its own business footprint.

About the Opportunity

The fellowship funds study at ESMT Berlin across three possible tracks: the Full-time MBA, MSc programs, and the Global Online MBA, each with a different level of tuition support. What unites all three tracks is BMW's direct involvement beyond funding, since fellows also gain access to BMW mentorship and networking opportunities, giving them exposure to the company's own professionals alongside their academic coursework.

Benefits

Tuition coverage varies meaningfully by program: the Full-time MBA receives 90 percent coverage, reducing tuition to around EUR 5,000, MSc programs receive 50 percent coverage, bringing tuition to roughly EUR 17,500, and the Global Online MBA receives full, 100 percent coverage. Beyond the tuition support, fellows gain mentorship from BMW professionals and access to the company's networking opportunities, benefits that can carry real weight for graduates interested in careers touching mobility, technology, or digital innovation.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need an undergraduate degree, or current employment, in a mathematics or technology field, since the fellowship is explicitly built around STEM backgrounds. Eligibility is further restricted to citizens or permanent residents of Germany, India, Portugal, Romania, Brazil, China, the United States, or South Africa, or those holding refugee status in Germany, a list that mirrors BMW's major markets and operations. MBA and MSc applicants must also be 30 years old or younger at the time of intake.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The fellowship is aimed squarely at mathematics, technology, IT, and computer science backgrounds, since the underlying MBA and MSc programs at ESMT are designed for candidates bringing technical expertise into a business and innovation context.

Required Documents

Applicants need to complete the standard ESMT program application, a CV, proof of citizenship or residency status, and academic transcripts. A required essay response addressing digital mobility or innovation is also part of the application and carries real weight in how candidates are assessed.

Application Process

Candidates apply directly to their intended ESMT Berlin program, whether the Full-time MBA, an MSc track, or the Global Online MBA, and indicate interest in the Change Maker Fellowship within that same application. The required essay on digital mobility or innovation functions as the fellowship-specific component of an otherwise standard ESMT application, so applicants should treat it as a genuine opportunity to demonstrate their fit with BMW's interests rather than a formality.

Deadline

Deadlines vary depending on which ESMT program an applicant is targeting, since the Full-time MBA, MSc tracks, and Global Online MBA each run on their own admissions calendar. Applicants should confirm the specific deadline for their chosen program directly with ESMT rather than assuming a single fixed date applies across all three tracks.

Why You Should Apply

For STEM-background candidates from one of the fellowship's eligible countries who are interested in the intersection of technology and business, the Change Maker Fellowship offers meaningful tuition support alongside direct access to BMW's own professional network, a combination that few other MBA or MSc scholarships in Europe can match. Because eligibility is genuinely restricted by both academic background and citizenship, candidates should confirm they meet both criteria before investing time in ESMT's application process.

For full program details and current deadlines, visit the official ESMT Berlin Change Maker Fellowship page: https://esmt.berlin/degrees/bmw-change-maker-fellowship
