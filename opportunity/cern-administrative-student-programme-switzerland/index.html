CERN Administrative Student Programme

Introduction

CERN is best known for the Large Hadron Collider and the physics discoveries that make headlines, but running an organization of that scale also requires a substantial administrative operation, and that is exactly where the Administrative Student Programme fits in. Aimed at students in fields like finance, HR, law, and communications rather than physics or engineering, the program gives them a paid placement inside one of the world's most prominent international research organizations, working on the operational side that keeps CERN's scientific work running.

About the Opportunity

Unlike CERN's technical internships, which place STEM students alongside physicists and engineers, the Administrative Student Programme is built for students in administration, communication, finance, HR, law, logistics, or procurement. Students join a specific department for their placement, contributing to real operational work rather than a purely observational role, and gain a rare inside view of how a major international scientific organization actually functions day to day.

Benefits

CERN pays a tax-free monthly allowance of approximately CHF 3,486, a substantial stipend that reflects Switzerland's cost of living and CERN's own compensation standards. Students also receive comprehensive health insurance, a travel allowance, and, where applicable, a family supplement. The program includes 30 days of paid leave, alongside CERN's annual closure period, giving students meaningful time off during their placement in addition to fair compensation.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants must be nationals of a CERN Member or Associate Member State, currently studying one of the eligible administrative fields, and must have completed at least 18 months, or three semesters, of their studies before applying. Students need to remain enrolled full-time throughout the placement, and the program explicitly excludes PhD students and PhD degree holders, reserving it for bachelor's and master's level students. Good working knowledge of English or French, CERN's official languages, is also required.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields include administration, communication, finance, HR, law, logistics, and procurement, covering the operational backbone that supports CERN's scientific mission rather than the research itself.

Required Documents

Applicants need a CV in English or French submitted as a PDF, along with an academic transcript. A letter of recommendation is recommended, though specific requirements can vary depending on the particular listing.

Application Process

Candidates apply online through the CERN Careers portal, which runs on the SmartRecruiters platform, signing in via LinkedIn, SmartProfile, or Indeed to create or access their application. Listings open across different departments on a rolling basis, so applicants should search the portal directly for current Administrative Student Programme openings that match their field and interests, applying to specific department listings rather than a single general application.

Deadline

Because listings open on a rolling basis across CERN's various departments rather than through a single annual cycle, there is no fixed application deadline. Applicants should check the CERN Careers portal regularly and apply to relevant listings as soon as they appear, since popular postings can close once enough qualified candidates apply.

Why You Should Apply

For students in administration, finance, HR, law, or related fields who want international experience with genuinely competitive pay, CERN's Administrative Student Programme offers something relatively rare: a paid placement at a world-renowned scientific organization for students whose skills lie outside physics or engineering. The compensation, health insurance, and generous leave allowance make this a strong option among paid international internships, particularly for students drawn to working inside a large, complex international organization without needing a STEM background to qualify.

For current listings and application details, visit the official CERN Careers portal: https://careers.cern/programmes/administrative-studentship/
