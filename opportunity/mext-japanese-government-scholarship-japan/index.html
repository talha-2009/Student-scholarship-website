MEXT Japanese Government Scholarship

Japan's flagship government scholarship, administered by the Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT), funding international students to study at Japanese universities across undergraduate, Master's, doctoral, and research (non-degree) tracks. Fully covers tuition, entrance and examination fees, and provides a monthly stipend (JPY 117,000–145,000 depending on level and programme), plus economy-class airfare at the start and end of the programme. Open to citizens of countries with which Japan has diplomatic relations; applicants must be under 35 (most tracks), have strong academic records, and meet Japanese or English language requirements depending on their intended programme. Applications are submitted through either the Japanese Embassy/Consulate in the applicant's home country (Embassy Recommendation, typically April–June annually) or through a Japanese university directly (University Recommendation, varies by institution); exact deadlines for the 2027 cohort vary by country and track — applicants should check with their nearest Japanese Embassy or target university for the specific cycle.
