Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program

Introduction

Across Africa, talented students often face a stark gap between their academic potential and their family's ability to finance a university education, especially abroad. The Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program was built specifically to close that gap, funding thousands of African students, including many with refugee or displaced backgrounds, at more than 60 partner universities spanning Africa, North America, and Europe. Rather than operating as a single scholarship with one application, it works through a network of partner institutions, each running its own version of the program, united by a shared mission: educating a new generation of African leaders who are expected, and supported, to give back to the continent after they graduate.

About the Opportunity

The program's partner universities include some of the world's most recognized institutions, among them Cambridge, UC Berkeley, McGill, and Sciences Po, alongside a substantial number of universities across Africa itself. Every Scholar is admitted to a specific undergraduate or graduate program at one of these partner institutions and receives coordinated support that goes well beyond tuition, including structured mentoring, career preparation, and psychosocial support designed to help students navigate the real challenges of studying far from home, often for the first time.

Benefits

Mastercard Foundation Scholars receive full tuition and fees at their host institution, along with accommodation and books for the duration of their degree. The program funds return airfare, addressing a cost that can otherwise put international study out of reach even for academically outstanding students. Beyond the financial coverage, Scholars gain access to structured mentoring relationships, dedicated leadership development programming, career services aimed at post-graduation employment, and psychosocial support intended to help students manage the personal and cultural adjustments that come with studying abroad or far from home.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants must be citizens of an African country, and the program explicitly includes those with refugee or displaced status within Africa, a deliberate choice reflecting the Foundation's focus on reaching students who might otherwise have no realistic path to higher education. Beyond citizenship, the program looks for demonstrated academic talent, real leadership potential, genuine financial need, and a clear, credible commitment to contributing to Africa's development after graduation. Most applicants should be 28 years old or younger, though this age limit extends to 32 for candidates applying under the refugee or disability categories. Because eligibility and selection ultimately run through each partner university's own admissions process, specific academic requirements can vary depending on which institution and program a candidate targets.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The program does not restrict Scholars to particular disciplines. Because it operates through more than 60 partner universities, the effective range of available fields spans nearly everything those institutions offer at undergraduate or graduate level, from engineering and the sciences through business, public policy, and the humanities.

Required Documents

Applicants need a standard university admission application for their chosen partner institution, alongside a separate Mastercard Foundation Scholars application specific to that university's program. Academic transcripts, proof of citizenship, and documentation supporting financial need are typically required, along with a personal statement addressing leadership potential and community impact, a section applicants should treat as seriously as their academic credentials given how central it is to the program's mission.

Application Process

The process begins with choosing a partner institution whose program fits an applicant's academic interests and career goals, since Scholars must first be admitted to that university's degree program in the ordinary way. Once that university application is underway, candidates separately complete that specific institution's Mastercard Foundation Scholars application, which typically asks for financial need documentation and a personal statement on leadership and community impact. Because each partner university manages its own selection process and timeline, applicants need to research their specific target institution's requirements directly rather than assuming a single unified process across the whole network.

Deadline

Because deadlines are set independently by each of the program's 60-plus partner universities, there is no single global date that applies to every applicant. Prospective Scholars should identify their preferred partner institution as early as possible and confirm that university's specific Mastercard Foundation Scholars deadline directly, since application windows can open and close well before a general university application deadline might suggest.

Why You Should Apply

For academically strong African students facing genuine financial barriers to higher education, the Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program offers something rare: full funding at globally recognized universities, paired with structured support designed specifically to help students succeed once they arrive, not just to get them there. Its explicit inclusion of refugee and displaced applicants sets it apart from many international scholarships that overlook this group entirely. For students weighing fully funded scholarships and study abroad options, this program combines prestige, comprehensive support, and a genuine mission-driven commitment to seeing Scholars return home and contribute to Africa's future.

For the current list of partner universities and their specific application details, visit the official Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program page: https://mastercardfdn.org/en/what-we-do/our-programs/mastercard-foundation-scholars-program/where-to-apply/
