Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Degrees

Introduction

While most scholarships fund study at a single university, Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Degrees take a fundamentally different approach: each programme is jointly designed and delivered by a consortium of universities spread across multiple European countries, and students physically study in at least two of them as part of a single combined degree. Funded through the EU's Erasmus+ programme, this structure exists to build genuinely international academic experiences and strengthen collaboration across Europe's higher education system, rather than simply subsidizing study at one institution.

About the Opportunity

Each Erasmus Mundus programme covers its own specific subject area and consortium of partner universities, meaning prospective students first need to identify a specific joint master's programme rather than applying to Erasmus Mundus as a single entity. Once accepted, students move between the consortium's partner institutions according to that specific programme's structure, gaining exposure to different academic cultures, faculty, and resources across the countries involved rather than remaining at a single campus for their entire degree.

Benefits

Scholarship recipients receive full tuition fees for their joint master's programme, a monthly living allowance to cover day-to-day costs, and support for travel and installation costs connected to moving between consortium countries. Insurance coverage is included throughout the programme, addressing a practical need given the multi-country nature of Erasmus Mundus study.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need a recognized first degree, though beyond that baseline, specific admission and language requirements are set independently by each Erasmus Mundus consortium rather than centrally by the EU. Applicants also need to be genuinely willing and able to study in at least two different countries over the course of the programme, since this mobility requirement is built into every joint master's degree rather than optional.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Erasmus Mundus programmes span a genuinely wide range of academic fields, since the catalogue includes joint master's degrees across disciplines from the sciences through the humanities and social sciences, each offered by a different specific consortium.

Required Documents

Applicants typically need degree certificates and transcripts, a CV, a motivation letter, and language proficiency proof, though exact document requirements vary by the specific joint programme and consortium an applicant targets.

Application Process

Prospective students begin by browsing the Erasmus Mundus catalogue to identify a joint master's programme matching their academic interests, since there is no single unified application covering every programme. Applicants then apply directly to the specific consortium of universities offering that programme, following whatever admissions process that consortium has set up, rather than a centralized EU application system.

Deadline

Because each Erasmus Mundus joint programme sets its own deadline independently, there is no single fixed date applicable across the whole catalogue. Prospective applicants should identify their target programme early and confirm that specific consortium's deadline directly.

Why You Should Apply

For graduate students who want a genuinely international academic experience rather than study confined to one country, Erasmus Mundus offers full funding alongside a structure few other scholarships replicate: a single degree built and delivered across multiple countries and universities simultaneously. Among fully funded international scholarships for master's study, its EU backing and genuinely multi-country design make it a strong option for students specifically seeking a cross-border academic and cultural experience as part of their degree.

For the current programme catalogue and application details, visit the official Erasmus Mundus website: https://www.eacea.ec.europa.eu/scholarships/erasmus-mundus-catalogue_en
