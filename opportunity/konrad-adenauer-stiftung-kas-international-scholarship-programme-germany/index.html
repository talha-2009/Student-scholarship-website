Scholarship - Germany

Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung (KAS) International Scholarship Programme

Introduction

Germany's Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung, the political foundation associated with the country's CDU party, funds German and international students and doctoral candidates who combine above-average academic ability with genuine commitment to democratic values and social responsibility. Named after the first chancellor of the Federal Republic of Germany, the foundation sees its scholarship programme as a direct extension of its broader mission to support democratic development, both within Germany and internationally, through the students and researchers it funds.

About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue study at any level, bachelor's, master's, or PhD, at a German institution, while also participating in KAS's programming beyond the classroom, including holiday academies and seminars designed to build both academic and civic skills. The foundation's emphasis on democratic values and social responsibility runs through the whole experience, not just the initial selection process, with scholars expected to engage actively with this dimension of the programme rather than treating it as incidental to their academic funding.

Benefits

Scholars receive a monthly stipend, with the exact amount depending on their financial situation and academic level, alongside participation in KAS holiday academies and seminars, and access to mentoring and the foundation's broader network, which extends into German civic and political life more broadly.

Who Can Apply?

German and international students or doctoral candidates of above-average academic ability, generally no older than 32, committed to democratic values and social responsibility, can apply for either of KAS's two annual intake windows, with the foundation explicitly weighing civic commitment alongside academic performance in its selection.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The scholarship covers all academic fields, with eligibility centered on academic ability and civic engagement rather than a specific discipline, meaning candidates from the sciences, humanities, and social sciences are all genuinely welcome to apply.

Required Documents

Applicants need an application form, a CV, a motivation letter, academic transcripts, and two recommendation letters, one academic and one addressing personal suitability specifically, a distinctive requirement compared with scholarships that ask for two purely academic references.

Application Process

Applicants apply directly to KAS with all required documents by one of two published deadlines, typically 15 January for the summer semester intake or 15 July for the winter semester intake, with the two-window structure giving applicants more flexibility than a single annual deadline would allow.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 15 July 2026, for winter semester intake. Applicants who miss this window should watch for the following January cycle rather than assuming a full year's wait before the next opportunity to apply.

Why You Should Apply

For students at any level who combine strong academics with genuine civic engagement, KAS offers funding plus a distinctive programme of seminars and mentoring built around democratic values, setting it apart from scholarships focused purely on academic merit. Among fully funded scholarships tied to a specific mission, KAS stands out for genuinely integrating its civic focus into the scholar experience itself, not just the selection criteria.

For full details, visit the official KAS scholarships page: https://www.kas.de/en/web/begabtenfoerderung-und-kultur/stipendien-und-foerderung
