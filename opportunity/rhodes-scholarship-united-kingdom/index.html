Rhodes Scholarship

## Introduction

The Rhodes Scholarship is the oldest international postgraduate award in the world, established in 1902 to bring exceptional young people from across the globe to study at the University of Oxford. It is run independently of Oxford by the Rhodes Trust and selects scholars through separate national or regional committees ("constituencies") covering 64 countries. Rhodes is aimed at people who combine strong academic ability with demonstrated character, leadership, and a commitment to using their education in service of others — it is not simply an academic prize, and the selection process reflects that.

## Opportunity Overview

- **Organization:** Rhodes Trust / University of Oxford - **Country of Study:** United Kingdom (University of Oxford only) - **Opportunity Type:** Scholarship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Master's or PhD (any full-time postgraduate degree Oxford offers, with limited restrictions) - **Duration:** 2-3 years depending on the degree - **Official Website:** rhodeshouse.ox.ac.uk

## Benefits

- Full tuition and college fees at the University of Oxford - A maintenance stipend to cover living costs for the full duration of the scholarship - Return flights to and from Oxford at the start and end of your tenure - Access to the Rhodes community — a global network of scholars and alumni across business, academia, government, and civil society - Opportunities to apply for additional Rhodes-funded grants for internships, research, or travel during your time at Oxford

## Eligibility Criteria

- Be a citizen (or otherwise meet residency rules) of an eligible Rhodes constituency — constituencies exist for individual countries and for regional groupings - Have, or be on track to complete, an undergraduate degree of a high standard by the time you would take up the scholarship - Demonstrate the Rhodes selection criteria: energy to use your talents to the full, as demonstrated by achievement in study, activities, or life circumstances overcome; a strong moral character and concern for others; ability to work with and lead others; and commitment to fighting the world's fight through service - If unsuccessful on a first application, you may reapply only once, in the same constituency

Eligibility rules differ slightly by constituency (for example, some regional constituencies group several countries together), so check the constituency list for your country's exact process.

## Required Documents

- Completed Rhodes application form (format varies slightly by constituency) - Personal statement explaining your motivation and plans - Academic transcripts and degree certificates - Multiple letters of recommendation (typically 5-8, addressing different aspects of your character and achievements) - Proof of eligibility for your constituency (citizenship or residency documentation) - In the US, university endorsement is required before a student can proceed to the national competition

## Application Process

**Step 1.** Confirm which Rhodes constituency you should apply through, based on your citizenship or residency.

**Step 2.** If your constituency requires university endorsement (as in the US), complete your institution's internal nomination process first, well ahead of the national deadline.

**Step 3.** Submit your constituency-specific application with personal statement, transcripts, and reference details by the national deadline (typically in the fall).

**Step 4.** Shortlisted candidates are invited to constituency-level interviews, usually in November.

**Step 5.** Final selection is made by each constituency's committee; successful applicants then apply separately to their chosen Oxford course through the standard Oxford graduate admissions process.

## Important Dates

- **Applications typically open:** Summer - **National/constituency deadline:** Early October (varies by country — see the deadline listed here for the general reference date) - **Interviews:** November - **Results announced:** Late November - **Programme start:** The following October at Oxford

Because timing and process vary meaningfully by constituency, always confirm exact dates through your specific constituency's Rhodes page.

## Selection Criteria

Rhodes selection panels weigh four qualities set out by Cecil Rhodes' original will, updated in modern language: literary and scholastic attainments, energy to use one's talents fully, truth, courage, devotion to duty, sympathy for the weak, kindliness and unselfishness, and moral force of character combined with instincts to lead. In practice, this means the panel is looking at your full record — academic results, extracurricular impact, and interview performance — rather than grades alone.

## Tips for Applicants

- Start building your file of recommenders well in advance; Rhodes requires more references than most scholarships, and they should speak to different sides of your record - If your constituency requires university endorsement, learn your institution's internal deadline early — it is usually well before the national deadline - Use your personal statement to show, not just tell — concrete examples of leadership and service carry more weight than descriptions of ambition - Prepare specifically for the interview stage: Rhodes interviews are known for testing how you think under pressure, not just what you know - If unsuccessful, remember you can reapply once in the same constituency — many successful scholars were not selected on their first attempt

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Who can apply for the Rhodes Scholarship?** Citizens or eligible residents of one of the 64 Rhodes constituencies, with a strong undergraduate record.

**Is the Rhodes Scholarship fully funded?** Yes — tuition, fees, a living stipend, and travel to Oxford are all covered.

**Can I choose any course at Oxford?** Most full-time postgraduate courses are eligible, with a small number of restrictions; confirm your intended course is eligible before applying.

**Does the Rhodes Scholarship require a university nomination?** It depends on your constituency — US applicants need institutional endorsement; many other constituencies accept direct applications.

**How many Rhodes Scholars are selected each year?** Around 100 scholars are selected annually across all constituencies worldwide.

**Can I apply for both a Rhodes and other scholarships at the same time?** Yes, many applicants apply to Rhodes alongside other major scholarships such as Marshall or Gates Cambridge.

**What happens if I am not selected?** You may reapply once more in the same constituency if you still meet the eligibility window.
