Rhodes Scholarship

Introduction

Established in 1902, the Rhodes Scholarship is the oldest international postgraduate award in the world, bringing exceptional young people from 64 countries and territories to the University of Oxford for fully funded graduate study. Run independently of Oxford by the Rhodes Trust, it selects scholars through separate national and regional committees rather than a single centralized process, meaning the exact application procedure a candidate follows depends heavily on which of the 64 eligible constituencies they belong to.

About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue two to three years of postgraduate study at Oxford, chosen from nearly any full-time postgraduate course the university offers, while also joining a global Rhodes community that spans well over a century of alumni across business, government, academia, and civil society. Selection panels weigh four historic qualities, literary and scholastic attainments, energy in using one's talents fully, moral force of character, and instincts to lead, evaluating candidates on their full record rather than academic results alone.

Benefits

Scholars receive full tuition and college fees at the University of Oxford, a maintenance stipend covering living costs for the full duration of their scholarship, and flights to and from Oxford at the start and end of their tenure.

Who Can Apply?

Exceptional young people from eligible Rhodes constituencies can apply, demonstrating academic excellence, character, leadership, and a genuine commitment to service; unsuccessful applicants may reapply only once within the same constituency.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Rhodes covers nearly any full-time postgraduate course Oxford offers, giving scholars genuine flexibility across disciplines from the sciences through humanities, law, and public policy.

Required Documents

Applicants need an application form, a personal statement, academic transcripts, recommendation letters, and proof of eligibility for their applicable constituency.

Application Process

Applicants apply through their national or constituency-specific Rhodes selection process, which in the US requires university endorsement before proceeding; nominees are shortlisted and interviewed before final selection is made.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 7 October 2026, though this can vary by specific constituency, so applicants should confirm the exact date for their country or region directly.

Why You Should Apply

For exceptional students from eligible constituencies who want comprehensive funding and entry into one of the world's oldest and most respected postgraduate scholar communities, few awards match Rhodes' combination of prestige, flexibility of study, and lasting global network.

For full eligibility details specific to your constituency, visit the official Rhodes Trust website: https://www.rhodeshouse.ox.ac.uk/scholarships/the-rhodes-scholarship/
