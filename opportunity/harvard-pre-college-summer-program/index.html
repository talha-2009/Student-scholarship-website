Harvard Pre-College Summer Program

## Introduction Rising juniors and seniors looking for a genuine taste of Ivy League academics before they even apply to college have long turned to Harvard's Pre-College Summer Program, which lets high school students take college-level courses on Harvard's campus or fully online, taught by Harvard-affiliated instructors. Unlike scholarship-funded programmes such as MIT's Research Science Institute, this is a fee-based experience open to any qualified applicant who can afford the cost, making it a considerably more accessible route to a Harvard-connected academic experience than competitive, fully funded research programmes with far lower acceptance rates. ## About the Opportunity The programme places students in genuine college-level coursework, either living on Harvard's campus for the residential option or studying remotely through the online format, with courses spanning a wide range of academic subjects taught in a style that mirrors actual undergraduate teaching rather than a simplified high-school-adjacent curriculum. Students choose specific courses aligned with their interests, whether that means exploring a subject they already love in more depth or sampling something entirely new before committing to it at the undergraduate level, and the residential format in particular gives participants a genuine sense of what living and studying on a major university campus actually feels like day to day. ## Benefits Participants gain a college-level academic experience, a Harvard certificate of completion documenting their participation, and, for residential participants, direct exposure to campus life at Harvard, including access to campus facilities and the general rhythm of university life that a typical high school visit would not provide. ## Who Can Apply? High school students, typically rising juniors and seniors, can apply, with no formal minimum GPA published, though admission does require demonstrating readiness for genuinely college-level coursework through the application materials submitted. ## Fields of Study or Available Areas The programme spans a wide multidisciplinary range of subjects, from the sciences and mathematics through humanities, social sciences, and writing-intensive courses, giving students real choice in shaping their own summer academic experience. ## Required Documents Applicants need a transcript, a personal statement explaining their interest in the programme and chosen course, a recommendation letter, and payment of the application fee as part of their submission. ## Application Process Students apply online via Harvard Summer School admissions, submitting their transcript and personal statement alongside their recommendation letter, then, if admitted, pay the full programme fee to confirm their place in either the residential or online track for their chosen course dates. ## Deadline Please refer to the official website for the current year's specific application deadlines, since Harvard Summer School runs its Pre-College admissions on an annual cycle with rolling or fixed deadlines depending on the specific course and session selected. ## Why You Should Apply For students curious about what genuine college-level academic work actually feels like before committing to a full degree programme, Harvard's Pre-College Summer Program offers a structured, credentialed way to explore that experience, whether through immersive residential campus life or a flexible online format, without requiring the extraordinarily competitive academic profile that fully funded research programmes at similar institutions typically demand. For full application details, visit the official Harvard Summer School Pre-College page: https://www.summer.harvard.edu/high-school-programs
