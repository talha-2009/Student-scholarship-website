DAAD WISE Research Internship

Introduction

At India's top engineering and science institutions, DAAD WISE offers a specific, well-worn path into a German research lab for undergraduates still partway through their degree. Unlike DAAD's broader RISE programme open to students from the US, UK, Canada, and Ireland, WISE is built exclusively for Indian nationals at a defined list of partner institutions, and it comes with an unusual twist: applicants need to independently secure an invitation from a German professor before they can even apply.

About the Opportunity

WISE places undergraduates from IITs, NITs, IISERs, IIITs, and other listed Indian institutions into research internships at German public or state institutions, working under the supervision of doctoral researchers on real ongoing projects. Because the professor invitation has to come first, the actual research topic and host lab are effectively chosen by the student through their own outreach, rather than assigned centrally, giving motivated students real influence over where and with whom they end up working.

Benefits

WISE interns receive a monthly stipend to cover living costs in Germany, along with health, accident, and liability insurance. Host institutes provide housing assistance, easing one of the more stressful logistical challenges of a short-term stay abroad, and interns receive mentorship directly from the doctoral researchers they work alongside during their placement.

Who Can Apply?

WISE is open only to Indian nationals enrolled at a selected DAAD WISE partner institution, in their 5th or 6th semester for a standard four-year bachelor's degree, or 5th through 8th semester for an integrated or dual degree, studying engineering, mathematics, or natural sciences. A strong academic record is expected, typically a CGPA of 8.5 or higher, and the defining eligibility hurdle is securing, independently, an invitation from a professor at an eligible German public or state institution before applying.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields cover engineering and the sciences broadly, matching the technical and scientific strengths of WISE's partner Indian institutions and the German host labs they connect with.

Required Documents

Applicants need the online application form, an official transcript, a certificate of enrollment showing their expected graduation date, a motivation letter tailored specifically to each project they apply for, and a reference letter.

Application Process

The real starting point for a WISE application is outreach: identifying and contacting a professor at a German public or state institution whose research interests the student, and securing that professor's agreement to host them. Only once that invitation is secured does a student register on the DAAD RISE database, which also hosts WISE listings, and submit their application with a motivation letter tailored to that specific project, alongside their transcript, enrollment certificate, and reference letter.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 1 November 2026. Because securing a professor's invitation typically takes weeks of back-and-forth communication, students interested in this cycle should begin that outreach well before the deadline, not treat it as a final step before submitting.

Why You Should Apply

For Indian engineering and science undergraduates who want genuine research experience in Germany before completing their degree, WISE offers full funding and a real say in which lab and project they join, provided they're willing to do the legwork of securing a professor's invitation themselves. Among international research internships available to undergraduates specifically, WISE stands out for combining strong funding with the kind of direct professor engagement that often proves valuable well beyond the internship itself.

For full eligibility details and application guidance, visit the official DAAD India page: https://www.daad.in/en/
