Cambridge Summer School Programme

Introduction

For high school and undergraduate students who want to experience Cambridge University's academic atmosphere without going through the university's competitive admissions process, the Cambridge Summer School Programme offers a residential taste of college life and seminar-style teaching, delivered through Cambridge colleges and affiliated providers like Cambridge Immerse. Because it is a fee-based program rather than a scholarship, it is genuinely open to any motivated student aged 16 or older who can cover the cost, making it one of the more accessible ways to spend time studying at Cambridge.

About the Opportunity

The program places students in residential college accommodation for a multi-week summer session, during which they attend university-style seminars in a subject of their choosing, taught in a format designed to resemble genuine Cambridge undergraduate teaching rather than a standard summer camp curriculum. Students live and study alongside peers from around the world, giving them exposure to both the academic style and the social experience of studying at one of the world's oldest universities.

Benefits

Participants gain a genuine university-style teaching experience, including seminar-based learning that mirrors how Cambridge itself teaches undergraduates, along with residential college life during their stay. At the end of the program, students receive a certificate of completion, a credential that, while not equivalent to Cambridge academic credit, documents the experience for future applications or personal records.

Who Can Apply?

The program is open to high school and undergraduate students aged 16 and older who have a genuine academic interest in their chosen subject area. Because this is a fee-based program rather than a competitive scholarship, admission standards are generally less stringent than Cambridge's own undergraduate or graduate admissions, though applicants should still demonstrate real interest and preparation in their application.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The program covers a genuinely multidisciplinary range of subjects, allowing students to choose a track aligned with their own academic interests rather than being restricted to a narrow set of fields.

Required Documents

Applicants need to submit an application form, an academic reference supporting their interest and readiness for the chosen subject, and pay an application fee as part of the process.

Application Process

Students apply online through the Cambridge Institute of Continuing Education or an affiliated program provider such as Cambridge Immerse, choosing their preferred subject track and session dates. Because sessions run at specific points during the summer and can fill up, applying with reasonable lead time gives students a better chance of securing their preferred subject and dates rather than having to settle for whatever remains available closer to the program's start.

Deadline

Deadlines vary depending on the specific summer session and provider a student chooses, so there is no single fixed date that applies across the whole program. Applicants should check the current year's session calendar directly and apply well ahead of their preferred session's specific deadline.

Why You Should Apply

For students curious about what studying at Cambridge actually feels like, this program offers a genuine, if condensed, taste of that experience without requiring the academic credentials Cambridge's own admissions process demands. As a self-funded summer school rather than a scholarship, it rewards students who are ready to invest in the experience itself, making it a strong option for those weighing study abroad opportunities during their high school or early undergraduate years and wanting firsthand exposure to a world-renowned academic environment before committing to a full degree application elsewhere.

For session dates, subject tracks, and application details, visit the official Cambridge Summer Programme page: https://www.ice.cam.ac.uk/course/cambridge-summer-programme
