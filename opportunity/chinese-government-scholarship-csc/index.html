Scholarship - China

Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC)

Introduction

Since 1996, the China Scholarship Council has grown its Chinese Government Scholarship from a few hundred annual awards to more than 60,000 a year, making it one of the largest fully funded international scholarship programmes in the world. Administered on behalf of China's Ministry of Education, the CSC scholarship funds international students at undergraduate, master's, and PhD level across more than 280 designated Chinese universities, though the practical process of applying is genuinely more layered than a single online form, since three distinct funding routes exist side by side.

About the Opportunity

Applicants choose between Type A, the Bilateral or Embassy programme applied for through their home country's Chinese embassy, Type B, the University Programme applied for directly with a CSC-designated Chinese university, or Type C, a provincial government route through cities like Beijing or Shanghai. Type B is generally the more straightforward path for most international applicants, since the university itself runs the academic review while CSC separately handles funding allocation, and PhD applicants in particular benefit from securing a written acceptance letter from a prospective Chinese supervisor well before the application window closes.

Benefits

Scholars receive full or partial tuition depending on their specific scholarship type, accommodation, basic medical insurance, and a monthly living allowance of up to CNY 3,000 to 3,500; some tracks also fund up to a year of preparatory Chinese-language study for students who need it before starting their main programme.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need to be non-Chinese citizens in good physical and mental health, holding a bachelor's degree for master's applications or a master's degree for PhD applications, and meeting the specific university's academic and language requirements; Chinese-medium programmes require HSK proficiency, generally Level 3 for general study and Level 4 for master's or doctoral study, while undergraduate applicants must additionally take the China Scholastic Competency Assessment.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

CSC funds study across essentially all fields offered at its designated Chinese universities, though CSC generally does not cover MBA or MBBS programmes, or courses with unusually high tuition fees.

Required Documents

Applicants need the completed CSC application form, a notarized highest diploma and transcripts, a passport copy, a study plan or research proposal, two recommendation letters for graduate applicants, a pre-admission document or acceptance letter from the target university, and, where applicable, HSK or CSCA test scores.

Application Process

Applicants complete the online CSC application via campuschina.org, selecting their funding type and the agency or university code, then typically apply in parallel directly to their target university, since most routes require a separate university-level application and pre-admission document alongside the CSC submission itself.

Deadline

The CSC application system typically opens in December and closes between January and April depending on the specific university or embassy channel, with C9 League universities generally closing earliest; applicants should confirm the exact date for their chosen route and target university directly.

Why You Should Apply

For students considering study in China, the sheer scale and reach of the CSC scholarship, spanning hundreds of universities and every major degree level, makes it one of the most accessible fully funded options globally, provided applicants carefully navigate the specific funding route and university-level requirements that apply to them.

For full details, visit the official CSC information page: https://www.cucas.cn/china_scholarship/about_chinascholarship
