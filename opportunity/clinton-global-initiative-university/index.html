Clinton Global Initiative University (CGI U)

## Introduction Most conferences ask attendees to listen and network. Clinton Global Initiative University asks something different: every participant arrives with a Commitment to Action, a concrete, original project they intend to carry out to address a specific global challenge. Run by the Clinton Foundation, CGI U brings together student changemakers from around the world not simply to discuss problems, but to commit publicly to doing something about one, with mentorship and networking support that continues well past the event itself. ## About the Opportunity CGI U's defining feature is the Commitment to Action itself. Rather than applying with a resume or personal statement in the traditional sense, prospective participants develop a specific, student-led project addressing a real global challenge, and that project effectively serves as their application. At the annual meeting, participants refine their commitments alongside mentors and fellow student leaders working on entirely different issues, from public health to environmental sustainability to economic development, creating a genuinely cross-cutting community organized around action rather than a single academic discipline. ## Benefits Selected students receive travel and registration funding to attend the annual meeting, removing a cost barrier that might otherwise exclude students without independent financial means. Beyond the funding, participants gain mentorship from experienced leaders and direct networking opportunities with prominent figures across sectors, connections that many participants continue drawing on well after their specific Commitment to Action concludes. ## Who Can Apply? CGI U is open to current undergraduate and graduate students from any country or institution, provided they can develop an original Commitment to Action addressing a genuine global challenge. There is no restriction by academic field, since past commitments have spanned public health, education, environmental sustainability, technology, and many other areas, but applicants do need a project that is specific and genuinely actionable rather than a general statement of concern about a global issue. ## Fields of Study or Available Areas Because participation is organized around a student-led project rather than a course of study, CGI U effectively spans social innovation and policy broadly, welcoming commitments from virtually any field where a student can identify a concrete problem and a realistic plan to address it. ## Required Documents Applicants need to submit their Commitment to Action proposal, enrollment verification confirming their current student status, and a personal statement explaining their motivation and connection to the challenge they are addressing. ## Application Process Prospective participants apply by submitting their Commitment to Action proposal through the CGI U online application, describing the specific challenge they intend to address and their concrete plan for doing so. Because the strength of this proposal is central to selection, applicants benefit from developing a genuinely specific, feasible project rather than a broad statement of intent, since reviewers are assessing both the originality of the idea and whether a student could realistically execute it. ## Deadline CGI U runs on an annual cycle with its own application window ahead of the yearly meeting, though the exact date for the current cycle was not confirmed at the time of writing. Prospective applicants should check the official CGI U website for the current year's specific deadline before beginning their Commitment to Action proposal. ## Why You Should Apply For students who already have an idea for addressing a real problem but lack the platform, funding, or mentorship to develop it further, CGI U offers something genuinely different from a typical academic conference or leadership program: a structure built entirely around turning an idea into action, backed by funding and access to experienced mentors and global leaders. Among youth programs and leadership programs aimed at students with a genuine action orientation, CGI U stands out for treating participants as changemakers from the outset rather than passive attendees. For full details on developing a Commitment to Action and applying, visit the official CGI U website: https://www.clintonfoundation.org/programs/cgi-u/
