National Geographic Explorer Grant

## Introduction The National Geographic Explorer Grant funds individuals and organizations pursuing research, conservation, education, or storytelling projects aligned with the National Geographic Society's mission. Unlike academic scholarships, it has no formal degree requirement at all — what matters is a clearly defined, compelling project proposal, which makes it accessible to independent researchers, conservationists, and storytellers who might not fit the eligibility profile of more traditional academic grants. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** National Geographic Society - **Country of Study:** Global - **Opportunity Type:** Research Grant - **Funding Type:** Partially Funded - **Degree Level:** Open to all - **Duration:** Varies by project scope - **Official Website:** nationalgeographic.org ## Benefits - Grant funding, typically ranging from USD 5,000 to 30,000 - Access to the National Geographic Explorer network - A platform for storytelling connected to your project ## Eligibility Criteria - Be an individual or organization with a clearly defined project proposal - There is no formal degree requirement - Your project should align with National Geographic's mission around science, conservation, education, or storytelling ## Required Documents - A project proposal - A budget plan - CV - Letters of support ## Application Process **Step 1.** Develop a clearly defined project proposal aligned with National Geographic's mission areas. **Step 2.** Prepare a detailed budget plan and timeline for your proposed project. **Step 3.** Gather letters of support relevant to your project. **Step 4.** Submit your online application, including the proposal, budget, timeline, and CV, via the National Geographic Society portal. **Step 5.** The Society reviews applications on a rolling basis against its funding priorities. **Step 6.** Successful applicants receive grant confirmation and access to the Explorer network. ## Important Dates Applications are accepted on a rolling basis rather than a single fixed annual deadline. Please refer to the official National Geographic Society portal for current submission windows. ## Selection Criteria Reviewers assess the clarity and feasibility of your project proposal, the realism of your budget, and how well your work aligns with National Geographic's priorities in science, conservation, education, or storytelling. ## Tips for Applicants - Make your project proposal specific and feasible, rather than broad or overly ambitious, since clarity is explicitly valued in review - Build a realistic, detailed budget plan, since vague or inflated budgets weaken otherwise strong proposals - Since there is no degree requirement, focus on demonstrating real capability and prior relevant experience through your CV and letters of support - Explain clearly how your project connects to National Geographic's specific mission areas - Apply when your project and materials are genuinely ready, since submissions are reviewed on a rolling basis rather than in one annual batch ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the National Geographic Explorer Grant?** Individuals or organizations with a clearly defined project proposal; no formal degree is required. **Is it fully funded?** No, it typically provides partial funding of USD 5,000-30,000, depending on the project. **Do I need an academic degree?** No, there is no formal degree requirement. **What kinds of projects are eligible?** Research, conservation, education, or storytelling projects aligned with National Geographic's mission. **How do I apply?** By submitting a project proposal, budget, timeline, and CV via the online portal. **Is there a fixed deadline?** No, applications are accepted on a rolling basis. ## Apply Now Submit your project proposal through the official National Geographic Society grants portal: https://www.nationalgeographic.org/society/grants-and-investments/
