Teagasc Walsh Scholars Programme

A fully funded postgraduate research programme run by Teagasc (Ireland's Agriculture and Food Development Authority) for PhD and Research Master's students in agriculture, food, environment, climate, and related disciplines. Provides a stipend of €25,000 per year plus tuition fee coverage of up to €6,000 annually, for up to four years (PhD) or two years (Research Master's). Open internationally — graduates from any country may apply — provided they hold a relevant BSc or MSc and secure a place on a specific advertised project supervised jointly by a Teagasc researcher and a partner university academic. Individual project deadlines vary and are listed on the Walsh Scholars opportunities page; new projects are posted throughout the year, with a main annual intake typically announced in June with deadlines in July–September. Check the official opportunities listing for currently open projects.
