Teagasc Walsh Scholars Programme

## Introduction The Teagasc Walsh Scholars Programme funds PhD and Research Master's candidates working on agriculture, food, environment, climate, or rural development topics through Ireland's national agriculture and food research authority. Unlike an open scholarship, it operates around specific advertised research projects with joint supervision between Teagasc and a partner university, meaning your application must be built around a particular listed project rather than a self-designed research proposal. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** Teagasc (Agriculture and Food Development Authority, Ireland) - **Country of Study:** Ireland - **Opportunity Type:** Fellowship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Master's / PhD - **Duration:** Length of the doctoral or research master's programme - **Official Website:** teagasc.ie ## Benefits - An annual stipend of €25,000 - Tuition fees covered up to €6,000 per year - Access to Teagasc research centres and facilities - Structured doctoral training - International collaboration and conference opportunities ## Eligibility Criteria - Be a graduate seeking to pursue a PhD or Research Master's in agriculture, food, environment, climate, or rural development topics - Apply to a specific advertised project with joint Teagasc/university supervision ## Required Documents - Application form - CV - Academic transcripts - A cover letter/statement of interest tied to the specific project - References ## Application Process **Step 1.** Browse currently advertised Walsh Scholars projects on the Teagasc website to find one matching your academic background and interests. **Step 2.** Prepare your CV, transcripts, and a cover letter specific to the advertised project. **Step 3.** Submit your application directly for that specific project via the Teagasc website by the published deadline. **Step 4.** Teagasc and the partner university jointly review applications for fit with the specific project. **Step 5.** Successful candidates begin their PhD or Research Master's with joint Teagasc/university supervision. ## Important Dates Application deadlines are set per advertised project rather than one fixed annual date, typically around March for a September start. Please refer to the official Teagasc website for current project listings and their specific deadlines. ## Selection Criteria Selection is based on academic fit with the specific advertised project's research area, the strength of your cover letter in demonstrating genuine interest and relevant background, and your overall academic record as shown in transcripts and references. ## Tips for Applicants - Browse current Walsh Scholars project listings regularly, since applications are tied to specific advertised projects, not an open general call - Tailor your cover letter specifically to the advertised project's research focus, referencing the actual project description - Apply around the typical March deadline if targeting a September start, though always confirm the specific project's exact date - Highlight any prior coursework or research experience directly relevant to agriculture, food, environment, climate, or rural development - Consider multiple current project listings if more than one fits your background, since selection is project-specific ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the Teagasc Walsh Scholars Programme?** Graduates seeking a PhD or Research Master's in agriculture, food, environment, climate, or rural development. **Is it fully funded?** Yes — an annual stipend of €25,000 plus tuition coverage up to €6,000 per year. **Do I design my own research proposal?** No, you apply to a specific advertised project with joint Teagasc/university supervision. **When is the deadline?** Typically around March for a September start, but this varies by specific project. **Where will I be based?** At Teagasc research centres and a partner university, depending on the specific project. **Can I apply to more than one project?** Yes, if multiple current listings fit your academic background. ## Apply Now Browse current project listings and apply through the official Teagasc Walsh Scholars page: https://www.teagasc.ie/about/research-innovation/the-walsh-scholars-programme/
