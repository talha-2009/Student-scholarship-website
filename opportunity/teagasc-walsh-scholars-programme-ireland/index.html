Teagasc Walsh Scholars Programme

Introduction

Ireland's Teagasc, its national Agriculture and Food Development Authority, runs the Walsh Scholars Programme to fund PhD and Research Master's candidates working on agriculture, food, environment, climate, or rural development topics, structured around specific advertised projects rather than an open scholarship. Because every Walsh Scholars position comes with joint Teagasc and university supervision already arranged, candidates apply directly to a defined project rather than proposing entirely independent research.

About the Opportunity

Scholars conduct doctoral or research master's work through a specific advertised project, gaining access to Teagasc's substantial research infrastructure while also benefiting from structured doctoral training and international collaboration opportunities. Because Teagasc is Ireland's national authority for agriculture and food research, scholars work within an institution directly connected to national policy and industry, not solely an academic department.

Benefits

Scholars receive an annual stipend of EUR 25,000, tuition fees covered up to EUR 6,000 per year, access to Teagasc research centres and facilities, structured doctoral training, and international collaboration and conference opportunities.

Who Can Apply?

Graduates seeking a PhD or Research Master's in agriculture, food, environment, climate, or rural development topics can apply, provided they apply to a specific advertised project with joint Teagasc and university supervision.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields include agriculture, food science, environmental science, climate science, and rural development.

Required Documents

Applicants need an application form, a CV, academic transcripts, a cover letter or statement of interest tied to the specific advertised project, and references.

Application Process

Applicants apply directly to a specific advertised Walsh Scholars project via the Teagasc website by the published deadline, typically around March for a September start.

Deadline

Application deadlines are set per advertised project rather than a single fixed date; interested candidates should check the official Teagasc website for current project listings and their specific deadlines.

Why You Should Apply

For graduates interested in agriculture, food, or environmental research with real policy and industry connections, the Walsh Scholars Programme offers strong funding and structured support through Ireland's national agricultural research authority.

For full details, visit the official Teagasc Walsh Scholars page: https://www.teagasc.ie/about/research-innovation/the-walsh-scholars-programme/
