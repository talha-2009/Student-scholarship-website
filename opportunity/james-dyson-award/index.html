James Dyson Award

Introduction

James Dyson built his engineering reputation on relentless prototyping, famously testing thousands of iterations before landing on a working design, and the award bearing his name reflects that philosophy directly: it rewards student and recent graduate inventors who have built genuine, working solutions to real-world problems, not just compelling ideas sketched on paper. Run by the James Dyson Foundation and judged in its final round by Dyson himself, the competition has become one of the most recognized global platforms for young engineers and designers to showcase original invention, spanning dozens of participating countries each year.

About the Opportunity

Entrants submit an invention addressing a genuine problem, complete with technical documentation and a short video demonstrating how it actually works in practice rather than merely describing the concept. National panels first select country-level winners from among that country's submissions, and those national winners then advance to international judging, where James Dyson personally reviews the shortlist alongside a panel of engineers to select the global winner, giving the competition a level of direct founder involvement rare among engineering awards of this scale and prestige. Past winning inventions have ranged from medical devices to environmental technologies to accessibility solutions, reflecting the competition's genuine openness to any field where engineering ingenuity can solve a real problem.

Benefits

The international winner receives GBP 30,000, while national winners in each participating country receive GBP 5,000, alongside significant global exposure for their invention through the competition's platform, media coverage, and connections within engineering and design industries that often follow a strong showing.

Who Can Apply?

Current university students or graduates within four years of graduation, studying design, engineering, or a science-related field, can enter, provided they can document a genuine invention rather than a purely conceptual idea still in early development.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

The competition welcomes entries from engineering and design broadly, spanning mechanical invention, product design, medical technology, and other technology-driven solutions to real-world problems across virtually any application area imaginable.

Required Documents

Entrants need a clear project description explaining the problem and solution, technical drawings or images documenting how the invention actually works mechanically, and a one-minute video demonstrating the invention in genuine action.

Application Process

Entrants submit their invention online via the official award website, including their project description, technical materials, and demonstration video. National panels review entries within each participating country first, with national winners then advancing to the final international round for review by James Dyson and his engineering panel.

Deadline

The current entry deadline is 15 July 2026. Given the level of documentation required, including a working demonstration video that genuinely shows the invention functioning, entrants should begin preparing their submission well ahead of this date rather than assembling materials in the final days.

Why You Should Apply

For student and recent graduate inventors who have built a genuine working solution to a real problem, few competitions offer this combination of substantial prize money, direct evaluation by a world-renowned engineer, and international media exposure. Among global design and engineering competitions specifically, the James Dyson Award stands out for rewarding demonstrated function over polished concept alone, rewarding the kind of persistent, iterative engineering work that produces genuinely working prototypes rather than compelling pitches.

For full entry details, visit the official James Dyson Award website: https://www.jamesdysonaward.org/en-US/
