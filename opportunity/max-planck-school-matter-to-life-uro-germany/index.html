Max Planck School Matter to Life URO

## Introduction The Max Planck School Matter to Life URO (Undergraduate Research Opportunity) internship gives bachelor's students in physics, chemistry, biology, informatics, materials science, or engineering a funded ten-week placement in Germany, connected to the Max Planck School Matter to Life's broader mission of studying how living systems emerge from non-living matter. It is genuinely selective on academic performance, requiring a specific German-scale GPA threshold, and asks applicants to choose a preferred host lab rather than being assigned one centrally. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** Max Planck School Matter to Life - **Country of Study:** Germany - **Opportunity Type:** Internship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Undergraduate - **Duration:** 10 weeks - **Official Website:** mattertolife.maxplanckschools.org ## Benefits - Covered travel to and from your home country - Accommodation for the duration of the internship - A living allowance ## Eligibility Criteria - Be an undergraduate (bachelor's) student in physics, chemistry, biology, informatics, materials science, engineering, or a related field - Hold a GPA of at least 1.7 on the German grading scale (roughly equivalent to a strong upper-second/first-class average) - Remain enrolled as a student both before and throughout the internship ## Required Documents - Online application - A personal statement highlighting your research interest or experience - Academic transcripts - CV ## Application Process **Step 1.** Review the list of participating host labs within the Matter to Life network and identify your preferred fit. **Step 2.** Complete the online application via the Max Planck Schools portal, which opens 1 August and closes 1 October. **Step 3.** Submit your personal statement, transcripts, and CV, and indicate your preferred host lab. **Step 4.** The programme reviews applications against the GPA threshold and research fit with available labs. **Step 5.** Successful applicants are matched with a host lab and receive confirmation of their ten-week internship dates. ## Important Dates - **Application window:** 1 August to 1 October - **Internship period:** Typically scheduled following selection, often in the following months Always confirm the exact current-year application window on the official portal, since it opens and closes on a set annual schedule. ## Selection Criteria Selection is based primarily on meeting the GPA 1.7 threshold, the strength of your personal statement in demonstrating genuine research interest or prior experience, and fit with your preferred host lab's specific research focus within the Matter to Life network. ## Tips for Applicants - Apply within the 1 August-1 October window strictly, since applications outside this period are not accepted - Research your preferred host lab's specific work in advance and reference it directly in your personal statement - Make sure your GPA genuinely meets the 1.7 German-scale threshold before applying, since this is a firm requirement - Highlight any prior lab or research experience clearly, even if limited, since the personal statement is explicitly assessed for this - Apply as early as possible within the window, since popular host labs can be competitive ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the Matter to Life URO internship?** Undergraduate students in physics, chemistry, biology, informatics, materials science, or engineering meeting the GPA 1.7 threshold. **Is it fully funded?** Yes — travel, accommodation, and a living allowance are all covered. **How long does the internship last?** Ten weeks. **When can I apply?** Only during the annual window, 1 August to 1 October. **Do I choose my host lab?** Yes, you indicate a preferred host lab as part of your application. **Is German language required?** Not specified as a requirement; the research environment is typically international. ## Apply Now Apply during the 1 August-1 October window through the official Max Planck Schools portal: https://mattertolife.maxplanckschools.org/uro-research-internship
