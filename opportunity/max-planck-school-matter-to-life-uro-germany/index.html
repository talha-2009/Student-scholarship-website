Internship - Germany

Max Planck School Matter to Life URO

Introduction

Most undergraduate research internships ask students to shadow a lab for a summer. The Max Planck School Matter to Life's Undergraduate Research Opportunity internship asks something more demanding: it places bachelor's students directly into ten weeks of hands-on research connected to one of the most conceptually ambitious questions in modern science, how living systems emerge from non-living matter. Run by the Max Planck School Matter to Life, a network spanning multiple Max Planck Institutes and partner universities across Germany, the internship is genuinely selective, but for the right student it offers a level of research immersion rarely available at the undergraduate stage.

About the Opportunity

Participants choose a preferred host lab from among the network's participating institutes, working alongside doctoral researchers and senior scientists on projects spanning physics, chemistry, biology, informatics, materials science, and engineering, all connected by the broader Matter to Life research theme. Rather than being assigned a lab centrally, students actively select their preferred placement as part of the application, giving motivated applicants real influence over which specific research group and topic they end up joining for the ten-week period.

Benefits

The internship covers travel to and from the student's home country, accommodation for the full ten weeks, and a living allowance intended to cover day-to-day costs throughout the placement, removing the usual financial barriers that make international research internships difficult for undergraduates without independent means.

Who Can Apply?

Undergraduate bachelor's students in physics, chemistry, biology, informatics, materials science, engineering, or related fields can apply, provided they hold a GPA of at least 1.7 on the German grading scale and remain enrolled as students both before and throughout the internship period.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields span the full range of disciplines connected to the Matter to Life research theme: physics, chemistry, biology, informatics, materials science, and engineering, reflecting the genuinely interdisciplinary nature of research into how life emerges from non-living systems.

Required Documents

Applicants need to submit an online application, a personal statement highlighting their research interest or prior experience, academic transcripts, and a CV.

Application Process

Applicants apply online via the Max Planck Schools portal during the annual application window, which opens 1 August and closes 1 October, selecting the Matter to Life URO program and indicating a preferred host lab as part of their submission.

Deadline

Applications are accepted during the annual window from 1 August to 1 October; interested students should confirm the current year's exact dates on the official portal before preparing their materials.

Why You Should Apply

For strong undergraduate science students who want genuine, hands-on research experience at the interdisciplinary frontier connecting physics, chemistry, and biology, this internship offers funded access to some of Germany's leading research institutes at a stage in their academic career when such placements are usually much harder to secure.

For full details and current host lab listings, visit the official Max Planck Schools portal: https://mattertolife.maxplanckschools.org/uro-research-internship
