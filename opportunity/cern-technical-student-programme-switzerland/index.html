CERN Technical Student Programme

## Introduction Alongside its Administrative Student Programme for operational fields, CERN runs a parallel Technical Student Programme for undergraduates and master's students in physics, engineering, computer science, and mathematics, placing them directly on teams supporting the research infrastructure behind discoveries like the Higgs boson. For STEM students who want hands-on experience at one of the world's most advanced scientific facilities, this program offers a paid route into that environment well before completing a doctorate. ## About the Opportunity Technical Students join specific engineering or physics-support teams at CERN, contributing to the detectors, computing systems, and infrastructure that make CERN's research possible, rather than working directly on theoretical physics research itself. The placement gives students genuine responsibility within a working technical team, exposing them to the scale and precision required to support experiments at the frontier of particle physics. ## Benefits CERN provides a tax-free monthly allowance comparable to approximately CHF 3,486, alongside comprehensive health insurance and a travel allowance. Where applicable, a family supplement is also available, and students receive paid leave during their placement, reflecting CERN's standard compensation structure for its student programs. ## Who Can Apply? Applicants must be nationals of a CERN Member or Associate Member State, currently studying a STEM or engineering field such as physics, engineering, or computing, and must have completed at least 18 months, or three semesters, of their studies. Students need to remain enrolled full-time throughout the placement and should have a good working knowledge of English or French. ## Fields of Study or Available Areas Eligible fields include applied physics, engineering, computer science, and mathematics, covering the technical disciplines that support CERN's experimental infrastructure and computing systems. ## Required Documents Applicants need a CV in English or French submitted as a PDF, an academic transcript, and, where possible, a letter of recommendation, which is recommended though not always strictly required. ## Application Process Candidates apply online through the CERN Careers portal, built on the SmartRecruiters platform, searching specifically for Technical Student Programme listings that match their field. Because postings are tied to specific technical teams and open on a rolling basis, applicants benefit from checking the portal regularly and applying to listings that align closely with their particular area of study, whether that is detector physics, software engineering, or another technical specialty. ## Deadline There is no single fixed annual deadline, since Technical Student Programme listings open across different teams throughout the year. Applicants should monitor the CERN Careers portal directly and apply promptly to relevant openings as they appear. ## Why You Should Apply For physics, engineering, computer science, or mathematics students who want practical experience at a facility operating at the true edge of scientific capability, few paid internships offer comparable exposure to large-scale experimental infrastructure. The combination of a strong stipend, health insurance, and genuine technical responsibility makes this program a standout choice among international internships for STEM students still completing their bachelor's or master's degree. For current listings and application details, visit the official CERN Careers portal: https://careers.cern/programmes/technical-studentship/
