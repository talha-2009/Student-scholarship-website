Erasmus+ Youth Exchange

Introduction

Since the Erasmus+ programme's founding, the European Union has funded thousands of short, intensive cultural exchanges between young people across countries, and Youth Exchanges remain one of its most accessible formats. Rather than requiring individual young people to navigate EU funding bureaucracy themselves, the programme works entirely through registered youth organizations, which handle the funding application and logistics on participants' behalf.

About the Opportunity

A Youth Exchange typically brings together groups of young people from different European or partner countries for a short, structured programme built around a shared theme, ranging from environmental sustainability to intercultural dialogue to civic participation. Because the format is non-formal education rather than a classroom setting, exchanges rely heavily on group activities, workshops, and shared living arrangements to build both the thematic learning and the cross-cultural connections that are central to the programme's purpose.

Benefits

Participation is fully funded, with the sending youth organization arranging full coverage of travel, accommodation, and activity costs. This structure means young people can participate without needing to cover any costs themselves, provided they connect with a participating youth organization willing to include them in an upcoming exchange.

Who Can Apply?

Any young person aged 13 to 30 can participate, typically by applying through a registered youth organization in their country rather than directly to Erasmus+ itself. Because youth organizations manage the actual participant selection for their specific exchanges, requirements can vary somewhat depending on which organization and exchange a young person connects with.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Youth Exchanges center on non-formal education and youth work broadly, with specific thematic focuses varying by exchange, spanning topics like environmental issues, civic engagement, arts and culture, and social inclusion.

Required Documents

Participants need registration through their sending youth organization, participant consent forms for those under 18, and an activity plan outlining their intended engagement with the exchange.

Application Process

Prospective participants need to connect with a youth organization in their country that is registered with a National Erasmus+ Agency and that runs Youth Exchange activities, since individual applications directly to the EU are not how this programme operates. Once connected with a suitable organization, that organization handles the funding application and logistics on behalf of its group of participants, submitting the required activity plan and other documentation to the relevant National Erasmus+ Agency.

Deadline

Because Youth Exchanges are organized by individual youth organizations on a rolling basis rather than a single EU-wide cycle, there is no fixed deadline. Interested young people should reach out to a local youth organization directly to learn about upcoming exchange opportunities and their specific timelines.

Why You Should Apply

For young people who want a genuinely funded, structured introduction to international, cross-cultural collaboration, Youth Exchanges offer a low-barrier entry point that covers travel, accommodation, and activities entirely. As one of the more accessible youth programs and exchange programs available in Europe, the key practical step is simply finding and connecting with an active youth organization in your area, since that relationship, not a direct EU application, is what actually opens the door to participation.

For general programme details and to find a participating organization, visit the official Erasmus+ Youth Exchanges page: https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/programme-guide/part-b/key-action-1/youth-exchanges
