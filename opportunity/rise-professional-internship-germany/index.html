Internship - Germany

RISE Professional Internship

Introduction

While RISE Germany serves undergraduates, DAAD's RISE Professional programme is built for graduate students and PhD candidates, placing them at German industrial companies and research institutes with a clear industry orientation rather than purely academic labs. This distinction matters: RISE Professional interns get direct exposure to how German industry applies research in practice, complete with logistical support, including a DAAD-arranged work-permit waiver, that graduate-level international interns often struggle to arrange independently.

About the Opportunity

Interns work at a German industrial company or research institute on a project connected to their graduate research or professional interests, gaining insight into applied, industry-focused research quite different from the purely academic environment many graduate students are used to. Because placements must begin on or after May 15 each year, students can plan their internship timing around their existing academic calendar with some flexibility, provided they respect this minimum start date.

Benefits

Interns receive a monthly stipend to cover living costs, health, accident, and liability insurance, a work-permit waiver arranged directly by DAAD, and partial reimbursement of German language course fees.

Who Can Apply?

Graduate students and PhD candidates from the US, Canada, UK, or Ireland seeking a research internship at a German industrial company or research institute with an industry focus can apply.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields include engineering, natural sciences, and related technical disciplines connected to industrial research applications.

Required Documents

Applicants need an online application form, an official transcript, a certificate of enrollment, a motivation letter tailored to each project, and a reference letter with official letterhead and signature.

Application Process

Applicants apply online via the RISE Professional internship database to specific listed positions, noting that internships must begin on or after May 15 of the relevant year.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 30 November 2026.

Why You Should Apply

For graduate students and PhD candidates who want applied, industry-focused research experience in Germany, RISE Professional offers comprehensive funding plus practical support around work permits and language training that make an international industry placement genuinely manageable.

For full details, visit the official RISE Professional database: https://www.daad.de/rise/en/rise-professional/find-an-internship/
