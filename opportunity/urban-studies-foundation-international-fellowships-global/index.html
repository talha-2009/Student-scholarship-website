Urban Studies Foundation International Fellowships

Introduction

The Urban Studies Foundation International Fellowship funds early-to-mid-career urban scholars from the Global South to spend a visiting sabbatical at a host university of their choice, specifically targeting researchers who have already completed data collection and are ready to focus on writing and analysis. Its eligibility is unusually precise: applicants must be nationals of, and formally affiliated with, a research organization in an OECD DAC ODA-recipient country.

About the Opportunity

Fellows secure a host institution and academic mentor before applying, then spend three to nine months at that host institution focused on writing and analysis rather than fresh data collection, with the Foundation providing a small mentor-engagement budget to support that working relationship throughout the fellowship.

Benefits

Fellows receive funding for a 3-9 month visiting sabbatical, covering accommodation and subsistence, return travel, research costs, and a small mentor-engagement budget; supplemental grants are available for caregiving or disability needs.

Who Can Apply?

Early-to-mid-career urban scholars holding a PhD awarded within the preceding 10 years, who are nationals of and formally affiliated with a research organization in an OECD DAC ODA-recipient country, can apply, provided they have already completed primary data collection and are not previous USF fellows.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields include urban studies broadly: urban planning, geography, sociology, and development studies with an urban focus.

Required Documents

Applicants need a CV (maximum 3 pages), a research proposal, a budget with evidence for costs over GBP 500, proof of nationality, two academic referee contacts, and their mentor's short CV and supporting letter.

Application Process

Applicants secure a host institution and academic mentor in advance, then submit the online application, with a template available for offline preparation, by the advertised annual deadline.

Deadline

Please refer to the official website for the current cycle's specific deadline, since the most recent published deadline has passed and the next cycle's date was not yet confirmed at the time of writing.

Why You Should Apply

For Global South urban scholars ready to focus on writing and analysis, this fellowship offers a genuinely funded sabbatical with mentor support, rare among grants targeting this specific career stage and region.

For full details, visit the official Urban Studies Foundation grants page: https://www.urbanstudiesfoundation.org/grants-fellowships/
