Urban Studies Foundation International Fellowships

## Introduction The Urban Studies Foundation International Fellowship funds early-to-mid-career urban scholars from the Global South to spend a visiting sabbatical at a host university of their choice, specifically targeting researchers who already have completed data collection and are ready to focus on writing and analysis. Its eligibility is unusually precise: applicants must be nationals of, and formally affiliated with, a research organization in an OECD DAC ODA-recipient country, reflecting the Foundation's specific mission of supporting urban scholarship from historically underfunded regions. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** Urban Studies Foundation (USF) - **Country of Study:** Global — host university of the applicant's choice - **Opportunity Type:** Fellowship - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** Postdoctoral / Early-to-Mid Career Researcher - **Duration:** 3-9 months - **Official Website:** urbanstudiesfoundation.org ## Benefits - Funding for a 3-9 month visiting sabbatical at a host university of your choice - Coverage of accommodation and subsistence - Return travel - Research costs - A small mentor-engagement budget - Supplemental grants available for caregiving or disability needs ## Eligibility Criteria - Hold a PhD awarded within the preceding 10 years - Be a national of, and formally affiliated with, a research organization in an OECD DAC ODA-recipient country (broadly, the Global South) - Have already completed primary data collection/analysis for your research - Not have held a USF fellowship previously ## Required Documents - Applicant CV (maximum 3 pages) - A research proposal - A budget with evidence for costs over GBP 500 - Proof of nationality - Two academic referee contacts - Your mentor's short CV and a supporting letter ## Application Process **Step 1.** Secure a host institution and an academic mentor willing to support your visiting sabbatical in advance. **Step 2.** Develop your research proposal, ensuring it reflects work where primary data collection is already complete. **Step 3.** Prepare your CV, budget with supporting evidence, and proof of nationality. **Step 4.** Arrange your two academic referees and your mentor's short CV and supporting letter. **Step 5.** Submit the complete online application (a template is available for offline preparation) by the advertised annual deadline. **Step 6.** Successful fellows begin their 3-9 month sabbatical at the chosen host institution. ## Important Dates The Urban Studies Foundation runs one advertised annual deadline; please refer to the official website for the current cycle's specific date, since the most recent published deadline has now passed and the next cycle's date was not yet confirmed at the time of writing. ## Selection Criteria USF assesses the strength and specificity of your research proposal, the genuine engagement and support of your prospective host institution and mentor, and confirmation that you meet the specific nationality, career-stage, and research-progress eligibility criteria. ## Tips for Applicants - Secure your host institution and mentor well before the deadline, since both a mentor's CV and supporting letter are required documents - Make sure your research is genuinely at the writing/analysis stage, since primary data collection must already be complete to qualify - Prepare a detailed, evidence-backed budget for any costs over GBP 500 - Confirm your specific home country is currently on the OECD DAC ODA-recipient list before applying - Check the official website regularly for the next cycle's deadline, since the most recent one has passed ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the Urban Studies Foundation Fellowship?** Early-to-mid-career urban scholars from OECD DAC ODA-recipient countries with a PhD from the last 10 years. **Is it fully funded?** Yes — accommodation, subsistence, travel, research costs, and mentor engagement are covered. **How long is the sabbatical?** 3 to 9 months. **Do I need a host institution before applying?** Yes, securing a host and mentor is required as part of the application. **Can I apply if my data collection isn't finished?** No, primary data collection/analysis must already be complete. **Can previous USF fellows reapply?** No, prior USF fellows are not eligible. ## Apply Now Secure your host institution and mentor, and review current deadlines on the official Urban Studies Foundation grants page: https://www.urbanstudiesfoundation.org/grants-fellowships/
