Scholarship - New Zealand

Manaaki New Zealand Scholarships

Introduction

Funded through the New Zealand Aid Programme and administered by Education New Zealand in partnership with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade, Manaaki New Zealand Scholarships fund students from more than 80 partner countries across Africa, Asia, and the Pacific to study full-time in New Zealand. The name itself, manaaki, reflects the programme's intent: to host, support, and honour scholars while building lasting connections between their home countries and New Zealand, not simply subsidise a degree.

About the Opportunity

Scholars pursue full-time undergraduate, postgraduate, or diploma study at one of eight New Zealand universities or three institutes of technology, or, for eligible Pacific citizens, at a partner university in the Pacific itself. New Zealand recommends specific subjects for each eligible country tied to that country's particular social and economic development needs, and scholars are expected to return home afterward to apply what they've learned, with the programme deliberately capping PhD-level awards at a maximum of 10 percent of scholarships granted each year in favour of master's-level study.

Benefits

Scholars receive full tuition fees, a living stipend of NZD 615 per week for those studying in New Zealand, an establishment allowance of NZD 3,000 to help with setup costs, return airfare, health and medical insurance, and, for research students, additional support for research and thesis-related costs.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants need to be citizens of an eligible partner country, at least 18 years old when the scholarship begins, with postgraduate applicants requiring at least one year of full-time or two years of part-time relevant work experience; applicants must also demonstrate genuine motivation to return home and contribute to their country's development for at least two years after finishing.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Eligible fields vary by country and are tied to each partner country's specific development priorities, commonly including agriculture, environment, renewable energy, disaster risk management, public policy, health, and education.

Required Documents

Applicants need a completed online application with their eligibility code, academic transcripts and completion certificates, evidence of relevant work experience for postgraduate applicants, and, once shortlisted, verified English-language test scores from IELTS, TOEFL, PTE Academic, or Cambridge Academic English.

Application Process

Applicants first complete an online eligibility questionnaire to receive an eligibility code, then register a Manaaki account and submit their full application indicating preferred institutions and courses; notably, applicants do not need an actual university admission offer before applying, only confirmation they are likely to meet entry requirements, with formal university applications only required once notified as a preferred candidate.

Deadline

The current application deadline is 31 March 2027, though most partner countries' application windows open in February or March each year and can close early if application volume is high, so applying as soon as the window opens is strongly recommended.

Why You Should Apply

For students from eligible developing countries in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific, Manaaki offers genuinely comprehensive funding and the flexibility of applying before securing university admission, though the process is highly competitive, drawing over 15,000 applications annually, making early, complete submissions essential.

For full country-specific eligibility and deadlines, visit the official Manaaki New Zealand Scholarships website: https://www.nzscholarships.govt.nz/
