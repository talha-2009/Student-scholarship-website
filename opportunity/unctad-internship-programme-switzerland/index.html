Internship - Switzerland

UNCTAD Internship Programme

Introduction

Headquartered at the Palais des Nations in Geneva, UNCTAD, the United Nations Conference on Trade and Development, runs an internship programme placing students inside the UN body focused on trade, investment, and development policy. Like several other UN-family placements, it is unpaid, but interns gain something distinctive in return: a UN grounds pass and library access at one of the UN's major diplomatic hubs, alongside genuine exposure to international trade and development policy work.

About the Opportunity

Interns support research, policy analysis, and administrative work connected to UNCTAD's trade and development mandate, working within a specific division whose focus, whether trade negotiations, investment, or development finance, shapes the actual day-to-day content of the internship. Because Geneva hosts many UN agencies and diplomatic missions, interns based there also gain informal exposure to the broader multilateral diplomatic environment surrounding UNCTAD's own work.

Benefits

Interns gain exposure to UNCTAD's trade and development work, a UN grounds pass, and library access; the internship is unpaid, with travel, accommodation, and insurance the intern's own responsibility.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants enrolled in, or having completed within the past year, a graduate programme or the final year of a bachelor's degree, with a strong command of English or French, can apply.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Relevant fields include economics, trade, development, law, and international relations.

Required Documents

Applicants need an Inspira profile, a cover letter, proof of enrollment or graduation, and proof of health insurance.

Application Process

Applicants apply directly via the UN Careers portal to UNCTAD-specific internship postings using an Inspira profile.

Deadline

Postings open on a rolling basis; interested applicants should check the UN Careers portal regularly.

Why You Should Apply

For students interested in international trade and development policy, an unpaid UNCTAD internship in Geneva offers direct exposure to multilateral policy work and UN diplomatic infrastructure that few other placements can match.

For full details, visit the official UN Careers portal: https://unctad.org/page/internship-programme
