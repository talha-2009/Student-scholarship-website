UNCTAD Internship Programme

## Introduction The UNCTAD Internship Programme places students inside the United Nations Conference on Trade and Development, the UN body focused on trade, investment, and development policy, headquartered at the Palais des Nations in Geneva. Like several other UN-family placements, it is unpaid, but it offers something distinctive in return: a UN grounds pass and library access at one of the UN's major diplomatic hubs, alongside genuine exposure to international trade and development policy work. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD) - **Country of Study:** Switzerland (Geneva) - **Opportunity Type:** Internship - **Funding Type:** Unpaid - **Degree Level:** Bachelor's (final year) or Master's - **Duration:** Varies by posting, generally a few months - **Official Website:** unctad.org ## Benefits - Direct exposure to UNCTAD's trade and development policy work - A UN grounds pass and access to UN library resources in Geneva - Professional experience within a major international organization - Unpaid — travel, accommodation, and insurance costs are the intern's own responsibility ## Eligibility Criteria - Be currently enrolled in a graduate programme, or in the final year of a bachelor's degree, or have completed either within the past year - Have a strong command of English or French, UNCTAD's primary working languages - Meet any additional field-specific requirements listed on the individual posting ## Required Documents - Inspira profile (the shared UN careers platform) - Cover letter tailored to the specific posting - Proof of enrollment or graduation - Proof of health insurance ## Application Process **Step 1.** Create or update your Inspira profile via the UN Careers portal. **Step 2.** Search for UNCTAD-specific internship postings within Inspira. **Step 3.** Submit a tailored cover letter and your proof of enrollment/graduation and health insurance for the specific posting. **Step 4.** If shortlisted, you may be interviewed by the relevant UNCTAD division. **Step 5.** Successful candidates receive an offer confirming their division, start date, and duration in Geneva. ## Important Dates UNCTAD internship postings open on a rolling basis rather than a single annual cycle. Please refer to the official UN Careers portal for currently open UNCTAD postings and their specific deadlines. ## Selection Criteria UNCTAD hiring divisions look for a clear fit between your academic background — often in economics, international trade, law, or development studies — and the specific division's policy focus, along with strong written communication in English or French. ## Tips for Applicants - Search Inspira specifically for UNCTAD postings, since it hosts vacancies across the whole UN Secretariat family - Plan your finances in advance, since this internship is unpaid and based in Geneva, a relatively high cost-of-living city - Tailor your cover letter to the specific division's policy area, whether trade negotiations, investment, or development finance - Make use of Geneva's UN grounds access if selected, attending relevant public meetings and events to deepen your experience - Apply as early as possible for postings in high-demand divisions ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for the UNCTAD Internship Programme?** Final-year bachelor's students, master's students, or recent graduates within the past year. **Is the UNCTAD internship paid?** No, it is unpaid, and interns cover their own travel, accommodation, and insurance. **Where is the internship based?** Primarily at UNCTAD headquarters in Geneva, Switzerland. **What languages do I need?** A strong command of English or French, UNCTAD's main working languages. **How do I apply?** Through the shared UN Careers Inspira portal, applying to UNCTAD-specific postings. **What kind of work will I do?** Support for research, policy analysis, and administrative work related to trade and development, depending on the division. ## Apply Now Search current UNCTAD postings and apply via the official UN Careers Inspira portal: https://unctad.org/page/internship-programme
