Fellowship - Singapore

A*STAR Research Attachment Programme (ARAP)

## Introduction

Singapore has spent the past two decades building one of Asia's most respected research ecosystems, and A*STAR, the Agency for Science, Technology and Research, sits at the center of that effort. The A*STAR Research Attachment Programme (ARAP) gives international PhD students at partner universities the chance to spend one to two years working directly inside an A*STAR research institute, embedded in a joint project alongside their existing doctoral supervisor. For PhD candidates whose research would genuinely benefit from Singapore's labs, equipment, and research culture, ARAP offers a structured, funded route into that environment without requiring them to abandon their home degree program.

## About the Opportunity

ARAP is built around joint research projects rather than a standalone Singapore-based doctorate. A student remains enrolled at their home university, and their existing supervisor works with an A*STAR-based researcher to design a project that genuinely spans both institutions. The attachment period, typically one to two years, gives the student sustained access to A*STAR's research facilities across fields including biomedical sciences, physical sciences, engineering, and infocomm technology, while keeping their primary academic relationship anchored at their home institution.

## Benefits

During the attachment, A*STAR provides a monthly stipend of approximately S$2,500, enough to cover living costs in Singapore, along with a one-time settling-in and IT allowance to help students get established when they arrive. Students also gain access to A*STAR's research facilities and equipment, resources that can be difficult to access outside a small number of leading research economies. It is worth noting clearly that A*STAR's funding covers the Singapore-based attachment specifically; it does not cover tuition fees at the student's home university, which remain that institution's and student's own responsibility.

## Who Can Apply?

ARAP is only open to PhD students already enrolled at one of A*STAR's designated partner universities, so the very first eligibility question for any prospective applicant is whether their institution is on that list. Beyond that, applicants need a bachelor's degree with at least Second-Class Upper Honours or an equivalent standard, and, critically, they need to be nominated by their existing PhD supervisor for a specific joint project with an A*STAR institute. Students cannot apply independently; the nomination has to come from their supervisor, which means the practical starting point for any interested student is a conversation with their own advisor about whether a Singapore-based joint project makes sense for their research.

## Fields of Study or Available Areas

A*STAR's research spans biomedical sciences, physical sciences, engineering, and infocomm technology, and ARAP projects are drawn from across these areas depending on which A*STAR institute a student's supervisor connects with. Students whose research already touches on one of these fields are best positioned to identify a genuine joint-project fit.

## Required Documents

The centerpiece of an ARAP application is the supervisor nomination itself, without which a student cannot be considered. Alongside that nomination, applicants need their degree certificate or transcripts and a joint research project proposal developed together with both their home supervisor and their prospective A*STAR-based collaborator.

## Application Process

Because nomination drives everything, the practical first step is for a student to discuss the possibility with their PhD supervisor and, together, identify a suitable A*STAR institute and researcher whose work aligns with the student's doctoral project. Once that connection is established, the supervisor submits the nomination along with the joint research proposal and the student's degree certificate or transcripts, following the specific procedure set by their partner university's designated office. A*STAR runs two nomination windows each year, so students should confirm with their university which window fits their planned timeline.

## Deadline

ARAP nominations are accepted twice yearly, by 1 June or 1 December. The next deadline is 1 December 2026. Because the nomination process depends on coordination between a student's home supervisor and a prospective A*STAR researcher, students interested in this cycle should begin those conversations well ahead of the deadline rather than waiting until the final weeks.

## Why You Should Apply

For PhD students whose research would benefit from world-class facilities and a genuinely international research environment, ARAP offers something most fully funded fellowships and research opportunities cannot: sustained, funded access to A*STAR's labs without requiring a student to restart their doctorate elsewhere. It rewards students who already have strong, engaged relationships with their supervisors, since that relationship is the actual gateway into the program. For PhD candidates in biomedical sciences, physical sciences, engineering, or infocomm technology looking to strengthen their research through international collaboration, ARAP is worth raising directly with a supervisor well before the next nomination deadline.

For full details on partner universities and the nomination process, visit the official A*STAR scholarships page: https://www.a-star.edu.sg/scholarships/home/international-awards/astar-research-attachment-programme-arap
