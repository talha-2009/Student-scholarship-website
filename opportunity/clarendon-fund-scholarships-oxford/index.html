Clarendon Fund Scholarships at Oxford

Introduction

Since its first cohort arrived in 2001, Oxford's Clarendon Fund has become one of the university's largest graduate scholarship schemes, funding over 200 new scholars every year across every academic division, with no restriction on nationality, field, or age. What distinguishes Clarendon from many prestigious peer scholarships is its complete lack of a separate application: every applicant to an eligible Oxford graduate course is automatically considered, removing an entire layer of paperwork that scholarships like Rhodes or Marshall require.

About the Opportunity

Clarendon scholars are selected from within Oxford's own graduate admissions process, with academics in each applicant's chosen field first assessing admission itself, then separately nominating the strongest candidates to their academic division for Clarendon funding. Because nomination happens at the department level, judged by specialists in the applicant's specific field, selection criteria in practice come down almost entirely to academic excellence and research potential within that field, rather than broader leadership or extracurricular achievement.

Benefits

Scholars receive full course and college fees plus a generous annual living grant, at least roughly GBP 18,600 for full-time students, for the entire period of fee liability on their course; part-time students receive a proportionate, modular funding package matched to their course structure.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants of any nationality, residency status, or field of study applying for a new full-time or part-time Master's or DPhil course at Oxford are eligible, though current Oxford students continuing the same degree into a following year are not eligible; selection rests on outstanding academic merit and potential rather than a published minimum grade.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

All degree-bearing graduate courses at Oxford are eligible, spanning every academic division from the sciences and medicine through humanities, social sciences, and mathematical, physical, and life sciences.

Required Documents

Applicants need only their standard Oxford graduate course application and its usual supporting materials, transcripts, references, and a personal statement or research proposal as required by the specific course; no separate Clarendon-specific documents are needed.

Application Process

Applicants apply for admission to their chosen Oxford graduate course by that course's relevant December or January funding deadline; academics in the applicant's field assess both admission and Clarendon nomination together, with each academic division reviewing departmental nominations in February or March before offers are typically issued by late April.

Deadline

The relevant deadline depends on the specific course, generally falling in December or January; applicants should confirm their exact course's deadline directly on the Oxford course page, since missing it means missing Clarendon consideration entirely.

Why You Should Apply

For academically outstanding graduate applicants to Oxford, Clarendon offers comprehensive funding with zero additional application burden, a genuine rarity among scholarships of this scale and prestige, making it worth factoring into application timing regardless of which specific Oxford course a candidate is pursuing.

For full course-specific deadlines and details, visit the official Clarendon Fund page: https://www.ox.ac.uk/admissions/graduate/fees-and-funding/funding/clarendon
