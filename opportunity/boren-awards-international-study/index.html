Boren Awards for International Study

Introduction

The Boren Awards occupy an unusual space among US-funded study abroad programs: they exist explicitly to serve American national security interests, not simply to broaden students' horizons. Funded through the National Security Education Program, Boren Awards send US undergraduate and graduate students to study critical languages in world regions the government considers strategically important, and in return, recipients commit to a period of federal government service after their studies. For students genuinely interested in a national security, diplomacy, or federal government career, that trade-off is a feature rather than a drawback.

About the Opportunity

Boren funds two tiers, Boren Scholarships for undergraduates and Boren Fellowships for graduate students, both built around the same core idea: immersive study of a language and region the US government has identified as critical to its interests, ranging well beyond the commonly-studied languages found in most standard study abroad programs. The distinguishing feature of a Boren Award compared with other study abroad funding is what comes after graduation, since recipients agree to work in a federal government position related to national security for a defined period.

Benefits

Boren Scholarships for undergraduates provide up to USD 25,000, while Boren Fellowships for graduate students provide up to USD 30,000, both intended to support the full cost of study abroad and language study. This level of funding is substantial compared with many study abroad scholarships, reflecting the strategic importance the program's funders place on building language and regional expertise among future government employees.

Who Can Apply?

Applicants must be US citizens, whether currently enrolled undergraduates or graduate students, planning study abroad in a country and language that the program considers eligible under its current national security priorities. Because the eligible countries and languages are tied to shifting government priorities, applicants need to confirm the current list directly rather than assuming a language or region qualifies based on past cycles.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Boren funds language study paired with international studies more broadly, with a deliberate focus on languages and regions that are less commonly taught in standard US university curricula and that the government considers strategically significant.

Required Documents

Applicants need an official acceptance into a study abroad program, academic transcripts, essays explaining their goals and connection to national security or government service, and recommendation letters supporting their application.

Application Process

Applicants apply online through the Boren Awards portal, and a key practical requirement is securing official acceptance into a specific study abroad program before or during the application process, since this acceptance is itself a required document. Essays play a significant role in selection, since reviewers are looking for a genuine, credible connection between an applicant's language and regional study plans and their stated interest in future federal government service, not simply enthusiasm for travel or language learning in the abstract.

Deadline

Boren Awards deadlines recur annually, typically in late January, though the exact date for the next cycle had not yet been officially confirmed at the time of writing. Applicants should confirm the current cycle's precise deadline directly on the Boren Awards website well ahead of when they plan to apply.

Why You Should Apply

For US students with genuine interest in a national security, diplomacy, or federal government career, few study abroad scholarships offer this level of funding specifically tied to career-relevant language and regional expertise. The post-award service requirement, rather than being a drawback, functions as a built-in career pathway for students who already see themselves working in government, making Boren one of the more strategically useful international scholarships for that specific group of applicants.

For current eligible countries, languages, and deadlines, visit the official Boren Awards website: https://www.borenawards.org/
