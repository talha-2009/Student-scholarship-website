MIT Research Science Institute (RSI)

## Introduction The MIT Research Science Institute (RSI), run by the Center for Excellence in Education in partnership with MIT, is widely regarded as one of the most prestigious free summer research programmes for high school students in the world. Over six weeks, participants combine intensive STEM coursework with hands-on research mentored directly by working scientists, entirely free of charge — a rare combination that makes RSI genuinely competitive among strong STEM-focused high school juniors globally. ## Opportunity Overview - **Organization:** MIT / Center for Excellence in Education - **Country of Study:** United States (MIT campus) - **Opportunity Type:** Summer School - **Funding Type:** Fully Funded - **Degree Level:** High School - **Duration:** Six weeks - **Official Website:** cee.org/programs/research-science-institute ## Benefits - Full funding for tuition, housing, and meals - A mentored research experience with practicing scientists - Six weeks on-campus at MIT ## Eligibility Criteria - Be a high school junior - Have a strong academic record in STEM subjects ## Required Documents - Transcript - Essays - Two recommendation letters - Standardized test scores ## Application Process **Step 1.** Apply online through the Center for Excellence in Education (CEE) application portal. **Step 2.** Submit your transcript, essays, and standardized test scores. **Step 3.** Arrange for two recommendation letters, typically from STEM teachers who know your academic work well. **Step 4.** CEE reviews applications for academic strength and research potential. **Step 5.** Successful applicants are matched with a research mentor and begin the six-week programme at MIT, combining coursework with hands-on research. ## Important Dates Please refer to the official website for the current year's specific application deadline, since RSI runs on an annual cycle with its own defined timeline. ## Selection Criteria RSI is highly selective, evaluating applicants on strong STEM academic performance, the quality of application essays demonstrating genuine research interest and maturity, standardized test scores, and recommendation letters that speak to independent research potential. ## Tips for Applicants - Apply as a high school junior specifically, since this is the primary eligible year for RSI - Use your essays to demonstrate genuine intellectual curiosity and prior engagement with STEM subjects beyond coursework alone - Choose recommenders who can speak specifically to your independent thinking and research readiness, not just grades - Apply as early as your junior year academic record allows, since RSI is highly competitive - Research past RSI participant profiles and projects to understand what a strong application typically looks like ## Frequently Asked Questions **Who can apply for MIT RSI?** High school juniors with a strong academic record in STEM subjects. **Is it fully funded?** Yes — tuition, housing, and meals are all covered at no cost to participants. **How long does the programme last?** Six weeks. **Where is it held?** On the MIT campus in the United States. **Do I need standardized test scores?** Yes, these are a required part of the application. **What will I do during the programme?** Combine STEM coursework with hands-on research mentored by scientists. ## Apply Now Apply through the official Center for Excellence in Education RSI page: https://www.cee.org/programs/research-science-institute
