MIT Research Science Institute (RSI)

Introduction

Among summer research programmes for high school students, MIT's Research Science Institute occupies a unique tier: it is entirely free, extraordinarily selective, and structured around genuine mentored research rather than a fixed curriculum. Run by the Center for Excellence in Education in partnership with MIT, RSI brings together roughly 80 rising seniors from around the world each summer for six weeks combining intensive STEM coursework with hands-on research directly mentored by working scientists at one of the world's leading technical universities.

About the Opportunity

Participants spend the first portion of the programme in condensed, intensive coursework covering STEM fundamentals at a pace and depth well beyond typical high school curricula, before transitioning into individual research projects paired with a mentor working in a relevant field at MIT or a nearby research institution. By the end of the six weeks, students complete and present original research, often producing work of a caliber more commonly associated with undergraduate or even early graduate research, a reflection of both the calibre of admitted students and the intensity of mentorship provided.

Benefits

RSI provides full funding for tuition, housing, and meals for the entire six-week programme, alongside a mentored research experience with practicing scientists and six weeks living and working on the MIT campus itself, giving participants genuine exposure to research university life well before college.

Who Can Apply?

High school juniors with strong academic records in STEM subjects can apply, with RSI consistently ranking among the most selective STEM summer programmes globally given its combination of full funding, small cohort size, and MIT affiliation.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

RSI spans STEM research broadly, with individual student projects touching physics, chemistry, biology, computer science, mathematics, and engineering depending on available mentors and each student's specific interests.

Required Documents

Applicants need a transcript, essays, two recommendation letters, and standardized test scores as part of their online application through CEE.

Application Process

Applicants apply online via CEE, submitting transcripts, essays, recommendation letters, and standardized test scores; given the programme's intense selectivity, essays demonstrating genuine research curiosity and intellectual maturity carry significant weight in review.

Deadline

Please refer to the official website for the current year's specific application deadline, since RSI runs on an annual cycle with its own defined timeline that can shift slightly year to year.

Why You Should Apply

For high school juniors with genuine STEM research ambition, few summer programmes combine full funding, MIT-level mentorship, and this level of academic prestige, making RSI one of the most valuable pre-college research experiences available anywhere in the world.

For full application details, visit the official CEE Research Science Institute page: https://www.cee.org/programs/research-science-institute
