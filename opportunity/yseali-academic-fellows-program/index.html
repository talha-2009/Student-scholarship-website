YSEALI Academic Fellows Program

Introduction

Since its launch by the U.S. Department of State, the Young Southeast Asian Leaders Initiative, better known as YSEALI, has become one of the primary channels through which the United States invests in relationships with young leaders across ASEAN and Timor-Leste. The YSEALI Academic Fellows Program is the flagship exchange within that broader initiative, bringing undergraduates and recent graduates to the United States for five intensive weeks combining academic coursework with a multi-city study tour. For young people in Southeast Asia interested in leadership development with a genuinely international dimension, it offers a fully funded introduction to American civic and academic life built specifically around themes relevant to the region's own development.

About the Opportunity

The program is organized around a small set of core themes: civic engagement, natural resources and environmental sustainability, entrepreneurship and economic development, and governance. Each Academic Fellows cohort is typically built around one of these focus areas, so fellows spend their five weeks studying alongside peers with related interests, attending coursework at a U.S. host institution, and then moving through a study tour that takes them to multiple American cities to see how these themes play out in different regional and institutional contexts across the country.

Benefits

YSEALI Academic Fellows receive full funding for the entire program: international travel to and from the United States, tuition and program costs at the host institution, accommodation for the full five weeks, and coverage of the associated study tour costs across multiple U.S. cities. The program also covers J-1 visa fees, removing a cost that can otherwise add up for participants without independent means. Beyond the direct financial coverage, fellows gain firsthand exposure to American academic and civic institutions and build a peer network spanning ASEAN countries and Timor-Leste, connections that often continue well after the program ends through YSEALI's broader alumni community.

Who Can Apply?

Eligibility is specific and firmly enforced: applicants must be between 18 and 25 years old, either currently enrolled undergraduates or recent graduates, and must be citizens and residents of an ASEAN member country or Timor-Leste. Strong English proficiency is required, since the program is conducted entirely in English and involves active participation in coursework and group discussion. There is no professional experience requirement, which makes the program accessible to younger applicants still early in their academic or civic involvement, provided they can demonstrate genuine engagement with one of the program's core themes.

Fields of Study or Available Areas

Rather than academic disciplines in the traditional sense, YSEALI Academic Fellows engage with one of four thematic tracks: civic engagement, natural resources, entrepreneurship, or governance. Applicants are generally expected to show some existing interest or activity connected to their chosen theme, whether through volunteer work, student organizations, early career experience, or academic study touching on that area.

Required Documents

Applicants need a valid passport copy and academic transcripts documenting their current or completed studies. The application also requires written responses to short-answer essay questions, which form a central part of how candidates are assessed, along with documentation proving citizenship or residency in an eligible ASEAN country or Timor-Leste.

Application Process

Applications are submitted entirely online through the official YSEALI Academic Fellows Program portal. Candidates complete the application form and respond to a set of short-answer essay prompts designed to reveal their leadership experience, motivations, and connection to their chosen thematic area. Because the essays carry significant weight in selection, applicants benefit from taking real time to develop specific, concrete examples rather than general statements of interest. Shortlisted candidates may be contacted for further review before final fellows are confirmed and matched with a specific cohort and host institution.

Deadline

The application deadline for this cycle is set for 21 July 2026. Given the volume of applications YSEALI typically receives from across the ASEAN region and Timor-Leste, applicants are encouraged to begin preparing their essays and gathering required documents well ahead of this date rather than waiting until the final days before the deadline.

Why You Should Apply

For young people across Southeast Asia and Timor-Leste, few fully funded exchange programs offer this specific combination: a short, intensive time commitment, direct exposure to U.S. institutions and multiple cities, and a thematic structure that connects directly to real regional challenges like governance, sustainability, and entrepreneurship. As an entry point into international exchange programs and leadership development, YSEALI Academic Fellows asks for relatively little prior experience while offering a genuinely substantive experience in return, making it a strong option for undergraduates and recent graduates just beginning to build an international profile.

For full eligibility details and to begin an application, visit the official YSEALI Academic Fellows Program portal: https://www.ysealiafp.org/apply
