Studying in Canada: A Quick Guide

Canada is one of the most welcoming destinations for international students, known for affordable tuition, post-graduation work permits, and a clear pathway to permanent residency.

Top universities for international students

  • University of Toronto — Canada's top-ranked university, leading in medicine, AI, and engineering
  • McGill University — Montreal's world-class institution, strong in medicine, law, and humanities
  • University of British Columbia (UBC) — top-ranked for sustainability, computer science, and environmental studies
  • University of Waterloo — globally recognised for computer science, engineering, and co-op education
  • McMaster University — leading in health sciences, engineering, and evidence-based medicine
  • University of Alberta — strong in energy research, AI, and environmental sciences

Tuition fees for international students

  • Undergraduate: typically CAD $20,000–$55,000 per year depending on the programme
  • Master's: typically CAD $15,000–$40,000 for the full programme
  • PhD: typically CAD $7,000–$20,000 per year, though most PhD students receive full funding packages
  • Professional programmes (MBA, MD, JD) can exceed CAD $60,000 per year

Student visa requirements

  • International students need a study permit to study in Canada
  • You must have a letter of acceptance from a designated learning institution (DLI)
  • You must demonstrate sufficient funds to cover tuition plus CAD $20,635 per year for living expenses (as of 2025)
  • You may also need a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) or an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) depending on your country
  • After graduation, you may be eligible for a Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) of up to three years

Living costs

  • Toronto and Vancouver: approximately CAD $1,500–$2,200 per month for rent, food, and transport
  • Montreal: approximately CAD $1,000–$1,500 per month, significantly cheaper than Toronto or Vancouver
  • Smaller cities (Halifax, Winnipeg, Saskatoon): approximately CAD $800–$1,200 per month
  • Most universities estimate total annual living costs of CAD $15,000–$20,000 depending on the city

Major scholarship programmes for international students

  • McCall MacBain Scholarship — fully funded master's or professional degree at McGill University
  • Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation Doctoral Scholarship — fully funded PhD in humanities and social sciences
  • Killam Fellowships Program — funded exchange for US and Canadian undergraduates
  • Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships — CAD $50,000 per year for doctoral students at Canadian universities
  • Ontario Graduate Scholarship — funding for graduate students at Ontario universities
