Studying in Australia: A Quick Guide

Australia is a top destination for international students, known for high-quality education, a multicultural environment, and post-study work opportunities.

Top universities for international students

  • University of Melbourne — Australia's highest-ranked university, strong in law, medicine, and arts
  • University of Sydney — one of the oldest universities in Australia, leading in business, health, and engineering
  • Australian National University (ANU) — research-intensive university in Canberra, strong in science and public policy
  • University of New South Wales (UNSW) — top-ranked for engineering, technology, and business
  • University of Queensland — leading in biological sciences, environmental studies, and medicine
  • Monash University — one of Australia's largest universities, strong in pharmacy, education, and engineering

Tuition fees for international students

  • Undergraduate: typically AUD $30,000–$50,000 per year depending on the programme
  • Master's: typically AUD $32,000–$55,000 per year
  • PhD: typically AUD $35,000–$45,000 per year, though many PhD students receive tuition offset scholarships
  • Medicine and veterinary programmes can exceed AUD $70,000 per year

Student visa requirements

  • International students need a Student visa (subclass 500) to study in Australia
  • You must have a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from a registered education provider
  • You must demonstrate financial capacity of at least AUD $29,710 per year for living costs (as of 2025)
  • You must meet English language requirements (IELTS, TOEFL, PTE Academic, or equivalent)
  • Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) is mandatory for the duration of your visa
  • After graduation, you may be eligible for a Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) for 2–4 years depending on your qualification

Living costs

  • Sydney and Melbourne: approximately AUD $1,800–$2,500 per month for rent, food, and transport
  • Brisbane and Perth: approximately AUD $1,400–$1,800 per month
  • Adelaide, Canberra, Hobart: approximately AUD $1,200–$1,500 per month
  • Most universities estimate total annual living costs of AUD $25,000–$30,000

Major scholarship programmes for international students

  • University of Melbourne Graduate Scholarship — fully funded master's for high-achieving international students
  • Australia Awards Scholarships — fully funded study for students from developing countries
  • Research Training Program (RTP) — tuition offset and stipend for research higher degrees
  • Sydney International Scholarship — tuition fee support for outstanding international students
  • Destination Australia Scholarship — AUD $15,000 per year for students studying in regional Australia
