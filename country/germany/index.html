Studying in Germany: A Quick Guide

Germany is one of the most affordable destinations for international students, with most public universities charging no tuition fees regardless of nationality.

Top universities for international students

  • Technical University of Munich (TUM) — Germany's highest-ranked university, strong in engineering, computer science, and natural sciences
  • Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität München (LMU) — one of Europe's leading research universities with a 500-year tradition
  • Heidelberg University — Germany's oldest university, renowned for medicine, physics, and humanities
  • Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin — research-intensive university in the capital, strong in social sciences
  • RWTH Aachen University — one of Europe's largest technical universities, leading in engineering research
  • Freie Universität Berlin — top-ranked for political science, humanities, and social sciences

Tuition fees for international students

  • Most public universities charge no tuition fees for undergraduate and master's programmes, regardless of nationality
  • Students pay a semester contribution (Semesterbeitrag) of €150–€350, which often includes a public transport ticket
  • Baden-Württemberg charges non-EU students €1,500 per semester (approximately €3,000 per year)
  • Some specialised or continuing education master's programmes may charge fees of €5,000–€20,000
  • PhD positions are typically salaried as research assistant roles, with monthly pay of €2,500–€4,500

Student visa requirements

  • EU/EEA citizens do not need a visa to study in Germany
  • Non-EU students need a student visa (Studentenvisum) or a language course visa if attending a preparatory language course first
  • You must demonstrate financial resources of €11,904 per year (as of 2025), typically via a blocked account (Sperrkonto)
  • Health insurance is mandatory for all students; public insurance costs approximately €120 per month
  • After graduation, non-EU students can stay in Germany for 18 months on a job-seeker visa

Living costs

  • Munich: approximately €1,000–€1,200 per month, the most expensive student city in Germany
  • Berlin, Hamburg, Frankfurt: approximately €850–€1,000 per month
  • Smaller cities (Leipzig, Dresden, Münster): approximately €700–€850 per month
  • Student housing through a Studentenwerk is the cheapest option, typically €250–€400 per month

Major scholarship programmes for international students

  • DAAD EPOS Scholarships — fully funded master's for professionals from developing countries
  • DAAD Research Grants — funding for doctoral candidates and postdoctoral researchers
  • Rosa Luxemburg Stiftung Scholarship — fully funded study for socially engaged students
  • Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung (KAS) Scholarship — funding for outstanding international students
  • Deutschlandstipendium — €300 per month merit-based scholarship at participating universities
  • DAAD Helmut Schmidt Programme — fully funded master's in public policy and good governance
