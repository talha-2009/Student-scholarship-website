Studying in the United Kingdom: A Quick Guide

The UK is home to some of the world's oldest and most respected universities. Understanding the basics can help you plan your application strategy and budget.

Top universities for international students

  • University of Oxford — consistently ranked in the global top 5, strong in humanities, sciences, and medicine
  • University of Cambridge — world-leading research output across all disciplines
  • Imperial College London — top-ranked for engineering, business, and natural sciences
  • UCL (University College London) — multidisciplinary research university with 100+ departments
  • London School of Economics (LSE) — global leader in social sciences, economics, and public policy
  • University of Edinburgh — Scotland's leading university with strong AI and data science programmes

Tuition fees for international students

  • Undergraduate: typically £15,000–£35,000 per year depending on the subject and institution
  • Master's: typically £12,000–£40,000 for the full programme (usually one year)
  • PhD: typically £18,000–£30,000 per year, though many PhD students receive funding packages
  • Medicine and laboratory-based programmes tend to be at the higher end of these ranges

Student visa requirements

  • Most international students need a Student visa (formerly Tier 4) to study in the UK
  • You need a Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) from a licensed sponsor university
  • You must demonstrate English language proficiency (IELTS, TOEFL, or equivalent)
  • You must show proof of funds to cover tuition and living costs for a set period
  • The visa application fee is approximately £490, plus the Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) of £776 per year

Living costs

  • London: approximately £1,300–£1,500 per month for rent, food, transport, and essentials
  • Outside London: approximately £900–£1,200 per month depending on the city
  • Students in Edinburgh, Manchester, and Birmingham typically spend less than those in London
  • Most universities estimate annual living costs of £12,000–£15,000 outside London and £15,000–£18,000 in London

Major scholarship programmes for international students

  • Chevening Scholarship — fully funded master's for future leaders from 160+ countries
  • Gates Cambridge Scholarship — full funding for outstanding postgraduates at Cambridge
  • Rhodes Scholarship — fully funded study at the University of Oxford for exceptional students
  • Marshall Scholarship — funded graduate study for US citizens at any UK university
  • Weidenfeld-Hoffmann Scholarships — fully funded master's at Oxford with leadership development
  • Ellison Undergraduate Scholars Program — fully funded undergraduate study at select UK institutions
