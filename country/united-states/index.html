Studying in the United States: A Quick Guide

The US has the largest number of international students of any English-speaking country, with world-class universities across every state.

Top universities for international students

  • Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) — consistently ranked #1 globally for engineering and technology
  • Stanford University — top-ranked for computer science, entrepreneurship, and interdisciplinary research
  • Harvard University — the oldest university in the US, leading in law, medicine, business, and public policy
  • California Institute of Technology (Caltech) — small but elite, focused on science and engineering
  • University of Chicago — renowned for economics, social sciences, and rigorous academic training
  • Columbia University — Ivy League research university in New York City with global reach

Tuition fees for international students

  • Public universities: $25,000–$50,000 per year for out-of-state and international students
  • Private universities: $40,000–$80,000 per year depending on the programme
  • Community colleges: $8,000–$15,000 per year, often used as a pathway to four-year universities
  • Graduate programmes vary widely; professional degrees (MBA, JD, MD) can exceed $70,000 per year

Student visa requirements

  • Most students need an F-1 visa for academic study or a J-1 visa for exchange programmes
  • You must be accepted by a SEVP-certified institution and receive Form I-20
  • You must pay the SEVIS I-901 fee (currently $350 for F-1 students)
  • You must demonstrate sufficient funds to cover at least one year of tuition and living expenses
  • Visa interviews are conducted at US embassies and consulates worldwide

Living costs

  • New York City, San Francisco, Boston: approximately $2,000–$3,000 per month
  • Midwest and Southern states: approximately $1,000–$1,500 per month
  • Most universities estimate total annual costs (tuition + living) of $40,000–$80,000 at private institutions
  • On-campus housing is often cheaper than off-campus, especially in the first year

Major scholarship programmes for international students

  • Fulbright Foreign Student Program — fully funded master's and PhD for students from 160+ countries
  • Knight-Hennessy Scholars Program — fully funded graduate study at Stanford University
  • Boren Awards — funding for US students studying critical languages and regions
  • Hubert H. Humphrey Fellowship — fully funded professional development for mid-career professionals
  • Global UGRAD — semester exchange programme for undergraduates at US universities
