International Research Grants

This page brings together verified listings related to research grants. This collection currently contains 2 matching opportunities. The collection is assembled for doctoral candidates, postdoctoral researchers, faculty members, and independent specialists. Each listing states the country, funding arrangement, degree level, field, and deadline so that applicants can evaluate multiple programs side by side. The consistent format across entries is designed to help you identify which opportunities match your goals without visiting every provider site first.

2 matching listings.

What applicants can expect

Benefits and support vary by program but may include research costs, travel, fieldwork, equipment, publication support, and collaboration with established institutions. The final terms are set by the provider. Applicants should verify whether tuition, travel, insurance, accommodation, or research costs are covered before applying. Reading the full documentation on the provider's site is the only way to confirm exact coverage. Some programs also include mentoring, networking events, or professional development components beyond direct financial support.

Eligibility and process

Before applying, review the published eligibility carefully. Typical checks include research stage, institutional affiliation, eligible costs, geographic scope, methodology, and project timetable. A practical application sequence is to confirm the grant remit, develop a costed work plan, secure institutional approvals, and submit the required proposal package. Read both the eligibility section and any listed exclusions, then confirm that your qualification timeline and location fit. When admissions and funding run on separate tracks, complete both processes; do not assume one covers the other. Save copies of submitted documents and confirmation receipts for your records.

Additional tips

To focus your search effectively, make the research question precise, connect every cost to an activity, and write outcomes that reviewers can evaluate. Compare each opportunity against your academic direction or career plan. Prepare a base CV and document folder, but customize each motivation statement to match the specific selection criteria of the program. A shortlist of well-matched applications generally produces better results than submitting broadly to every available option.
